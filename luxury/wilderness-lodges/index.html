Iconic

Great Bear Rainforest, BC

Nimmo Bay Wilderness Resort

"Where the rainforest meets the sea"

For over 40 years, Nimmo Bay has defined Canadian wilderness luxury. Nestled in the heart of the Great Bear Rainforest, this family-owned retreat offers just nine floating cabins and chalets, ensuring an intimate experience in one of Earth's most biodiverse ecosystems. Watch grizzlies fish for salmon steps from your door, kayak through emerald fjords, or soak in the famous waterfall hot tub while eagles soar overhead.

Vancouver Island, BC

Clayoquot Wilderness Resort

"Luxury safari tents in temperate rainforest"

Experience the ultimate in glamping luxury at this UNESCO Biosphere Reserve property. Twenty-five prospector-style tents feature antique furnishings, wood-burning stoves, and ensuite bathrooms, all surrounded by 600,000 acres of pristine temperate rainforest. From whale watching to bear viewing, from forest bathing to horseback riding along remote beaches, Clayoquot offers adventures as refined as its accommodations.

Floating

Princess Royal Island, BC

King Pacific Lodge

"A floating five-star hotel in the wild"

Anchored in a remote inlet of Princess Royal Island, King Pacific Lodge floats in one of the world's most pristine marine environments. This converted barge has been transformed into an elegant 17-room hotel with a world-class kitchen, full spa, and direct access to Spirit Bear territory. The lodge moves seasonally to follow wildlife migrations, offering guests front-row seats to nature's greatest shows.

Adventure

Bella Coola Valley, BC

Tweedsmuir Park Lodge

"Gateway to grizzly country"

Located on the doorstep of BC's largest provincial park, Tweedsmuir combines heritage charm with adventure luxury. The historic main lodge and modern chalets offer comfortable accommodations, while the surrounding wilderness delivers some of Canada's best grizzly bear viewing, heli-hiking, and fly fishing. In autumn, watch hundreds of grizzlies gather at the nearby Atnarko River for the salmon run.
