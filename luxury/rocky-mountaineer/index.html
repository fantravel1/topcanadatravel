Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning your Rocky Mountaineer journey.

GoldLeaf Service features bi-level glass-dome coaches with a separate dining car below, an outdoor viewing platform, premium gourmet meals served in a dedicated restaurant, unlimited premium beverages including cocktails, and upgraded hotel accommodations. SilverLeaf offers single-level dome coaches with oversized windows, meals served at your seat, and standard hotel accommodations‚Äîstill an excellent experience at a lower price point. Both offer the same spectacular scenery and attentive host service.
Rocky Mountaineer travels exclusively during daylight hours so passengers never miss any of the spectacular scenery‚Äîthat's the entire point of the journey! Overnight stops at hotels along the route (like Kamloops or Quesnel) allow guests to rest comfortably, enjoy local restaurants, and explore the towns. This format also means you're fresh and rested each morning for another full day of viewing.
The train operates from mid-April through mid-October. June through August offers the warmest weather and longest days‚Äîideal for the outdoor viewing platform. September brings stunning fall colors with golden larches. Spring (April-May) and late fall (October) offer better prices and fewer crowds, though weather can be more variable. Each season has its own unique beauty, so there's truly no wrong time to go.
Your main luggage is transferred separately and delivered directly to your hotel rooms‚Äîyou won't need to handle it at all during the journey. Simply pack a small day bag with essentials like camera, binoculars, layers for the outdoor platform, and any medications. Your bags will be waiting in your room when you arrive each evening.
Rocky Mountaineer journeys include the rail travel, all meals aboard the train, and overnight hotel accommodations at intermediate stops. You can book rail-only packages (just the train and required overnight hotels) or complete vacation packages that include additional nights at your starting/ending destinations, activities, and transfers. Our concierge can help design the perfect itinerary for your trip.
For peak summer dates (June-August) and popular routes, booking 6-12 months in advance is recommended‚Äîpopular departure dates sell out quickly. For shoulder season travel (April-May, September-October), 3-6 months is usually sufficient. GoldLeaf Service has limited capacity and sells out before SilverLeaf, so if that's your preference, book early. Last-minute availability sometimes opens up, but selection will be limited.
Rocky Mountaineer strives to accommodate all travelers. GoldLeaf coaches require climbing stairs between levels, so SilverLeaf single-level coaches may be more suitable for those with mobility limitations. Wheelchairs can be accommodated with advance notice. The train makes station stops where passengers can step off to stretch‚Äîplatforms are accessible. Contact us to discuss specific needs and we'll help find the best solution.
Rocky Mountaineer accommodates most dietary requirements including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and common allergies. Notify us at booking and confirm again 30 days before travel. The culinary team takes dietary needs seriously and will prepare alternative meals that match the quality and presentation of standard dishes. Kosher and Halal meals can sometimes be arranged with sufficient advance notice.
