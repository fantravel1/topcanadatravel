FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

October to November is prime polar bear season in Churchill. This is when bears gather along the shores of Hudson Bay, waiting for the sea ice to form so they can return to hunting seals. During this window, you can see 30+ bears in a single day. July-August offers summer beluga whale experiences with occasional polar bear sightings.

Spirit bears (white Kermode bears) are extremely rare ‚Äî only about 400 exist in the wild. However, our experienced First Nations guides have intimate knowledge of their territories and achieve 70-80% sighting success rates during peak salmon season (August-October). Multi-day trips significantly increase your chances.

Most experiences are accessible to average fitness levels. Polar bear trips involve minimal walking. Grizzly viewing is primarily from boats and platforms. Spirit bear trips may include moderate hiking on forest trails. Beluga snorkeling requires basic swimming ability. We can customize activities based on your fitness level.

Distances vary by species and situation. Polar bears often approach Tundra Buggies within meters. Grizzly viewing platforms position you 10-30 meters from fishing bears. Spirit bears are viewed from 15-50 meters. Belugas actively seek out swimmers, often coming within arm's reach. All viewing follows strict wildlife safety protocols.

We recommend a DSLR or mirrorless camera with a 100-400mm telephoto lens for most wildlife. Polar bear trips benefit from longer 500-600mm lenses. Waterproof cameras or housings are essential for beluga snorkeling. Our guides provide photography coaching, and professional photographers are available for hire. Basic smartphone photography can also yield excellent results.

Weather delays are built into our itineraries. We maintain flexibility in schedules and have contingency plans for all trips. If significant delays occur, we provide alternative activities, extended stays where possible, or partial refunds. Our 25+ years of experience means we rarely face insurmountable weather challenges.

We maintain a 98% success rate for primary species sightings. While we cannot guarantee nature, if you don't see the primary species on your trip (polar bears in Churchill, grizzlies at Knight Inlet, etc.), we offer a 25% credit toward a future trip. Spirit bear sightings have a 70-80% success rate due to their rarity.

Popular experiences book 6-12 months in advance. Polar bear trips for October-November should be booked by January-March. Spirit bear expeditions fill by spring for fall season. Some last-minute availability exists, but specific dates and premium accommodations require early booking. Contact us to check current availability.
