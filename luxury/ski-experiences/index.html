FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Most operators require strong intermediate to advanced skiing ability. You should be comfortable skiing black diamond runs at resorts in varied snow conditions and able to ski 20,000+ vertical feet per day. Some operators offer programs for strong intermediates, while others like Bella Coola cater to experts seeking extreme terrain.

January through March offers the most consistent powder conditions. January typically brings fresh storms and cold temperatures. February is peak season with deep base and all terrain accessible. March and April offer spring skiing with longer days and corn snow on glaciers. December can be excellent but terrain may be limited.

Weather days are built into most packages. Operators maintain vertical guarantees (typically 25,000-30,000 feet) and may extend your trip if needed. Many lodges have snowcat backup for ground-based skiing. Alternative activities like spa, yoga, or touring may be offered. Credits or refunds vary by operator policy.

All operators provide avalanche transceivers, probes, and shovels. Airbag packs are available at most operations (some included, some rental). Fat skis optimized for powder are included. Helmets are mandatory. Guides carry extensive rescue equipment and communication devices. Safety briefings are conducted before the first run.

Prime weeks (February) should be booked 6-12 months in advance. January and March dates typically fill 3-6 months out. December and April offer better last-minute availability. For the best selection of dates and accommodations, we recommend booking as early as possible. Deposits are typically 50% at booking.

Yes! Most operators welcome snowboarders and splitboarders. Some have dedicated snowboard weeks or groups. Terrain selection may be adjusted for mixed groups. Splitboard-specific trips are available at certain operators. Let us know your preference when booking so we can match you with the best program.

Helicopter groups typically consist of 4-5 skiers plus a guide. The helicopter carries the group to each run location. Lodges may host 20-44 total guests split into multiple groups based on ability. Private groups can be arranged for families or corporate retreats. Smaller boutique operations like Bella Coola host maximum 11 guests total.

Most packages include transfers from the nearest major airport or meeting point. CMH includes helicopter transfers to remote lodges. Mike Wiegele provides ground transfers from Kamloops. Some operators charge separately for transfers. We'll provide detailed logistics for your specific program and can arrange additional transportation as needed.
