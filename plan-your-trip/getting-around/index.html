Getting Around Canada

Navigating the World's Second-Largest Country

Canada is huge‚Äîthe second-largest country in the world. Getting from Toronto to Vancouver (4,400 km) is like traveling from London to Baghdad! Understanding your transportation options is essential for planning a successful trip.

For most visitors, a combination of flights between regions and rental cars for exploration works best.

üöó Rental Cars

Best for: Freedom, flexibility, rural areas, road trips

Why Rent a Car?

  • Essential for exploring national parks, small towns, scenic routes
  • Often the only way to reach remote attractions
  • Freedom to stop anywhere, anytime
  • Cost-effective for groups

Rental Tips

  • Book early: Especially summer in tourist areas (Banff, Vancouver)
  • Companies: Enterprise, Budget, Hertz, Avis widely available
  • Cost: $40-100+ CAD/day depending on vehicle and season
  • Age: Usually 21+ (under 25 may pay surcharge)
  • License: Valid home country license accepted for visits up to 3-6 months
  • Insurance: Check your credit card and home insurance for coverage

Driving Considerations

  • Winter tires: Required Oct 1 - Apr 30 in BC mountains, recommended elsewhere
  • Wildlife: Moose, deer, elk common‚Äîespecially dawn/dusk
  • Distances: Fill up regularly‚Äîgas stations can be far apart in rural areas
  • Speed limits: Generally 100-110 km/h on highways, strictly enforced
  • Right-hand traffic: Same as USA, opposite to UK/Australia

‚úàÔ∏è Domestic Flights

Best for: Covering long distances quickly

When to Fly

  • Between major regions (Vancouver ‚Üî Toronto, Calgary ‚Üî Montreal)
  • To remote destinations (Yellowknife, Churchill, northern communities)
  • When time matters more than cost

Airlines

  • Air Canada: Largest network, most routes
  • WestJet: Major competitor, good Western Canada coverage
  • Porter: Eastern Canada, flies from Toronto Billy Bishop
  • Flair/Swoop: Ultra low-cost carriers (extra fees for bags)
  • Regional: Calm Air, Canadian North for northern routes

Tips

  • Book early: 2-3 months ahead for best prices
  • Flexibility: Tuesday/Wednesday often cheapest
  • Connections: Toronto (YYZ), Calgary (YYC), Vancouver (YVR) are main hubs
  • Cost: $150-500+ one-way depending on route and timing

üöÇ Trains

Best for: Scenic journeys, relaxed travel, avoiding airports

VIA Rail

  • The Canadian: Toronto to Vancouver (4 days) - iconic scenic journey
  • Corridor: Toronto-Ottawa-Montreal-Quebec City - practical option
  • Ocean: Montreal to Halifax - scenic Maritime route
  • Churchill: Winnipeg to Churchill (2 days) - adventure travel

Rocky Mountaineer

  • Luxury scenic train through Canadian Rockies
  • Vancouver to Banff/Jasper routes
  • Daylight only‚Äîovernight in hotels
  • Premium experience: $2,000+ per person

Considerations

  • Speed: Not faster than flying‚Äîit's about the journey
  • Cost: Can be expensive, especially sleeper class
  • Booking: Book sleeper cabins well in advance
  • Canrailpass: Unlimited travel pass available for tourists

üöå Buses

Best for: Budget travel, city connections

Bus Companies

  • Greyhound: Discontinued most Canadian routes (2021)
  • FlixBus: Growing network in Ontario and Western Canada
  • Megabus: Toronto-Montreal, Toronto-Niagara routes
  • Rider Express: Western Canada (Vancouver to Winnipeg)
  • Maritime Bus: Atlantic Canada
  • Orleans Express: Quebec routes

When to Consider

  • Budget is primary concern
  • Short distances between cities
  • Don't want to drive or pay for rental
  • One-way journeys (avoid car rental drop-off fees)

üìè Key Distances to Know

Canada is vast! Some common routes:

  • Toronto ‚Üí Montreal: 540 km (5 hrs drive, 1 hr flight)
  • Vancouver ‚Üí Calgary: 970 km (10 hrs drive, 1.5 hr flight)
  • Calgary ‚Üí Banff: 130 km (1.5 hrs drive)
  • Toronto ‚Üí Vancouver: 4,400 km (4-5 hr flight, 4 days train)
  • Halifax ‚Üí Montreal: 1,250 km (13 hrs drive, 2 hr flight)
  • Whitehorse ‚Üí Yellowknife: No road! (flight only)

üí° Transportation Tips

  • Fly between regions, drive within: Best strategy for most trips
  • One-way rentals: Often available but may have drop-off fees
  • Book ahead: Summer rentals in BC/Alberta book up fast
  • City transit: Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver have excellent public transit
  • Uber/Lyft: Available in major cities
  • Gas: Prices vary by province‚ÄîAlberta cheapest, BC most expensive
