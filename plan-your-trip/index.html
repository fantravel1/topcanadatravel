FAQ

Common Questions

It depends on your nationality. Citizens of visa-exempt countries (USA, UK, EU, Australia, Japan, South Korea, etc.) need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) costing $7 CAD. US citizens only need a valid passport. Other nationalities require a visitor visa. Apply through the official IRCC website.
It depends on what you want to do. Summer (June-August) is best for hiking, wildlife, and city exploration. Fall (September-October) offers beautiful foliage. Winter (December-March) is ideal for skiing and Northern Lights. Spring (April-May) brings mild weather and cherry blossoms in Vancouver.
Yes, Canada is very safe for tourists. It consistently ranks among the world's safest countries. Standard precautions apply in cities. In wilderness areas, be aware of wildlife (bears, moose) and weather conditions. Always carry travel insurance and check conditions for remote areas.
Yes, travel insurance is highly recommended. Canada has no public healthcare for visitors. A simple ER visit can cost $500-1,000+ CAD, and hospital stays run $3,000+ per day. Comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies is essential.
