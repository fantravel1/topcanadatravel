The Rideau Canal is a remarkable feat of 19th-century engineering and North America's oldest continuously operated canal system. Stretching 202 km from Ottawa to Kingston, this UNESCO World Heritage Site transforms with the seasonsâ€”a scenic boating route in summer, the world's largest naturally frozen skating rink in winter.

Built between 1826-1832, the canal was originally constructed as a military supply route, but today it's one of Canada's most beloved recreational destinations and Ottawa's defining landmark.

â„ï¸ Winter: The World's Largest Skating Rink

The Rideau Canal Skateway is a bucket-list Canadian experience:

  • Length: 7.8 km of groomed ice (equivalent to 90 Olympic rinks!)
  • Season: Usually January to early March, weather dependent
  • Cost: Completely FREE! Skate rentals available nearby
  • Winterlude: Festival in February with ice sculptures, concerts, and more
  • BeaverTails: Must-try fried dough pastries sold along the canal
  • Change huts: Heated rest stops every few hundred metres

Navigate the same waters boats have traveled for nearly 200 years:

  • Through-trip: Ottawa to Kingston takes 3-5 days by boat
  • Locks operation: Mid-May to mid-October (Parks Canada)
  • Lock fees: Single lockage from $1.60, day passes available
  • Boat rentals: Houseboats, kayaks, canoes available along route
  • Swimming: Several beaches along the canal system
  • Fishing: Bass, pike, walleyeâ€”license required
