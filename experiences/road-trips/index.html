Canada was made for road trips. With the world's second-largest landmass, dramatic landscapes from coast to coast, and some of the most scenic highways on Earth, there's no better way to experience Canada than behind the wheel.

From the glacier-fed turquoise lakes of the Icefields Parkway to the dramatic Atlantic cliffs of the Cabot Trail, from rainforest-lined BC highways to the endless Prairie horizons, Canada's road trips offer adventures for every season and style.

üèîÔ∏è The Icefields Parkway: Canada's #1 Drive

Often called the world's most beautiful highway, the Icefields Parkway deserves its own spotlight:

  • Route: Lake Louise to Jasper (or reverse)
  • Highlights: Peyto Lake, Columbia Icefield, Athabasca Falls, endless glaciers
  • Wildlife: Bears, elk, mountain goats, bighorn sheep
  • Stops: 100+ viewpoints and pullouts
  • Tip: Start early, go slow, stop often‚Äîrushing this drive is a mistake!
  • Parks pass: Required for both Banff and Jasper National Parks

Road Trip Essentials

Rental Car Tips

Book in advance for summer. Consider 4WD/AWD for mountain routes. One-way rentals available but pricey. Check insurance coverage carefully.

Fuel Planning

Fill up in towns‚Äîgas stations are sparse in mountains and north. Some routes have 200+ km between stations. Track fuel range carefully.

Seasonal Driving

Winter tires required Oct-Apr on many BC/Alberta routes. Summer construction common. Check road conditions before mountain drives.

Wildlife Safety

Animals on roads are common, especially dawn/dusk. Slow down, don't stop in traffic lanes, and never approach wildlife.

üí° Road Trip Tips

  • Start early: Best light for photos, fewer crowds at viewpoints, wildlife more active.
  • Don't rush: Plan fewer kilometers than you think‚Äîstops are the best part!
  • Download offline maps: Cell coverage is spotty in mountains and remote areas.
  • Pack snacks: Remote stretches may have limited food options.
  • Book accommodations: Popular routes book up fast in summer‚Äîreserve ahead.
  • Check road conditions: DriveBC, 511 Alberta, etc. for real-time updates.
