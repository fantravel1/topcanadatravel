Canada offers some of the world's best whale watching experiences, with three distinct coastlines and a staggering diversity of species. Whale Species in Canada

Orcas (Killer Whales)

BC's resident and transient pods. See them from Vancouver Island year-round. Highly social and intelligent.

Best: Vancouver Island, BC | When: May-October peak

Humpback Whales

Acrobatic giants known for breaching and tail slaps. Found on both coasts during feeding season.

Best: BC, Newfoundland, Maritimes | When: May-October

Blue Whales

Largest animals ever to exist! Up to 30m long. St. Lawrence has one of the best viewing areas in the world.

Best: Tadoussac, Quebec | When: August-October

Beluga Whales

White whales known as "canaries of the sea." Resident in St. Lawrence; thousands migrate to Churchill each summer.

Best: Churchill, Tadoussac | When: June-September

Gray Whales

Annual Pacific migration passes Vancouver Island. Best seen during spring migration northward.

Best: Tofino, BC | When: March-April

North Atlantic Right Whale

Critically endangered (fewer than 350). Bay of Fundy is a key feeding ground. Rare and special sighting.

Best: Bay of Fundy | When: August-October

üìÖ Whale Watching Calendar

  • Year-round: Orcas in BC (resident pods)
  • March-April: Gray whale migration, Tofino
  • May-October: Peak season most locations
  • July-August: Beluga season in Churchill
  • August-September: Blue whales in Tadoussac, right whales in Bay of Fundy
  • September-October: Fall migration, fewer crowds, still excellent viewing

üí° Whale Watching Tips

  • Book early: Popular tours sell out, especially Churchill belugas and peak summer.
  • Dress warm: It's always colder on the water‚Äîbring layers even in summer.
  • Motion sickness: Take medication before boarding if you're prone to seasickness.
  • Camera ready: Waterproof bag for gear. Whales surface quickly!
  • Zodiac vs. larger boat: Zodiacs get closer; larger boats are more stable.
  • Sunrise/sunset: Often calmer seas and beautiful light for photography.
