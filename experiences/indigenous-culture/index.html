Canada's lands are the traditional territories of Indigenous peoples who have lived here for thousands of years. As travelers, we have the privilege of visiting these lands and the responsibility to learn, respect, and support Indigenous communities and their ongoing connection to the land.

Indigenous tourism offers some of Canada's most meaningful travel experiences. From ancient totem poles in British Columbia to Inuit art in the Arctic, from pow wows on the Prairies to traditional crafts in Quebec, Indigenous-led experiences connect visitors with living cultures that have shaped this land for over 15,000 years.

When you choose Indigenous tourism, you support communities directly, learn authentic stories from those who live them, and contribute to cultural preservation and economic self-determination.

Canada's Indigenous Peoples

First Nations

Over 600 distinct nations

First Nations peoples have diverse cultures, languages, and traditions across every region of Canada. From the coastal Haida and Squamish to the Plains Cree and Blackfoot to the Haudenosaunee in the east, each nation has unique art, stories, and ways of life.

Regions: All of Canada

Inuit

Arctic peoples

Inuit are the Indigenous peoples of Canada's Arctic. Their culture is deeply connected to the northern environment‚Äîice, sea mammals, and the changing seasons. Inuit art, particularly sculpture and printmaking, is world-renowned.

Regions: Nunavut, Nunavik (Quebec), NWT, Labrador

M√©tis

Distinct Indigenous nation

The M√©tis are a distinct Indigenous nation with their own culture, language (Michif), and traditions. Emerging from the fur trade era, M√©tis culture blends Indigenous and European heritage into something uniquely its own.

Regions: Primarily Prairie provinces, Ontario
