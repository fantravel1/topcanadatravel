ğŸ· Canadian Wine Regions

Canada's wine industry has earned international acclaim, producing everything from bold reds to the world's best ice wine:

Okanagan Valley, BC

200+ wineries, 84 grape varieties. Known for Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Riesling, and Syrah. Desert-like climate creates concentrated flavors.

Niagara Peninsula, ON

World's premier ice wine region. Also excellent Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and sparkling. 100+ wineries within drive of Toronto.

Prince Edward County, ON

Cool-climate wines, limestone terroir. Boutique producers, Pinot Noir specialists. Charming small-town wine country.

Annapolis Valley, NS

Unique "Tidal Bay" appellation. Crisp, coastal whites. Emerging sparkling wine scene. Beautiful bay-side tasting rooms.

Canada is the world's largest producer of ice wineâ€”a luxurious dessert wine made from grapes frozen naturally on the vine.

  • How it's made: Grapes harvested at -8Â°C or colder, pressed while frozen
  • Taste: Intensely sweet, concentrated flavors of apricot, honey, tropical fruit
  • Best varieties: Vidal (most common), Riesling, Cabernet Franc
  • Where: Niagara Peninsula and Okanagan Valley
  • Price: $30-100 CAD for 375ml (half bottle)
  • Pairing: Foie gras, blue cheese, fruit desserts, or alone as dessert

Tip: Visit in January for ice wine festivals when harvest happens!

Must-Visit Food Markets

St. Lawrence Market, Toronto

Named world's best food market. 120+ vendors selling peameal bacon sandwiches, cheese, produce since 1803.

Granville Island Public Market, Vancouver

Iconic waterfront market with local seafood, artisan foods, and incredible people-watching.

Jean-Talon Market, Montreal

Quebec's largest outdoor market. Local cheeses, maple products, seasonal produce, and prepared foods.

Halifax Seaport Farmers' Market

North America's oldest continuously operating market (since 1750). Fresh seafood, local crafts, Saturday mornings.

ByWard Market, Ottawa

Historic market since 1826. Beaver tails, Ottawa bagels, local produce, and lively outdoor cafes.
