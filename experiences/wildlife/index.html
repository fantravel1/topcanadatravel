Frequently Asked Questions

Churchill, Manitoba is known as the "Polar Bear Capital of the World." Located on Hudson Bay, it's the most accessible place on Earth to see polar bears in the wild. Peak season is October-November when 900+ bears gather along the coast waiting for the bay to freeze. Specialized tundra vehicles allow safe viewing. Tours typically cost $4,000-8,000 for 5-7 day packages including flights from Winnipeg. Churchill also offers beluga whale watching in July-August and Northern Lights viewing.

Whale watching seasons vary by region: Vancouver Island/BC coast ‚Äî orcas year-round (peak May-Sept), humpbacks April-October. Tadoussac, Quebec ‚Äî belugas and blue whales May-October. Bay of Fundy ‚Äî right whales and humpbacks June-October. Tofino ‚Äî gray whale migration March-April. Churchill ‚Äî beluga congregation July-August with 3,000+ whales. Summer offers the best weather and conditions across most regions.

British Columbia offers the best grizzly viewing: Knight Inlet (boat-based viewing from lodges), Great Bear Rainforest (remote wilderness lodges), Khutzeymateen (BC's only grizzly sanctuary, permit required), and Bella Coola valley. In the Rockies, Jasper and Banff have grizzlies though sightings are less predictable. Peak season is August-October during salmon runs when bears congregate at rivers, offering spectacular feeding photography opportunities.

Costs vary significantly: Day tours (whale watching, bear viewing) run $100-300 CAD. Polar bear packages in Churchill cost $4,000-8,000+ for 5-7 days including flights, hotels, and guided tours. Grizzly bear lodges in BC range $3,000-6,000 for 3-4 nights all-inclusive. Budget option: Self-guided wildlife viewing in national parks ‚Äî just pay entry fees ($11/day or $145/year pass) with good chances of seeing elk, deer, bears, bighorn sheep, and more.

Wildlife viewing is safe when done responsibly. On guided tours, experienced operators maintain safe distances and know animal behavior. In national parks: maintain 100m from bears/wolves, 30m from other large animals. Never approach, feed, or attract wildlife. Carry bear spray in bear country and know how to use it. Make noise while hiking. Store food properly. Most incidents occur when people get too close for photos or leave food accessible. Follow park guidelines and your trip will be safe and memorable.
