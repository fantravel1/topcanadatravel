Frequently Asked Questions

Whistler Blackcomb is consistently ranked #1 in North America with 8,171 acres and incredible variety. For scenery, Lake Louise is unmatched. Revelstoke has the most vertical drop (1,713m). Mont-Tremblant is the best in Eastern Canada. The "best" depends on your priorities: terrain, powder, scenery, or convenience.

Most resorts operate late November through April. Banff Sunshine often opens November 1st and closes in late May. Whistler runs into June on the glacier. Peak season is January-March with the most reliable snow. Christmas and February breaks are busiest (and priciest). For powder and fewer crowds, try January or March.

Lift tickets: $80-180 CAD/day (Whistler is $200+ peak). Rentals: $50-80/day. Lessons: from $150 half-day. A budget ski day runs $150-200. Multi-day passes save significantly ‚Äî the Ikon Pass (Banff resorts, Tremblant) and Epic Pass (Whistler) offer great value for multiple visits.

Yes! All major resorts have excellent beginner terrain and ski schools. Whistler, Lake Louise, and Tremblant have dedicated learning areas. Many resorts offer "learn to ski" packages. Big White and Sun Peaks are particularly beginner-friendly with wide groomed runs and a welcoming atmosphere.
