Discover Montreal

Montreal is a city unlike any other in North America. As Canada's second-largest city and the world's second-largest French-speaking city after Paris, Montreal blends European sophistication with North American energy. The city pulses with creativity, from its renowned festival scene to its vibrant street art and thriving culinary landscape.

Walk the cobblestone streets of Old Montreal, where 17th-century architecture meets trendy restaurants and boutiques. Climb Mount Royal for panoramic city views. Experience world-class festivals that transform the city into a celebration of music, comedy, and culture. And discover why Montreal is considered one of the world's great food cities.

The best time to visit Montreal is June to September for warm weather and world-famous festivals including Jazz Fest (late June-early July), Just for Laughs (July), and Osheaga (August). Winter (December-February) offers holiday markets, ice skating, and a magical snowy atmosphere, though temperatures drop to -10¬∞C to -20¬∞C. The shoulder seasons of May and October offer pleasant weather with fewer crowds.

While Montreal is officially French-speaking and French is the primary language, the city is highly bilingual. Most people in tourist areas, restaurants, and hotels speak English fluently. Learning basic French phrases like "Bonjour" (hello), "Merci" (thank you), and "S'il vous pla√Æt" (please) is appreciated but not necessary. Montreal has excellent public transit. The Metro (subway) has 4 lines serving 68 stations, and an extensive bus network covers the rest. The STM operates both with a single fare ($3.75 CAD, day pass $11). BIXI bike-sharing is popular May-November with 900+ stations city-wide. Old Montreal, downtown, and the Plateau are very walkable. Montreal is one of Canada's most affordable major cities. Budget travelers can find hostels from $40-60/night, and hotels from $120-200/night.
