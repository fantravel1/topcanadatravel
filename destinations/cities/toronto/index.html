About Toronto

Toronto is Canada's largest city and one of North America's most multicultural destinations. Home to over 2.9 million people speaking 200+ languages, it's a vibrant metropolis where world-class culture, diverse cuisine, and iconic attractions come together.

The city skyline is dominated by the CN Tower, standing 553 meters tall and offering breathtaking views. Beyond the tower, Toronto boasts world-renowned museums, thriving arts districts, and neighborhoods that feel like miniature cities within the city.

Frequently Asked Questions

The best time to visit Toronto is June to September when temperatures are warm (20-27¬∞C) and outdoor festivals abound, including TIFF in September. Winter is cold but great for indoor attractions, holiday markets, and lower hotel prices.
Plan for 3-5 days to experience Toronto's highlights. Three days covers the CN Tower, downtown, and major museums. Five days allows exploration of diverse neighborhoods and a day trip to Niagara Falls (just 1.5 hours away).
Toronto is moderately expensive. Budget travelers can manage on $100-150 CAD/day with hostels and casual dining. Mid-range travelers should budget $200-300 CAD/day. The CityPASS saves up to 40% on major attractions.
