Niagara Falls, Ontario

The World's Most Famous Waterfall

üíß 750,000 Gallons/Second üö¢ Boat Tours üç∑ Wine Country üåà Nightly Illumination

Niagara Falls needs no introduction‚Äîit's one of the most awe-inspiring natural wonders on Earth. Over 750,000 gallons of water thunder over the falls every second, creating a spectacle that has drawn visitors for centuries. The Canadian side offers the best views, facing directly toward Horseshoe Falls, the largest and most powerful of the three waterfalls.

Beyond the falls, discover world-class wineries in Niagara-on-the-Lake, enjoy thrilling attractions on Clifton Hill, and experience the magic of the falls illuminated at night. It's a must-see Canadian destination that delivers on the hype.

üíß Falls Facts

  • Horseshoe Falls: 57 meters (187 ft) high, 670 meters wide‚Äîthe main Canadian falls
  • Water flow: 750,000 gallons per second during peak tourist hours
  • Age: About 12,000 years old, formed at the end of the last Ice Age
  • Erosion: The falls erode about 1 foot per year (slowed by water diversion)
  • Maid of the Mist: Boat tours have run since 1846!

üç∑ Niagara Wine Country

Just 20 minutes from the falls, the Niagara Peninsula is Canada's largest wine region with 100+ wineries:

  • Niagara-on-the-Lake: Charming historic town, main wine touring hub
  • Icewine: Canada's famous dessert wine‚ÄîNiagara produces 75% of world's supply
  • Wine route: Self-drive or take organized tours ($80-150 CAD)
  • Top wineries: Inniskillin, Peller Estates, Jackson-Triggs, Two Sisters
  • Shaw Festival: World-class theatre in Niagara-on-the-Lake (April-December)

üí° Insider Tips

  • Canadian side is better: You face the falls directly for the best views and photos.
  • Skip Clifton Hill restaurants: Better food on Queen Street or in Niagara-on-the-Lake.
  • Adventure Pass: Save money with combo tickets for multiple attractions.
  • Stay overnight: The falls are magical at night when illuminated‚Äîdon't just day trip!
  • Visit early or late: Crowds thin out before 9 AM and after 7 PM.
  • Bring a poncho: Provided on tours, but bring your own if walking the gorge paths.
