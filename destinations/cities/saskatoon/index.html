Saskatoon is Saskatchewan's largest city, a beautiful prairie gem where the South Saskatchewan River winds through the heart of downtown, crossed by seven stunning bridges. With more hours of sunshine than any other Canadian city and legendary Prairie sunsets, Saskatoon offers a unique blend of natural beauty, Indigenous heritage, and emerging culinary scene.

Named after the sweet saskatoon berries that grow wild across the Prairies, this is a city where you can kayak downtown, explore 6,000 years of Indigenous history at Wanuskewin, and dine at farm-to-table restaurants serving ingredients from nearby farms.

One of the longest-running archaeological sites in North America, Wanuskewin ("seeking peace of mind" in Cree) has been a gathering place for Northern Plains peoples for over 6,000 years. Today it offers:

  • Plains bison herd returned to ancestral lands
  • Traditional dance performances and storytelling
  • Authentic Indigenous cuisine at the restaurant
  • Tipi camping under the Prairie stars
  • Guided archaeological tours and workshops

Frequently Asked Questions

Saskatoon is known as the 'City of Bridges' for its seven beautiful bridges spanning the South Saskatchewan River. It's also famous for saskatoon berries, Wanuskewin Heritage Park, stunning Prairie sunsets, and a vibrant farm-to-table food scene.

The best time to visit is June to August when temperatures are warm, festivals are running, and saskatoon berries are in season. The city enjoys more sunshine than anywhere else in Canada!

Saskatoon berries are a sweet, purple berry native to the Canadian Prairies. They look similar to blueberries but have a unique almond-like flavor. The city is named after them. Try them in pies, jams, and wine during berry season (July-August).
