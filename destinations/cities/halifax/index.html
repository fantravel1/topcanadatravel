Explore Halifax Neighborhoods

The bustling heart of Halifax with the boardwalk, cruise terminal, shops, restaurants, and Alexander Keith's historic brewery.

Frequently Asked Questions

The best time to visit Halifax is June to September when temperatures are warm (18-23¬∞C), outdoor festivals are in full swing, and whale watching is at its peak. July and August are busiest with cruise ships. September offers beautiful fall colors and the Atlantic Film Festival. Shoulder seasons (May and October) offer lower prices and fewer crowds.

Peggy's Cove is 43 km (about 45 minutes) southwest of Halifax via Highway 333, known as the Lighthouse Route. You can drive yourself, take a guided tour (from $60 CAD), or use the Peggy's Cove Express shuttle in summer. Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.

Must-try Halifax seafood includes fresh Atlantic lobster (lobster rolls and whole lobster dinners), Digby scallops, fish and chips with local haddock, Maritime chowder, donairs (Halifax's official food), and local oysters. Visit the Halifax Seaport Farmers' Market for the freshest options.

Halifax is one of Canada's most popular cruise ports, welcoming over 300,000 cruise passengers annually. The cruise terminal is right downtown on the waterfront boardwalk, within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and the Maritime Museum. Popular shore excursions include Peggy's Cove and Lunenburg.

Winter in Halifax offers cozy Maritime charm. Enjoy the museums, brewery hopping, live Celtic music in pubs, and fresh winter seafood at lower prices. The Halifax Christmas Parade and New Year's celebrations are local highlights. Ski Martock is just 45 minutes away.
