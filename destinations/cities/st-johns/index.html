üßä Iceberg Alley Guide

Each spring, 10,000-year-old icebergs drift past Newfoundland from Greenland‚Äîone of the world's greatest natural spectacles:

  • When: Late April to early July (peak: May-June)
  • Where to see: Signal Hill, Cape Spear, boat tours from harbour
  • Iceberg beer: Taste beer brewed with 20,000-year-old iceberg water at Quidi Vidi Brewery
  • Boat tours: Get up close on iceberg tours ($75-100 CAD)
  • Track them: Check icebergfinder.com for current locations

üêü Get Screeched In!

Become an honorary Newfoundlander through this beloved local tradition:

  1. Take a shot of Screech rum (dark Jamaican rum)
  2. Kiss a cod fish (yes, a real one!)
  3. Recite: "Long may your big jib draw!"
  4. Receive your official Screeched-In certificate

Where: George Street bars nightly, Christian's Pub is the classic spot!

Explore St. John's Neighborhoods

Downtown & George Street

Colorful row houses, shops on Water Street, and legendary nightlife. The heart of St. John's culture and history.

Working fishing village with the famous Quidi Vidi Brewery. Walk "The Gut" and see traditional Newfoundland life.

Historic heights with the best views. Hike the North Head Trail or visit Johnson Geo Centre underground.

The Battery

Charming houses clinging to cliffs at the harbour entrance. Dramatic scenery and local character.

Frequently Asked Questions

Iceberg season runs from late April through June, with peak viewing in May and early June. Icebergs drift south from Greenland along 'Iceberg Alley.' Best viewing spots are Signal Hill, Cape Spear, and boat tours from St. John's harbour.

A Screech-In is where visitors become 'honorary Newfoundlanders.' You take a shot of Screech rum, kiss a cod fish, recite a Newfoundland phrase, and receive a certificate. George Street bars perform Screech-Ins nightly!

Jellybean Row refers to the brightly painted row houses in downtown St. John's. The tradition dates back centuries when fishermen painted their houses in bright colors to identify them from the sea in fog. They represent Newfoundland's vibrant spirit.

George Street has more bars per square foot than any other street in North America! This pedestrian street features 30+ pubs, live music every night (especially traditional Newfoundland folk), and Screech-In ceremonies.
