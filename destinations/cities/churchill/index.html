üêª‚Äç‚ùÑÔ∏è Polar Bear Viewing

Churchill is the world's best place to see wild polar bears:

  • Season: Mid-October to mid-November (peak)
  • Why here: Bears gather waiting for Hudson Bay ice to form for seal hunting
  • How: Tundra buggies‚Äîelevated vehicles designed for safe bear viewing
  • Numbers: 900+ bears in the region; you'll see dozens
  • Tours: Multi-day packages required ($5,000-10,000+)
  • Book ahead: 12-18 months in advance for peak season!

üêã Beluga Whale Encounters

Churchill's summer secret‚Äîthousands of friendly belugas:

  • Season: July and August
  • Numbers: 3,000+ belugas migrate to the Churchill River estuary
  • Experience: Kayak, paddleboard, snorkel, or boat among them
  • Behavior: Belugas are incredibly curious and approach humans!
  • Sound: Known as "sea canaries" for their singing
  • Cost: Tours from $150-400 depending on activity

üåå Northern Lights

Churchill is one of the world's best aurora viewing locations:

  • Season: January to March (darkest, clearest skies)
  • Location: Directly under the auroral oval
  • Activity: 300+ nights of aurora per year
  • Combine with: Dog sledding, igloo building, wildlife tracking
  • Temperature: Very cold! -25¬∞C to -35¬∞C common
  • Tours: Heated viewing domes and guided excursions available

‚úàÔ∏è Getting to Churchill

Churchill is remote‚Äîno roads connect it to the rest of Canada:

  • By air: Calm Air and Canadian North fly from Winnipeg (2 hours)
  • By train: VIA Rail from Winnipeg (2 days, scenic adventure!)
  • Cost: Flights $800-1,500 return depending on season
  • Packages: Most visitors book all-inclusive tours that include flights
  • No driving: Churchill has roads within town, but no highway connection
