Charlottetown, PEI

Birthplace of Canadian Confederation

Charlottetown is where Canada was born. This charming waterfront capital of Prince Edward Island hosted the 1864 Charlottetown Conference that led to Canadian Confederation. Today, this walkable city of Victorian architecture, tree-lined streets, and warm Maritime hospitality is your gateway to PEI's red sand beaches, Anne of Green Gables magic, and legendary lobster suppers.

With a population of just 40,000, Charlottetown offers big experiences in a small-town package‚Äîworld-class theatre, historic sites, exceptional dining, and the friendliest people you'll meet anywhere in Canada.

Day Trips from Charlottetown

Cavendish & North Shore

Anne of Green Gables sites, PEI National Park beaches, red sand dunes, and family attractions. The island's most visited area (40 min).

Victoria-by-the-Sea

Tiny, charming village with chocolate shops, theatre, and lighthouse. Perfect half-day trip (30 min).

North Rustico & Fishing Villages

Working fishing harbours, lobster boats, and authentic Maritime culture. Fresh seafood direct from fishermen.

Points East Coastal Drive

Lighthouses, secluded beaches, seal watching, and the Confederation Trail for cycling. Less touristy PEI.
