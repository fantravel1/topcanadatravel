Discover Calgary

Calgary is a dynamic city where western heritage meets modern ambition. As the gateway to the Canadian Rockies, most visitors pass through en route to Banff and Jasper ‚Äî but this energetic city deserves more than just a layover.

With 333 days of sunshine annually (Canada's sunniest major city), a vibrant downtown, world-class dining scene, and the legendary Calgary Stampede, there's plenty to explore. The stunning Rocky Mountain skyline backdrop reminds you that wilderness is always close by.

Frequently Asked Questions

Banff is only 130 km (80 miles) west of Calgary ‚Äî about 1.5 hours by car via the Trans-Canada Highway (Hwy 1). The drive is scenic with mountain views appearing after about 45 minutes. Brewster Express and other shuttles run between Calgary Airport and Banff multiple times daily (around $70 one-way). Many visitors fly into Calgary specifically to access the Rockies, making it the primary gateway to Banff, Lake Louise, and Jasper.

The Calgary Stampede is the "Greatest Outdoor Show on Earth" ‚Äî a 10-day festival every July (typically first two weeks). It features the world's largest rodeo with $2 million in prizes, chuckwagon races, grandstand shows, concerts, midway rides, agricultural exhibits, and western culture celebrations. Over 1 million visitors attend annually. Gate admission is around $23, with extra costs for rodeo seats ($50-150), concerts, and rides. The entire city embraces western culture ‚Äî even banks have employees in cowboy hats!

Best time depends on your interests: July for the Calgary Stampede and peak summer weather (20-25¬∞C). June-September for pleasant outdoor activities and access to mountain parks. December-March for skiing at nearby resorts (Banff Sunshine, Lake Louise, Nakiska). Calgary experiences Chinook winds in winter ‚Äî warm Pacific air that can raise temperatures 20¬∞C in hours. The city has 333 sunny days/year, so even winter has bright skies.

Calgary is moderately priced compared to Vancouver or Toronto. A major advantage: Alberta has no provincial sales tax (only 5% GST), making shopping cheaper than BC or Ontario. Hotels range $100-200/night mid-range (much higher during Stampede ‚Äî book early!). Meals cost $15-30 for casual dining. The downtown C-Train is free in the central core. Budget $150-200 CAD/day for comfortable travel including accommodation, meals, and activities.

Calgary Airport (YYC) is 20 km northeast of downtown. Options: Taxi/Uber costs $40-50 CAD, takes 25-35 minutes. Route 300 bus connects to downtown via CTrain for $11.50 (takes 45 minutes). Car rental is recommended if heading to the Rockies ‚Äî all major companies at the airport. Tip: If going directly to Banff, book a Brewster Express shuttle ($70, 2 hours) instead of renting a car you won't need in the mountains.
