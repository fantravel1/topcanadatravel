FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning your trip to Canada.

The best time to visit Canada depends on your interests. Summer (June-August) offers warm weather ideal for hiking, wildlife viewing, and city exploration. Winter (December-March) is perfect for skiing, Northern Lights viewing, and winter festivals like Quebec's Winter Carnival. Fall (September-October) provides stunning foliage colors, while Spring (April-May) offers mild temperatures and fewer crowds.
It depends on your nationality. Citizens of visa-exempt countries (including USA, UK, EU, Australia, Japan, South Korea) need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) costing $7 CAD. Other nationalities require a visitor visa. The eTA can be obtained online in minutes and is valid for 5 years or until your passport expires. US citizens only need a valid passport.
Budget travelers can explore Canada for $80-120 CAD/day using hostels, public transport, and cooking meals. Mid-range travelers should budget $150-250 CAD/day for comfortable hotels, restaurant meals, and attractions. Luxury travelers can expect $400+ CAD/day for premium hotels and exclusive experiences. Major costs include accommodation (40%), transportation (25%), food (20%), and attractions (15%).
The best places to see Northern Lights in Canada are: Yellowknife (Northwest Territories) - the Aurora Capital with 240+ viewing nights per year; Whitehorse (Yukon) - great accessibility and aurora packages; Churchill (Manitoba) - combine aurora with polar bear viewing; and northern Alberta, Saskatchewan, and Manitoba. Best viewing is from September to March on clear, dark nights away from city lights.
Canada's top must-see destinations include: Banff & Jasper National Parks (stunning Rocky Mountain scenery), Toronto (CN Tower, diverse neighborhoods), Vancouver (where mountains meet ocean), Niagara Falls (iconic waterfalls), Montreal & Quebec City (French-Canadian culture), Whistler (world-class skiing), and Yellowknife (Northern Lights capital). For first-timers, a Toronto-Niagara-Montreal trip or a Banff-Jasper road trip are excellent introductions.
