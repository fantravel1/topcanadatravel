The Beluga Whales of Churchill

Every summer, something magical happens in the waters off Churchill, Manitoba. Over 3,000 beluga whales ‚Äî those unmistakable white whales known as "sea canaries" for their extraordinary vocalizations ‚Äî migrate to the warm, shallow waters of the Churchill River estuary. This is the most accessible beluga whale viewing experience anywhere in the world.

The belugas arrive in late June and their numbers peak through July and August. They come to the estuary to feed on capelin and other small fish concentrated by the mixing of river and ocean waters. The shallow, relatively warm waters also serve as a nursery for calves ‚Äî you will often see mothers swimming with small grey babies tucked close beside them.

What makes Churchill's beluga experience truly special is the whales' remarkable curiosity toward humans. Unlike most marine mammals that shy away from boats and people, belugas actively approach kayaks, boats, and even snorkelers. They are gregarious, playful, and seemingly delighted by human interaction ‚Äî making encounters feel genuinely reciprocal.

Sea Canaries

Belugas are one of the most vocal whale species, producing an incredible range of clicks, whistles, chirps, and squeals. Drop a hydrophone into the water and you will hear an underwater symphony. Their vocalization earned them the nickname "sea canaries" from early sailors. On a quiet day, you can sometimes hear them from above the surface.

Ways to Experience Belugas

Boat Tours

The most popular and accessible option. Zodiac and larger vessel tours take you into the heart of beluga pods. Whales regularly surface alongside and beneath the boat. Hydrophones let you listen to their songs. Half-day tours from $100+.

Kayaking with Belugas

Paddle among hundreds of belugas in a sea kayak ‚Äî an utterly surreal experience. The whales often swim directly beneath and alongside your kayak, sometimes nudging it gently. No prior kayaking experience needed for guided tours. From $150+.

Snorkeling with Belugas

For the most immersive encounter, don a drysuit and slip into the water with the whales. Belugas approach snorkelers with intense curiosity, swimming within arm's reach and making eye contact. A truly transformative wildlife experience. From $200+.

Stand-Up Paddleboarding

SUP offers a unique elevated perspective on the belugas while maintaining an intimate water-level connection. Watch white shapes glide beneath your board as whales investigate this unusual floating visitor. From $150+.

What to Expect

Whale Behaviour

Belugas are among the most social and curious whale species. In Churchill, they travel in pods of 10 to several hundred individuals. They are constantly communicating, playing, and investigating their surroundings. When they encounter boats or kayaks, they typically approach rather than flee ‚Äî surfacing nearby, swimming underneath, and sometimes hovering just below the surface to observe you observing them.

Pod Sizes and Sightings

On any given day during peak season, you may encounter pods ranging from a dozen to several hundred belugas. The Churchill River mouth and nearby coastline host the densest concentrations. Seeing belugas during a summer tour is essentially guaranteed ‚Äî the question is not whether you will see them, but how many hundreds you will see.

Best Experience Tip

Book a kayaking or snorkeling experience if you are physically able. While boat tours are wonderful, the in-water encounters are what make Churchill's belugas truly world-class. The whales interact with people in the water in ways they simply cannot from a boat. Many visitors describe it as one of the top wildlife experiences of their lives.

Photography Tips

  • Waterproof camera: A GoPro or waterproof housing is essential for snorkeling and kayaking
  • Polarizing filter: Cuts glare on the water surface to reveal whales below from a boat
  • Wide angle: Belugas come so close that a wide-angle lens often works better than telephoto
  • Underwater audio: If you have a waterproof audio recorder, capture beluga songs ‚Äî they are extraordinary
  • Video over photos: Whale encounters are dynamic ‚Äî video often captures the experience better than stills

Planning Your Visit

Getting to Churchill

Churchill has no road access. Fly from Winnipeg (2 hours) via Calm Air or Canadian North, or take the scenic VIA Rail train (2 days). Summer is the most pleasant season to visit, with long daylight hours and mild temperatures. Many operators offer multi-day packages including flights.

What to Bring

  • Layers: Summer temperatures range from 12-20¬∞C but can feel cooler on the water
  • Windproof jacket: Essential for boat tours and kayaking
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses: Long daylight hours mean extended UV exposure
  • Waterproof camera: For in-water experiences (drysuits and gear provided by operators)
  • Binoculars: Useful for shore-based whale watching from the port area
  • Bug spray: Mosquitoes and blackflies can be significant in July

Summer Bonus: Fewer Crowds, Lower Costs

Beluga season is Churchill's best-kept secret. While polar bear season (Oct-Nov) sells out over a year in advance and costs $5,000+, beluga experiences are more affordable (from $100 for half-day tours) and easier to book. Many wildlife experts consider the beluga experience equally or even more rewarding than polar bear viewing.
