About Assiniboine Park Zoo

The Assiniboine Park Zoo is one of Winnipeg's most beloved attractions, home to over 200 animal species set within the beautiful 1,100-acre Assiniboine Park. The zoo has been welcoming visitors since 1904 and underwent a transformative expansion in 2014 with the opening of the award-winning Journey to Churchill exhibit.

The zoo is renowned for its focus on northern and Arctic species, reflecting Manitoba's unique geography as a gateway to the sub-Arctic. Polar bears, Arctic wolves, muskoxen, and snowy owls are among the featured residents, many of which are rescue animals that cannot be returned to the wild.

Beyond the Arctic exhibits, the zoo features a nature playground that encourages children to explore and connect with the natural world, along with exhibits showcasing species from grasslands, boreal forests, and around the globe.

Journey to Churchill

The crown jewel of the zoo, Journey to Churchill is an immersive Arctic exhibit that recreates the landscapes of northern Manitoba. Watch polar bears swim underwater through a glass viewing tunnel, see harbour seals playing, and learn about Arctic conservation. The exhibit has won international awards for design and animal welfare.

Top Exhibits

Polar Bear Underwater Tunnel

Walk through an underwater glass tunnel and watch polar bears swim directly overhead. One of the most unique wildlife viewing experiences in Canada.

Arctic Wildlife

See muskoxen, Arctic foxes, snowy owls, and wolves in naturalistic habitats that recreate Manitoba's northern landscapes.

Nature Playground

A massive nature-based playground where children can climb, explore, and discover. Features water play areas, climbing structures, and natural materials.

Grasslands & Gardens

Explore exhibits featuring bison, elk, and prairie wildlife, plus the adjacent Leo Chicken Fry English Garden and the Assiniboine Park Conservatory.

Best Time to Visit

Polar bears are most active in cooler weather, making fall and winter excellent times to visit. The zoo is open year-round, and winter visits often mean fewer crowds and livelier polar bears. Summer offers the full nature playground experience and longer hours.

Getting There

The zoo is located within Assiniboine Park in the west end of Winnipeg, about 20 minutes from downtown.

By Transit

Winnipeg Transit route 66 serves Assiniboine Park. The bus stop is a short walk from the zoo entrance.

By Car

Free parking is available at the zoo. Enter from Corydon Avenue or Roblin Boulevard. The drive from downtown takes about 15-20 minutes.
