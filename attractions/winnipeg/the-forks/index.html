About The Forks

The Forks is Winnipeg's premier gathering place, located at the historic junction of the Red and Assiniboine rivers. For over 6,000 years, Indigenous peoples gathered at this confluence for trade, celebration, and sustenance. Today, it remains the heart of the city, drawing over 4 million visitors annually to its markets, river walks, cultural sites, and events.

The site was designated a National Historic Site of Canada in recognition of its extraordinary significance to Indigenous peoples and the fur trade. Archaeological digs have uncovered artifacts spanning millennia, telling the story of continuous human gathering at this natural meeting point.

Today, The Forks seamlessly blends history with modern urban life. The restored rail buildings house the Forks Market, a vibrant food hall and artisan marketplace. The river walk stretches along both rivers, connecting to the Canadian Museum for Human Rights, the Children's Museum, and the historic St. Boniface neighbourhood across the Esplanade Riel pedestrian bridge.

The Forks Market

Housed in renovated rail buildings, the Forks Market is a food lover's paradise with diverse vendors offering everything from Indigenous bannock to Vietnamese pho, artisan cheeses, fresh produce, and Winnipeg's famous honey dill sauce. The market also features local artisans, galleries, and specialty shops.

Things to Do at The Forks

River Walk & Trails

Stroll along scenic pathways by both rivers. In summer, enjoy patios and buskers. In winter, walk the cleared paths to warming huts along the frozen river trail.

Nestaweya River Trail

The world's longest naturally frozen skating trail at over 9 km. Skate rentals available on-site. Features architecturally designed warming huts by international artists.

The Common

A modern food hall and gathering space with restaurants, a beer hall, and event space. Perfect for evening dining with river views.

Oodena Celebration Circle

An outdoor amphitheatre and gathering space that serves as a sundial and references the site's 6,000-year history. Host to cultural celebrations and events.

Seasonal Tip

Visit in winter (January-March) for the unforgettable experience of skating on the frozen rivers. The warming huts art and architecture competition brings creative structures to the trail each year, making it a unique open-air gallery.

Getting There

The Forks is centrally located in downtown Winnipeg, easily accessible from all parts of the city.

By Transit

Multiple Winnipeg Transit routes serve The Forks. The site is also accessible via the Southwest Transitway rapid transit corridor.

By Car

Large parkade and surface parking available on-site. Enter from Main Street or Waterfront Drive. Parking costs $4-8 CAD.

By Bike or Foot

Connected to Winnipeg's cycling network and river walk system. The Esplanade Riel pedestrian bridge provides a scenic connection from St. Boniface.
