About the Winnipeg Art Gallery & Qaumajuq

The Winnipeg Art Gallery (WAG) is one of Canada's oldest and largest public art galleries, founded in 1912. With over 27,000 works in its collection, it showcases Canadian, Indigenous, and international art across multiple gallery spaces in downtown Winnipeg.

In 2021, the gallery was transformed with the opening of Qaumajuq (pronounced KHOW-ma-juq), a purpose-built centre for Inuit art. The name means "it is bright" in Inuktitut, and the building lives up to its name with luminous galleries and an extraordinary visible vault that displays approximately 5,000 Inuit carvings in floor-to-ceiling glass cases.

Together, WAG-Qaumajuq holds the world's largest public collection of contemporary Inuit art, with over 14,000 Inuit artworks including carvings, prints, textiles, and drawings. The collection represents decades of relationships with Inuit communities and artists, making it an unparalleled resource for understanding the artistic traditions and contemporary expressions of the Canadian Arctic.

The Visible Vault

Qaumajuq's most breathtaking feature is the visible vault on the ground floor. Approximately 5,000 Inuit carvings are displayed in soaring glass cases, visible from the street through the building's glass facade. This radical approach to art storage allows visitors to see works that would normally be hidden away, creating an immersive experience unlike any other gallery in the world.

What to See

Qaumajuq Visible Vault

Floor-to-ceiling glass cases displaying approximately 5,000 Inuit carvings. An awe-inspiring space that reimagines how art can be stored and shared.

Inuit Art Galleries

Rotating exhibitions showcasing the breadth and depth of the Inuit art collection, from historical pieces to cutting-edge contemporary work by emerging artists.

Canadian Art Collection

Works spanning Canadian art history including important pieces by the Group of Seven, historical Manitoba art, and contemporary Canadian artists.

Rooftop Sculpture Garden

An outdoor sculpture terrace with views of downtown Winnipeg. Features rotating sculptural installations and is a peaceful space for reflection.

Visitor Tip

The gallery offers free admission on certain evenings. Check the website for current free days. Guided tours of Qaumajuq provide deeper context about Inuit art traditions and the stories behind the carvings in the visible vault.

Getting There

The gallery is located on Memorial Boulevard in downtown Winnipeg, within walking distance of the Manitoba Legislative Building and the Exchange District.

By Transit

Multiple Winnipeg Transit routes serve the Memorial Boulevard area. The gallery is easily accessible from most downtown bus stops.

By Car

Street parking and nearby parkades available. The gallery is at 300 Memorial Boulevard, a 5-minute drive from downtown hotels.

Walking

A pleasant 15-minute walk from The Forks via Broadway and Memorial Boulevard. The route passes the Manitoba Legislative Building and its beautiful grounds.
