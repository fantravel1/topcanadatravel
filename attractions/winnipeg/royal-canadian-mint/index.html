About the Royal Canadian Mint

The Royal Canadian Mint's Winnipeg facility is where the magic of coin production happens on a massive scale. This high-tech plant produces all of Canada's circulation coins and manufactures coins for over 75 countries worldwide, making it one of the most advanced minting facilities on the planet.

The Winnipeg facility opened in 1976, complementing the original Ottawa location (which focuses on collector coins and gold refining). Each year, the Winnipeg plant produces billions of coins using patented multi-ply plating technology that sets the global standard for coin manufacturing.

Interactive guided tours take visitors through the production process, from blank metal discs to finished coins. You will see high-speed presses stamping thousands of coins per minute, quality control systems, and the remarkable precision engineering that goes into every coin.

The Gold Bar Experience

The undisputed highlight of every tour is the chance to try lifting a real gold bar worth approximately $750,000 CAD. Weighing about 12.5 kilograms (28 pounds), the bar is secured in a display case with an opening for your hands. It is heavier than most people expect!

Tour Highlights

High-Speed Production

Watch coin presses stamp out thousands of coins per minute. The scale and speed of production is mesmerizing, with billions of coins produced annually.

Gold Bar Lifting

Try to lift a real gold bar worth about $750,000. The bar weighs 12.5 kg and is deceptively heavy for its size. A must-do photo opportunity.

Interactive Exhibits

Learn about coin design, anti-counterfeiting technology, and the history of Canadian currency through hands-on displays and multimedia presentations.

Boutique & Gift Shop

Browse an extensive collection of collector coins, medals, and mint-branded merchandise. Purchase limited edition coins and unique souvenirs.

Booking Tip

Tours run on weekdays and are most interesting when the production floor is operating (Monday to Friday). Book in advance during summer months. Photography is not permitted on the production floor for security reasons, but is allowed in the exhibit and boutique areas.

Getting There

The Mint is located in east Winnipeg, about 15 minutes from downtown by car.

By Car

Free parking available on-site. The facility is located at 520 Lagimodiere Boulevard, easily accessible from the Trans-Canada Highway and Regent Avenue.

By Transit

Winnipeg Transit routes serve the Lagimodiere Boulevard corridor. Check current schedules for the most direct route from your location.
