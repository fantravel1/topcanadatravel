About the Exchange District

The Exchange District is a 20-block National Historic Site in the heart of Winnipeg, home to one of the most intact collections of early 20th-century heritage architecture in North America. Over 150 buildings dating from the 1880s to 1920s line its streets, their ornate facades a testament to Winnipeg's boom years as the "Chicago of the North."

Named after the former Winnipeg Grain Exchange, this neighbourhood was once the commercial heart of western Canada. When Winnipeg's economic centre shifted, the buildings were preserved rather than demolished, creating a rare time capsule of turn-of-the-century urban architecture.

Today, the Exchange District is Winnipeg's cultural epicentre. Artists' studios fill former warehouses, independent galleries showcase local and international work, boutiques occupy ground-floor storefronts, and some of the city's best restaurants serve creative cuisine in beautifully restored spaces. The neighbourhood is also home to several theatres, live music venues, and Old Market Square, a public gathering space that hosts festivals and concerts.

First Fridays Art Walk

On the first Friday of every month, galleries throughout the Exchange District open their doors late with free admission, live music, and artist talks. It is the best way to experience the neighbourhood's creative energy and meet local artists.

Things to Do

Gallery Hopping

Dozens of galleries showcase everything from Indigenous art to contemporary photography. Many are artist-run and free to enter.

Heritage Walking Tours

Guided walking tours explore the district's architecture, history, and stories. Self-guided options available with the Exchange District BIZ app.

Dining & Nightlife

Home to Winnipeg's best restaurant scene, from farm-to-table dining to craft cocktail bars. The area comes alive at night with live music venues.

Old Market Square

The neighbourhood's outdoor living room hosts the Winnipeg Fringe Festival, Jazz Festival, and summer concerts. A perfect spot for people-watching year-round.

Film Location Favourite

The Exchange District's heritage streetscapes are a popular film location, standing in for early 1900s cities. Keep an eye out for film crews and look for the district in movies and TV shows set in historical periods.

Getting There

The Exchange District is in the heart of downtown Winnipeg, easily walkable from most downtown hotels and attractions.

By Transit

Multiple Winnipeg Transit routes serve the area. The Exchange District is a short walk from most downtown bus stops.

By Car

Street parking and parkades available throughout the district. Rates are reasonable, especially evenings and weekends. The area is very walkable once parked.

Walking

A 10-minute walk from The Forks and 15 minutes from the Winnipeg Art Gallery. The flat terrain makes it easy to explore on foot.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Exchange District is a 20-block National Historic Site in downtown Winnipeg featuring over 150 heritage buildings from the early 1900s. It is now the city's cultural hub with art galleries, boutiques, restaurants, theatres, and nightlife. It is free to explore on foot.

First Fridays is a monthly event where galleries throughout the Exchange District open their doors late with free admission, live music, and artist talks. It runs year-round on the first Friday of each month and is the best way to experience the creative energy of the neighbourhood.

The main streets of the Exchange District are generally safe, especially on weekends when restaurants and bars are busy. Like any urban area, use common sense and stick to well-lit, busier streets. The First Fridays art walk and festival evenings bring large, friendly crowds.

The Exchange District offers diverse dining from upscale farm-to-table restaurants to casual eateries. Popular spots include Deer + Almond for creative sharing plates, Clementine for brunch, and Peasant Cookery for locally sourced Canadian cuisine. The area also has excellent craft cocktail bars and brewpubs.
