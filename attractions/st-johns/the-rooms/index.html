The Rooms

Newfoundland's Premier Museum, Art Gallery & Archives

Visitor Information

Address 9 Bonaventure Ave, St. John's

Free Admission Wednesdays 6-9 PM
Facilities Museum, Gallery, Archives

Time Needed 2-3 hours

About The Rooms

The Rooms is Newfoundland and Labrador's largest public cultural space, bringing together the provincial museum, art gallery, and archives under one striking roof. Opened in 2005, this iconic building sits atop a hill overlooking St. John's Harbour, offering some of the best views in the city alongside world-class exhibitions.

The building's distinctive architecture was inspired by the traditional Newfoundland "fishing rooms"--the waterfront buildings where fishermen processed and stored their catch. This connection between the modern cultural institution and the province's fishing heritage is intentional and deeply symbolic.

Inside, you will find comprehensive exhibitions covering Newfoundland and Labrador's natural history, human history spanning thousands of years, Indigenous cultures, military history, and a vibrant art gallery featuring both local and international works. The Rooms is the essential cultural stop for understanding what makes this province unique.

Free Wednesday Evenings

Every Wednesday from 6 PM to 9 PM, admission to The Rooms is completely free. This is an excellent opportunity to explore the museum and gallery without cost, and the evening atmosphere is relaxed and uncrowded. It's one of the best free things to do in St. John's.

What to See

Provincial Museum

Explore Newfoundland's story from ancient geological formations through Indigenous cultures, European contact, the cod fishery, Confederation, and modern life. Hands-on exhibits and multimedia displays bring history alive.

Art Gallery

The province's premier art gallery features rotating exhibitions of contemporary and historical art, with a strong focus on Newfoundland and Labrador artists alongside national and international works.

Indigenous Exhibitions

Powerful exhibitions explore the cultures and histories of the Beothuk, Mi'kmaq, Innu, and Inuit peoples of Newfoundland and Labrador. The Beothuk exhibition is particularly moving and unique to The Rooms.

Harbour Views

The building's elevated position offers stunning panoramic views of St. John's Harbour, the Narrows, and Signal Hill. The fourth-floor windows provide one of the best vantage points in the city.

Key Exhibitions

The Rooms features both permanent and rotating exhibitions. Here are some of the highlights that define the museum experience.

Our People, Our Stories

The permanent museum exhibition tells the human story of Newfoundland and Labrador, from the earliest Indigenous peoples through European settlement, the cod fishery that shaped the culture, two World Wars, Confederation with Canada in 1949, and the cod moratorium of 1992 that transformed the province.

The Beothuk Exhibition

One of the most significant exhibitions in The Rooms tells the story of the Beothuk--the Indigenous people of Newfoundland who were driven to extinction by European colonization. The exhibition treats this tragic history with sensitivity and respect, and includes rare Beothuk artifacts found nowhere else in the world.

Provincial Archives

The archives hold millions of documents, photographs, maps, and records dating back to the 1600s. While primarily a research facility, the archives mount public exhibitions and are available by appointment for genealogical and historical research.

Plan Your Visit

Start on the top floor for the harbour views, then work your way down through the galleries. The gift shop on the main level has an excellent selection of Newfoundland art, books, and crafts--some of the best souvenirs in St. John's.

Getting There

The Rooms is located at 9 Bonaventure Avenue, on a hill above downtown St. John's. It's within easy walking distance of most downtown hotels and attractions.

On Foot

From Water Street downtown, walk uphill on Cochrane Street or Cathedral Street. The walk takes about 10-15 minutes and is moderately steep. The building is visible from much of downtown.

By Car

Free parking is available in The Rooms parking lot. Enter from Bonaventure Avenue. The lot can fill up on busy days and during special exhibitions.
