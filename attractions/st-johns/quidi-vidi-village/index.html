Quidi Vidi Village

A Historic Fishing Village in the Heart of St. John's

Visitor Information

Location Quidi Vidi, St. John's
Pronunciation "Kiddy Viddy"
Admission Free to explore
Key Attractions Brewery, Mallard Cottage, Studios
Distance from Downtown 5 min drive / 20 min walk
Time Needed 1-3 hours

About Quidi Vidi Village

Quidi Vidi Village (pronounced "Kiddy Viddy") is one of St. John's most enchanting secrets--a genuine historic fishing village tucked into a sheltered cove just minutes from downtown. Despite being within the city limits, Quidi Vidi feels like stepping back in time to a traditional Newfoundland outport, complete with colourful fishing stages, working boats, and the narrow gut (harbour entrance) that gives the village its dramatic character.

The village has been a fishing settlement for centuries, and while it has evolved to include a renowned craft brewery, award-winning restaurants, and artists' studios, it retains its authentic fishing village character. You will still see fishermen heading out through the gut in the morning, nets drying in the sun, and the timeless rhythm of a community connected to the sea.

Today, Quidi Vidi is a vibrant blend of old and new--traditional fishing culture alongside craft beer, fine dining, and contemporary art. It is one of the most unique and memorable experiences in St. John's.

Iceberg Beer at Quidi Vidi Brewery

Quidi Vidi Brewery is famous for its Iceberg Beer, brewed with water harvested from 20,000-year-old icebergs that drift past Newfoundland each spring. The brewery offers tours and tastings in a beautiful waterfront setting overlooking the gut. It is one of the most unique brewery experiences in Canada.

What to See & Do

Quidi Vidi Brewery

Tour the brewery and taste their famous Iceberg Beer, brewed with 20,000-year-old iceberg water. The taproom overlooks the gut with stunning views. Tours run daily in summer; check seasonal hours.

Mallard Cottage

Award-winning restaurant in one of North America's oldest wooden buildings (circa 1750). Chef Todd Chicken's menu celebrates hyper-local Newfoundland ingredients. Reservations essential.

Artists' Studios

The Quidi Vidi Village Plantation houses multiple artists' studios and craft shops. Watch local artisans at work creating pottery, jewellery, textiles, and paintings inspired by Newfoundland's landscape and culture.

The Gut

Walk along the dramatic narrow harbour entrance (the "gut") where fishing boats navigate between towering cliffs. It is one of the most photographed spots in St. John's and a working harbour at the same time.

The Village Experience

The best way to experience Quidi Vidi is simply to wander. The village is small enough to explore on foot in an hour, but captivating enough to spend an entire afternoon.

Walking the Gut

The gut--the narrow channel connecting Quidi Vidi harbour to the open Atlantic--is the heart of the village. Walking along its edges, you can watch fishing boats navigate the tight passage, see the dramatic cliffs on either side, and feel the power of the ocean surging through. On calm days, the water in the gut is mirror-still; on stormy days, waves crash through with tremendous force.

Fishing Heritage

Quidi Vidi is still a working fishing village. You will see traditional fishing stages (waterfront buildings for processing fish), boats moored in the harbour, and the daily rhythm of an outport community. In summer, fishermen head out for cod, capelin, and crab, continuing traditions that go back centuries.

Quidi Vidi Lake

Just above the village, Quidi Vidi Lake is the site of the Royal St. John's Regatta--the oldest continuing sporting event in North America (since 1825). The lake trail is a popular walking and running loop for locals.

Combine with Signal Hill

Quidi Vidi Village sits at the base of Signal Hill's north side. You can hike from Signal Hill down to Quidi Vidi via the Cuckold's Cove Trail, combining two of St. John's best attractions in one outing. The trail offers stunning coastal views.

Getting There

Quidi Vidi Village is just northeast of downtown St. John's, accessible by car, foot, or as part of a Signal Hill visit.

By Car

From downtown, follow King's Bridge Road north to Quidi Vidi Village Road. The drive takes about 5 minutes. Limited free parking is available in the village--arrive early on busy summer days.

On Foot

Walk from downtown via King's Bridge Road (about 20 minutes) or hike down from Signal Hill via the Cuckold's Cove Trail (30-40 minutes, moderate difficulty with steep sections).

From Signal Hill

If visiting Signal Hill, take the trail down the north side to Quidi Vidi Village. This combines two major attractions and offers excellent coastal scenery along the way.
