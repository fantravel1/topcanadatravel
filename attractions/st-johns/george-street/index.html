About George Street

George Street is the heart and soul of St. John's nightlife and one of the most unique entertainment districts in the world. This two-block pedestrian street in the centre of downtown packs more than 30 bars, pubs, and clubs into an incredibly small area--earning it the distinction of having the most bars per square foot in all of North America.

But George Street is far more than just a drinking strip. It is the centre of Newfoundland's legendary music scene, where traditional folk music, rock, indie, and everything in between pour out of every doorway every night of the week. The street embodies the famous Newfoundland hospitality--within minutes of arriving, you will feel like a local, invited to join in songs, conversations, and celebrations.

The street comes alive after 9 PM and stays lively well past midnight. Most bars do not charge cover, making it easy to hop between venues and discover your favourite spot. Whether you want traditional Newfoundland folk music, rock, dance, or a quiet pint with new friends, George Street has a pub for you.

The Screech-In Ceremony

No visit to George Street is complete without getting "Screeched In"--the beloved Newfoundland tradition where visitors become honorary Newfoundlanders. You take a shot of Screech rum, kiss a cod fish, recite "Long may your big jib draw!" and receive your official certificate. Christian's Pub is the classic spot, but many bars offer the ceremony nightly.

What to Experience

Live Traditional Music

Hear authentic Newfoundland folk music performed live every night. Fiddles, accordions, guitars, and bodhrans create the soundtrack of the island. Many pubs host open sessions where anyone can join in.

Screech-In Ceremony

Become an honorary Newfoundlander! Take a shot of Screech rum, kiss a cod, recite the oath, and receive your certificate. A fun, welcoming tradition that captures the spirit of Newfoundland.

George Street Festival

Every August, the entire street becomes a massive outdoor concert venue for six nights. Multiple stages host Canadian and international artists. One of the largest outdoor music festivals in North America.

Pub Crawl Culture

With 30+ bars in two blocks and most charging no cover, George Street is made for pub crawling. Start at one end and work your way through, discovering live music, characters, and stories at every stop.

Notable Bars & Venues

While the lineup changes over the years, these are some of the most beloved establishments on George Street.

  • Christian's Pub: The original Screech-In venue and a George Street institution. Traditional music and a true Newfoundland experience
  • Trapper John's: Named after the famous Newfoundland folk character. Live music, Screech-Ins, and a welcoming atmosphere
  • O'Reilly's: Irish-Newfoundland pub with excellent live music, traditional sessions, and good pub food
  • The Fat Cat: Blues and rock venue with some of the best live bands on the street
  • Bridie Molloy's: Irish pub with traditional music sessions and a warm, intimate atmosphere

Best Nights to Visit

Tuesday through Sunday nights have the best live music and atmosphere. Friday and Saturday are the busiest. Wednesday and Thursday are great for a more relaxed experience with excellent music and fewer crowds. Most pubs don't charge cover any night of the week.

George Street Festival

The George Street Festival, held annually in late July or early August, is one of the biggest events in St. John's and one of the largest outdoor music festivals in North America. For six consecutive nights, the entire street is transformed into an open-air concert venue.

Multiple stages host a diverse lineup of Canadian and international artists alongside beloved Newfoundland performers. Past headliners have included major Canadian and international acts. The festival draws tens of thousands of visitors and is a highlight of the St. John's summer calendar.

Festival Tips

  • Tickets: Individual night and full festival passes available. Book early--popular nights sell out
  • Accommodation: Book St. John's hotels months in advance during festival week
  • Weather: Bring a rain jacket--the festival goes on rain or shine
  • Age: The festival is 19+ (legal drinking age in Newfoundland)

Getting There

George Street is in the heart of downtown St. John's, running parallel to Water Street. It is easily walkable from any downtown hotel or accommodation.

Location

George Street runs between Adelaide Street and George Street East, one block south of Water Street. Look for the famous George Street arch marking the entrance to the pedestrian zone.

Getting Around

George Street is a pedestrian-only zone. Walk from your downtown hotel or take a taxi. If driving, there are several parking lots and on-street parking within a few blocks. Taxis are plentiful on and around George Street at night.

Most George Street bars do not charge cover on regular nights, making it easy and affordable to hop between venues. Some may charge cover for special events, featured bands, or during the George Street Festival.

The legal drinking age in Newfoundland and Labrador is 19. You will need valid photo ID (driver's licence or passport) to enter bars. This is strictly enforced on George Street.

George Street is generally very safe. The pedestrian-only zone is well-lit and monitored, especially on busy nights. As with any nightlife area, use common sense, watch your belongings, and arrange safe transportation home. Newfoundlanders are exceptionally friendly and welcoming.
