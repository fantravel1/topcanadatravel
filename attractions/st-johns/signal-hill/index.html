About Signal Hill

Rising dramatically above the Narrows at the entrance to St. John's Harbour, Signal Hill is one of the most iconic and historically significant sites in all of Canada. This windswept hill has been a strategic lookout for centuries, playing roles in military defence, communications history, and now serving as the most visited National Historic Site in Newfoundland and Labrador.

The site is best known for two landmark events: Cabot Tower was built in 1897 to commemorate the 400th anniversary of John Cabot's discovery of Newfoundland and Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee. Then in December 1901, Guglielmo Marconi made history by receiving the first transatlantic wireless signal here--three dots representing the letter "S" in Morse code--transmitted from Poldhu, Cornwall, England, over 3,400 kilometres away.

Today, Signal Hill offers visitors breathtaking panoramic views of St. John's Harbour, the colourful city below, the rugged Atlantic coastline, and on clear days, icebergs and whales. The site combines deep history, stunning natural beauty, and excellent hiking trails in one unforgettable destination.

Marconi's Historic Moment

On December 12, 1901, Guglielmo Marconi and his assistants flew a kite antenna from Signal Hill and received the first transatlantic wireless signal--three faint clicks representing the Morse code letter "S." This moment revolutionized global communications and proved that wireless signals could travel beyond the horizon.

Cabot Tower

Cabot Tower is the centrepiece of Signal Hill and one of the most recognizable landmarks in Newfoundland. Built between 1897 and 1900, this stone tower stands at the summit of Signal Hill and offers 360-degree views from its upper levels.

Inside the tower, exhibits tell the story of Signal Hill's military history, the development of communications from flag signals to wireless telegraphy, and the story of Marconi's groundbreaking experiment. The views from the tower are among the finest in eastern Canada, looking out over the harbour, the city, and the vast Atlantic Ocean.

Best Photo Spots

For the classic postcard shot of Cabot Tower with the harbour below, photograph from the lower parking area looking up. For the best harbour and city views, head to the top of the tower. Sunset is magical here--the tower glows golden against the Atlantic sky.

Hiking Trails

Signal Hill offers several excellent hiking trails that combine stunning coastal scenery with historical sites. Whether you want a gentle stroll or a more challenging hike, there is a trail for you.

North Head Trail

The most popular trail on Signal Hill, this 1.7-kilometre loop takes you along dramatic cliff edges with incredible views of the Atlantic Ocean, The Narrows, and the coastline. Watch for whales and icebergs in season. Allow 45-60 minutes.

Ladies' Lookout Trail

A shorter, easier walk leading to a panoramic viewpoint overlooking the harbour and city. Named for the women who historically watched from this spot for ships returning to port. Allow 20-30 minutes.

Gibbet Hill Trail

This trail connects Signal Hill to The Battery neighbourhood below. Moderate difficulty with steep sections. Excellent views of the colourful houses clinging to the cliffs. Allow 30-45 minutes one way.

  • Difficulty: Easy to moderate depending on trail
  • Footwear: Sturdy shoes recommended--trails can be muddy and windy
  • Safety: Stay on marked trails--cliffs are steep and unguarded
  • Wind: Expect strong winds at the summit year-round

Wildlife & Nature Watching

Iceberg Spotting (Apr-Jun)

Signal Hill is one of the best land-based spots in Newfoundland for viewing icebergs as they drift south along Iceberg Alley. Bring binoculars and check icebergfinder.com for current sightings.

Whale Watching (Jun-Sep)

Humpback whales frequently feed near the entrance to St. John's Harbour, visible from Signal Hill. You may also spot minke whales and occasionally orcas from the clifftop trails.

Seabirds

The cliffs support nesting seabirds including common murres, Atlantic puffins (visible offshore), black-legged kittiwakes, and northern gannets diving for fish.

Sunrise Views

Signal Hill faces east over the Atlantic, making it a spectacular sunrise location. As one of the easternmost points in North America, you'll be among the first to see the sun rise.

Getting There

Signal Hill is located just minutes from downtown St. John's and is easily accessible by car or on foot.

By Car

From downtown St. John's, follow Signal Hill Road uphill to the parking areas near Cabot Tower. The drive takes about 5-10 minutes. Parking is $7.90 per vehicle per day (Parks Canada fees apply mid-May to mid-October).

On Foot

You can walk to Signal Hill from downtown St. John's via Signal Hill Road or through The Battery neighbourhood. The uphill walk takes 20-30 minutes and is moderately strenuous but rewarding.

Tattoo & Signal Hill Military Tattoo

In summer, Parks Canada presents the Signal Hill Tattoo--a military re-enactment featuring soldiers in period costume firing muskets and cannons. Check Parks Canada for the current schedule (typically July-August, Wednesday and weekend afternoons).
