About Jellybean Row

Jellybean Row is the affectionate nickname for the brightly painted row houses that have become the defining image of St. John's, Newfoundland. These vibrant homes--painted in every colour imaginable from candy apple red to electric blue, sunshine yellow to emerald green--create one of the most photogenic and recognizable streetscapes in all of Canada.

The colourful houses are not confined to a single street. You will find them throughout downtown St. John's, but the most stunning and photographed examples are concentrated along Gower Street, Cochrane Street, and the streets surrounding the Basilica of St. John the Baptist. Walking through these neighbourhoods feels like stepping into a living painting.

The architecture itself is a beautiful mix of Victorian and Georgian styles, featuring distinctive elements like bay windows, ornate trim, clapboard siding, and decorative doorways. Each house is a unique expression of its owner's personality, and the tradition of painting homes in bold colours remains a beloved part of St. John's identity.

Why So Colourful?

The tradition dates back centuries when fishermen painted their houses in bright, distinctive colours so they could identify their homes from the sea through Newfoundland's thick fog. Today, residents continue the tradition out of pride and community spirit. There are no bylaws dictating colours--people simply choose what makes them happy.

Best Streets to See Jellybean Row

Gower Street

The most iconic stretch of colourful houses in St. John's. Long rows of vibrant Victorian homes line both sides, creating the classic Jellybean Row postcard view. Start near the Basilica and walk east.

Cochrane Street

Steep and photogenic, Cochrane Street offers colourful houses climbing the hillside with glimpses of the harbour below. The elevation change adds dramatic perspective to photos.

Prescott Street

Another excellent street for row house photography, with well-maintained Victorian homes in a rainbow of colours. Less crowded than Gower Street for a more peaceful stroll.

The Battery

For the most dramatic setting, visit The Battery neighbourhood where colourful houses cling to the steep cliffs at the harbour entrance beneath Signal Hill. Utterly unique and unforgettable.

Photography Tips

Jellybean Row is the most photographed spot in St. John's and one of the most photographed locations in Canada. Here are tips for capturing the best shots.

  • Best Light: Morning light (before 10 AM) is ideal for Gower Street, which faces south. Overcast days actually make the colours pop more vibrantly than harsh sunlight
  • Composition: Include the entire row of houses in your frame for the classic shot. Step across the street for the best angle showing the full colour palette
  • Details: Don't miss the decorative details--ornate doorways, window boxes with flowers, colourful mailboxes, and unique trim work
  • The Battery: For dramatic shots, photograph the colourful Battery houses from the harbourfront or from Signal Hill looking down
  • Respect Residents: These are private homes. Photograph from the sidewalk and don't enter private property or peer into windows

Instagram Tip

The most popular Instagram angle is looking down Gower Street with the row of colourful houses receding into the distance. For a unique shot, try capturing the houses from above on Signal Hill or from the harbour looking up at The Battery.

History & Architecture

St. John's has been settled since the early 1500s, making it one of the oldest European settlements in North America. The city has been devastated by fire multiple times, most notably the Great Fire of 1892 which destroyed much of downtown. The rebuilding effort created the Victorian and Georgian row house streetscape we see today.

The architectural style reflects the practical needs of a harbour city--row houses conserve heat in Newfoundland's harsh winters, and the narrow lots maximized the limited buildable land on St. John's steep hillsides. The distinctive bay windows were added to capture maximum light during the long, dark winters and to allow residents to see up and down the street.

Architectural Features to Look For

  • Bay Windows: Projecting windows that capture light and provide harbour views
  • Clapboard Siding: Horizontal wooden boards, the traditional exterior finish
  • Ornate Trim: Decorative woodwork around windows, doors, and rooflines
  • Mansard Roofs: French-inspired rooflines with dormer windows
  • Transom Windows: Decorative glass panels above doorways

Walking Tour Route

The best way to experience Jellybean Row is on foot. Here is a suggested walking route that covers the most colourful streets in about 45-60 minutes.

  • Start: Basilica of St. John the Baptist on Military Road
  • Walk east on Gower Street for the classic Jellybean Row views
  • Turn down Cochrane Street toward the harbour for hillside colour
  • Continue to Water Street for shops, cafes, and more colourful buildings
  • Walk along the harbourfront to see The Battery houses from below
  • Optional: Continue to The Battery neighbourhood for the most dramatic setting

Combine with Other Attractions

Jellybean Row is in the heart of downtown, so easily combine it with a visit to George Street (nightlife), Water Street (shopping), or walk uphill to Signal Hill for aerial views of the colourful city.
