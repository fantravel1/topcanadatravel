About Takhini Hot Springs

Takhini Hot Springs is a natural mineral hot springs facility located 28 kilometres north of downtown Whitehorse along the Takhini Hot Springs Road. The geothermally heated water rises from deep underground, rich in minerals including calcium, magnesium, and iron, and fills outdoor pools maintained at temperatures between 36°C and 42°C year-round.

The hot springs have been used for centuries by the indigenous peoples of the region, and the modern facility has been welcoming visitors since the 1900s. Today, it is one of the most popular attractions in the Whitehorse area, drawing visitors who come to soak in the warm mineral waters surrounded by boreal forest and, in winter, under the dancing Northern Lights.

The outdoor pools provide a uniquely Canadian experience: sitting in steaming hot water while surrounded by snow-covered spruce trees and sub-arctic air. In summer, the midnight sun allows late-night soaks under bright skies, while winter visits offer the possibility of watching the aurora borealis from the warm comfort of the pool.

International Hair Freezing Contest

Takhini Hot Springs hosts the internationally famous Hair Freezing Contest each winter. When air temperatures drop below -20°C, bathers wet their hair and let it freeze into wild, creative shapes while their bodies stay warm in the pool. The contest has attracted worldwide media attention and has become one of Yukon's most iconic winter experiences. Prizes are awarded for the most creative frozen hairstyles.

The Soaking Experience

The facility features outdoor pools of varying temperatures, allowing you to find your perfect comfort level. The mineral-rich water is odourless and clean, continuously flowing from the natural spring source. Many visitors report that the minerals leave their skin feeling soft and rejuvenated.

Facilities & Amenities

  • Outdoor Pools: Multiple pools at different temperatures ranging from 36°C to 42°C
  • Change Rooms: Heated changing facilities with lockers and showers
  • Rentals: Towels and swimsuits available for rent on-site
  • Campground: On-site camping with access to the hot springs
  • Cafe: Light snacks and beverages available
  • Parking: Free parking lot

Winter Soaking Tips

In winter, bring a robe or towel you can quickly wrap up in between the change room and pool. Keep your hair dry unless you are participating in the Hair Freezing Contest. The walk from the change room to the pool is short but can be bracing at -30°C. Once in the water, you will be perfectly comfortable.

Best Times to Visit

Winter Evenings

Soak under the Northern Lights with steam rising around you. The combination of hot water and freezing air creates a magical atmosphere. Hair Freezing Contest runs Dec-Feb.

Summer Midnight Sun

Soak under the midnight sun with daylight until nearly midnight. Pleasant temperatures outside and a completely different experience from winter visits.

Weekday Mornings

Fewest crowds and a peaceful, meditative experience. The pools are quieter and you may have sections to yourself, especially in shoulder season.

After Aurora Hunting

Many visitors combine a Northern Lights tour with a late-night hot springs soak. Some aurora tour operators include a hot springs stop in their packages.

Getting There

Takhini Hot Springs is located 28 km north of downtown Whitehorse. From the city centre, take the Alaska Highway north and turn left onto Takhini Hot Springs Road. Follow the road for approximately 10 km to the springs. The drive takes about 25-30 minutes.

The road is maintained year-round but winter driving conditions can be icy. Drive carefully and ensure your vehicle has winter tires if visiting between October and April. There is no public transit to the hot springs, so a car or tour shuttle is necessary.
