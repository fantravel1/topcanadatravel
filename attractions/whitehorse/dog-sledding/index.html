About Dog Sledding in Whitehorse

Dog sledding is one of the most iconic Yukon experiences, and Whitehorse is the perfect place to try it. The region has a deep mushing heritage, as home to the legendary Yukon Quest International Sled Dog Race, a gruelling 1,600-kilometre race between Whitehorse and Fairbanks, Alaska, considered one of the toughest sled dog races in the world.

Several experienced operators near Whitehorse offer dog sledding tours ranging from half-day introductory trips to multi-day wilderness expeditions. On a typical tour, you will meet the sled dogs, learn how to harness and drive a team, and then mush through the Yukon wilderness on snowy trails through boreal forest, across frozen lakes, and along river valleys.

The dogs are the stars of the show, and they are athletes that love to run. Visitors are often struck by the excitement and joy the dogs display when they know a run is about to begin. The experience of standing on the runners of a sled, the only sounds being the dogs' breathing and the swoosh of the runners on snow, is one that stays with people for a lifetime.

The Yukon Quest

Whitehorse is one of the two endpoints of the Yukon Quest International Sled Dog Race, held each February. The 1,600 km race between Whitehorse and Fairbanks, Alaska is considered one of the most challenging sled dog races in the world, crossing four mountain summits and following historic Gold Rush trails. Several Whitehorse-based tour operators are run by former Yukon Quest mushers.

Tour Operators

Muktuk Adventures

Run by legendary musher Frank Turner, Muktuk Adventures offers half-day, full-day, and multi-day dog sledding experiences. Located 30 minutes from Whitehorse with over 120 sled dogs. Also offers cabin accommodation and summer kennel tours.

Sky High Wilderness Ranch

Family-operated ranch offering intimate dog sledding experiences. Half-day and full-day tours through stunning wilderness terrain. Known for personalized attention and smaller group sizes. Summer cart rides and kennel tours also available.

What Tours Typically Include

  • Introduction & Safety Briefing: Learn about the dogs, equipment, and how to drive the sled
  • Meet the Dogs: Time to interact with the sled dogs before the run
  • Harnessing: Help harness your dog team and prepare for departure
  • The Run: Drive your own sled through wilderness trails (or ride as a passenger)
  • Warm-Up Break: Hot drinks and snacks at a trail shelter or campfire
  • Winter Gear: Most operators provide warm parkas, boots, and mitts if needed
  • Transportation: Hotel pickup and return from Whitehorse

Types of Experiences

Half-Day Tour (3-4 hours)

The most popular option. Includes meeting the dogs, instruction, and a 1-2 hour sled run through the wilderness. Perfect for first-timers and families. From $180+ per person.

Full-Day Adventure (6-8 hours)

Extended wilderness run with lunch on the trail. Cover more distance and experience deeper wilderness. Includes campfire lunch and more one-on-one time with the dogs. $300-500 per person.

Multi-Day Expedition (2-5 days)

The ultimate mushing experience. Camp in wilderness cabins, care for your own dog team, and travel deep into the Yukon backcountry. A true adventure for those wanting a deeper connection. $1,000+ per person.

Summer Kennel Tour

Visit year-round to meet the sled dogs, learn about mushing culture, and see puppies. Some operators offer wheeled cart rides in summer. A great option when there is no snow. $60-100 per person.

What to Wear & Bring

  • Warm Layers: Dress in multiple layers; temperatures can drop to -30°C or below during winter runs
  • Winter Boots: Insulated, waterproof boots rated for extreme cold (most operators provide these)
  • Warm Gloves/Mitts: Thick mitts preferred over gloves; bring hand warmers as backup
  • Face Protection: Balaclava, neck gaiter, or scarf to protect against windchill on the sled
  • Camera: Action cameras work well; protect batteries from the cold in an inside pocket
  • Sunglasses: Essential for bright winter days with snow glare

Booking Tips

Book well in advance, especially for December-February visits. Tours are weather-dependent and may be adjusted for extreme conditions. Most operators accommodate children aged 5+ on family tours. If you are visiting in summer, ask about kennel tours and wheeled cart rides as an alternative to sledding.
