About Dog Sledding in Yellowknife

Dog sledding is one of Yellowknife's most iconic winter experiences, connecting visitors with a transportation tradition that has sustained northern peoples for thousands of years. In the vast frozen landscape of the Northwest Territories, dog teams were once the primary means of travel between communities, delivering mail, supplies, and people across hundreds of kilometres of snow and ice.

Today, several experienced operators in and around Yellowknife offer dog sledding tours ranging from short introductory rides to multi-day wilderness expeditions. The dogs used are typically Canadian Inuit dogs, Alaskan huskies, or Siberian huskies -- breeds perfectly adapted to Arctic conditions with thick double coats, incredible endurance, and an unmistakable enthusiasm for running.

The experience goes beyond just riding a sled. You'll meet the dogs, learn their names and personalities, help harness the team, and often get the chance to mush (drive) the sled yourself. The dogs' excitement as they're harnessed is contagious -- the kennel erupts with barking and howling, and the moment the brake releases, the team surges forward with astonishing power.

Beck's Kennels -- Yellowknife's Premier Operator

Beck's Kennels is the most established dog sledding operation in Yellowknife, with decades of experience and a team of well-cared-for Canadian Inuit dogs. Their tours range from 2-hour introductions to multi-day backcountry adventures, and they're known for their genuine passion for mushing and dog care.

Tour Options

Introductory Ride (2 Hours)

Perfect for first-timers. Meet the dogs, learn basic mushing, and enjoy a ride through the boreal forest. Hot drinks included. From $150 per person.

Half-Day Adventure (4 Hours)

Extended mushing across frozen lakes and through forest trails. Learn to steer, brake, and command the team. Includes lunch by a campfire. From $200 per person.

Full-Day Expedition (6-8 Hours)

A full day on the trail covering significant distance. Deep wilderness immersion with trail lunch and multiple rest stops. From $300 per person.

Multi-Day Wilderness Trip

The ultimate Arctic adventure. Camp in heated tents, cook over open fires, and travel deep into the NWT backcountry with your dog team. 2-5 days available.

The Dogs

The stars of every dog sledding tour are, of course, the dogs themselves. Yellowknife operators primarily use Canadian Inuit dogs (also called Canadian Eskimo dogs), one of the oldest and rarest purebred indigenous breeds in the world. These powerful, thick-coated dogs were bred by the Inuit people over thousands of years for hauling heavy loads across Arctic terrain.

Other teams may include Alaskan huskies, bred for speed and endurance in competitive mushing, and Siberian huskies, known for their striking blue eyes and friendly temperament. Regardless of breed, all sled dogs share an incredible work ethic and genuine love of running.

Dog Interaction Tips

Ask your guide before approaching any dog. Most sled dogs love attention, but each has its own personality. Always approach from the front so the dog can see you, and let it sniff your hand first. The dogs are working animals and athletes -- treat them with the respect they deserve.

What to Expect

  • Arrival & Introduction: Meet your guide and the dog team. Learn about dog sledding history, commands, and safety procedures
  • Harnessing: Help harness the excited dogs to the sled -- their enthusiasm is infectious as they bark and howl in anticipation
  • On the Trail: Experience the thrill of gliding across frozen landscapes. You may ride as a passenger first, then try mushing yourself
  • Commands: Learn traditional mushing commands: "Hike!" (go), "Gee!" (right), "Haw!" (left), and "Whoa!" (stop)
  • Breaks: Stop for hot chocolate, bannock, and photos along the trail
  • Return: Help unhitch the dogs and reward them with well-deserved affection

  • Insulating mid-layers (fleece jacket, warm pants)

  • Neck gaiter or balaclava
  • Sunglasses or goggles (snow glare is intense)
  • Camera with secure strap (hands get busy on the sled)

Yes, dog sledding with reputable operators is very safe. Guides are experienced mushers who know the trails and weather conditions. You'll receive a full safety briefing before departure. The main risks are cold exposure (addressed by proper clothing) and the occasional spill if the sled tips -- a minor and common occurrence that's part of the adventure.

Reputable Yellowknife operators take excellent care of their dogs. Sled dogs are working athletes who genuinely love to run -- their excitement at harness-up time is unmistakable. Dogs receive regular veterinary care, proper nutrition, comfortable shelters, and daily exercise and socialization. Look for operators certified by Mush with PRIDE or similar organizations.

Most operators welcome children aged 4+ as passengers on the sled. Children under 12 typically ride with an adult or guide. The experience is family-friendly, and kids especially love meeting and petting the dogs. However, the extreme cold means very young children may not enjoy extended time outdoors.
