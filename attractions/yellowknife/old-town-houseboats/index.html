About Yellowknife's Old Town

Yellowknife's Old Town is where the city began and where its frontier spirit lives on. Perched on the rocky, windswept shore of Great Slave Lake, this compact neighbourhood is the historic heart of the Northwest Territories capital. Founded during the gold rush of the 1930s, Old Town retains a rugged, artistic character that sets it apart from the more modern "New Town" up the hill.

The most iconic feature of Old Town is its collection of colourful houseboats bobbing in Yellowknife Bay. These floating homes -- painted in vivid blues, reds, yellows, and greens -- are private residences that have become one of the most photographed scenes in northern Canada. In winter, the houseboats sit frozen in the ice, creating an equally striking scene against the white landscape.

Old Town is also home to some of Yellowknife's most beloved landmarks and restaurants. The Bush Pilot's Monument offers the best panoramic viewpoint in the city, while restaurants like Bullock's Bistro and the historic Wildcat Cafe serve up legendary northern cuisine. Art galleries, studios, and a thriving creative community add cultural depth to this small but vibrant neighbourhood.

Bush Pilot's Monument

Climb the rocky outcrop to the Bush Pilot's Monument for the best view in Yellowknife. This stone cairn honours the bush pilots who opened up Canada's North, and from its summit you get 360-degree views over Great Slave Lake, the houseboats, Old Town, and the surrounding boreal landscape. It's a short but steep climb -- well worth the effort.

Must-See Highlights

Colourful Houseboats

The floating homes of Yellowknife Bay are an iconic sight. View them from the shoreline path -- each one is unique, hand-built, and painted in bold colours. Best photographed in summer with reflections on the water or winter frozen in ice.

Bush Pilot's Monument

A short, steep climb to the best viewpoint in Yellowknife. The monument honours pioneer bush pilots, and the summit offers panoramic views over the lake, houseboats, and city. Free to visit anytime.

Bullock's Bistro

A Yellowknife institution famous for fresh lake fish. The rustic interior is covered in signed business cards. Cash only, generous portions, and quintessentially northern. Try the Great Slave Lake whitefish.

Wildcat Cafe

One of the oldest buildings in Yellowknife, this log cabin restaurant has been serving northern cuisine since 1937. Open seasonally in summer, it's a must-visit for its history, atmosphere, and fresh fish dishes.

Walking Tour Route

Old Town is best explored on foot. Here's a suggested walking route covering all the highlights in about 2 hours:

  • Start at Pilot's Monument: Climb the rock for panoramic views and orient yourself to the neighbourhood
  • Descend to the Waterfront: Walk down to the shore of Yellowknife Bay and follow the path past the houseboats
  • Houseboat Alley: Stroll along the waterfront for the best views of the colourful floating homes (remember, these are private residences)
  • Wildcat Cafe: Stop at this historic log cabin for coffee or a meal (summer only)
  • Weaver & Devore: Browse this legendary northern trading post and general store, stocked with everything from mukluks to mining equipment
  • Art Galleries: Visit local galleries and studios showcasing Indigenous and northern art
  • Bullock's Bistro: End your walk with a legendary fish meal at this iconic restaurant

Summer vs Winter Visits

Summer (June-August) is the best time for the full Old Town experience: houseboats bob on open water, the Wildcat Cafe is open, and 20+ hours of daylight make for leisurely exploring. Winter offers a completely different beauty -- houseboats frozen in ice, snow-covered rocks, and the chance to walk on the frozen bay. Both seasons are rewarding.
