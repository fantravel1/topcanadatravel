About the Snowking Winter Festival

The Snowking Winter Festival is one of Canada's most unique cultural celebrations -- a month-long festival centred around a massive snow castle built entirely by hand on the frozen surface of Great Slave Lake in Yellowknife Bay. Every year since 1994, the self-proclaimed "Snowking" (Anthony Foleyet) and a dedicated team of volunteers have constructed an elaborate snow and ice structure that serves as the festival's venue.

The castle is built using snow harvested from the frozen lake, packed and shaped into walls, rooms, hallways, and performance spaces. The result is a stunning architectural feat that houses live music concerts, art exhibitions, film screenings, comedy shows, and community events -- all within walls of snow and ice. Coloured lights illuminate the interior, creating an otherworldly atmosphere.

What makes the Snowking Festival truly special is its grassroots, community-driven nature. In a city where winter lasts seven months, this festival transforms the coldest season into a celebration. Locals and visitors alike gather inside the snow castle to enjoy world-class entertainment in the most unusual venue imaginable.

A True Northern Original

The Snowking Festival has been running since 1994, making it one of the longest-running winter festivals in northern Canada. The snow castle is rebuilt from scratch each year with a new design, ensuring every visit is a unique experience. It's entirely volunteer-built and community-funded.

What to Experience

The Snow Castle

Explore multiple rooms, hallways, and chambers made entirely of snow and ice. Each year's design is different, featuring intricate carvings, ice furniture, and coloured lighting throughout.

Live Music & Performances

The castle's main hall hosts concerts featuring local and touring musicians. The acoustics inside snow walls create a unique sonic experience. Genres range from folk and rock to Indigenous music.

Art Installations

Northern artists create ice and snow sculptures, murals, and installations throughout the castle. The blend of natural materials and creative vision produces stunning frozen art.

Community Events

Film screenings, comedy nights, children's programs, and special cultural events fill the festival calendar. The atmosphere is warm and welcoming despite the frozen surroundings.

Planning Your Visit

Best Time to Go

The festival runs throughout March, with opening and closing weekends featuring the biggest events. Weekends generally have more programming than weekdays. Check the festival website for the current year's schedule and special event nights.

What to Wear

The castle is outdoors on a frozen lake, so dress for extreme cold even though you're "indoors." The snow walls provide wind shelter but temperatures inside remain well below freezing. Layer up with warm winter gear, insulated boots, and a good toque. The castle warms up slightly during busy concerts from body heat.

Getting There

The snow castle is located on Yellowknife Bay, accessible by walking from Old Town across the frozen lake. It's approximately a 10-15 minute walk from downtown. Follow the marked path on the ice -- it's well-lit and maintained during the festival.

Combine with Aurora Viewing

March is one of the best months for Northern Lights in Yellowknife, making it ideal to combine a Snowking Festival visit with aurora viewing tours. Enjoy the festival in the evening and catch the aurora later that same night -- a perfect northern double feature.
