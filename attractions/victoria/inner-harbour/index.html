About Victoria's Inner Harbour

Victoria's Inner Harbour is the vibrant, picturesque heart of British Columbia's capital city. This historic waterfront serves as the main focal point for visitors and locals alike, surrounded by some of the city's most iconic landmarks including the stately Parliament Buildings and the ivy-covered Fairmont Empress Hotel.

Unlike many urban waterfronts, Victoria's Inner Harbour remains an active working harbour. Float planes take off and land throughout the day, connecting Victoria to Vancouver and other coastal communities. Whale watching boats depart for the open waters of the Salish Sea, ferries connect to destinations across the region, and kayakers paddle alongside all of it.

The harbour promenade stretches along the entire waterfront, offering stunning views at every turn. Street performers, buskers, and artists line the causeway in summer, while flower baskets overflow with colour from the famous hanging baskets the city is known for. It's a place where you can spend an entire day simply soaking in the atmosphere, or use it as a launching point for countless adventures.

Parliament Illumination

Every evening at dusk, over 3,300 lights outline the Parliament Buildings, creating a magical reflection in the harbour waters. This illumination has been a Victoria tradition since the buildings opened in 1898.

Iconic Landmarks

Parliament Buildings

The British Columbia Parliament Buildings dominate the south side of the Inner Harbour. Designed by architect Francis Rattenbury and completed in 1898, these stunning neo-Baroque buildings house the provincial legislature. Free guided tours are available when the legislature is not in session, taking you through the ornate rotunda, legislative chambers, and historic halls. The buildings are beautifully lit at night with thousands of lights outlining the architecture.

Fairmont Empress Hotel

The iconic Fairmont Empress Hotel has been the grande dame of Victoria since 1908. Its ivy-covered walls and chateau-style architecture are instantly recognizable and have become synonymous with the city itself. Even if you're not a guest, you can enjoy afternoon tea in the lobby lounge, dine at Q at the Empress restaurant, or simply admire the building from the harbour promenade.

Royal BC Museum

Adjacent to the Parliament Buildings, the Royal BC Museum is one of Canada's great museums. Explore galleries dedicated to British Columbia's natural history, First Nations culture, and modern history. The museum also features an IMAX theatre and regularly hosts world-class traveling exhibitions.

Photo Tip

For the classic postcard shot of the Parliament Buildings and Empress Hotel, position yourself on the north side of the harbour near the visitor centre. Golden hour (the hour before sunset) provides the best light, and you can stay for the building illumination at dusk.

Things to Do

Watch Float Planes

Harbour Air operates regular float plane service directly from the Inner Harbour. Watching these small aircraft take off and land on the water is endlessly entertaining and unique to Victoria.

Whale Watching Tours

Multiple operators depart from the harbour for 3-5 hour whale watching excursions to see orcas, humpbacks, and other marine life in the Salish Sea.

Street Performers

In summer months, the causeway comes alive with musicians, magicians, artists, and performers. Many are professional entertainers who return year after year.

Water Taxi Adventures

Cute water taxis zip around the harbour offering transportation to Fisherman's Wharf, West Bay Marina, and other destinations. An affordable way to see the harbour from the water.

Kayak the Harbour

Rent kayaks near the harbour and paddle alongside float planes and marine traffic. Guided tours are available for those new to kayaking.

Explore Fisherman's Wharf

A short walk or water taxi ride away, this quirky floating community features colourful houseboats, fresh seafood, and resident harbour seals.

Dining and Entertainment

The Inner Harbour area offers dining options for every taste and budget, from quick waterfront fish and chips to elegant fine dining.

  • Afternoon Tea at the Empress: The quintessential Victoria experience. Reservations essential.
  • Red Fish Blue Fish: Famous waterfront takeout serving sustainable seafood from a pier-side shipping container.
  • Steamship Grill: Upscale waterfront dining with harbour views and West Coast cuisine.
  • Flying Otter Grill: Casual dining next to the float plane terminal - watch planes while you eat.
  • Barb's Fish & Chips: At Fisherman's Wharf, serving Victoria's favorite fish and chips since 1984.

Summer Night Markets

During summer evenings, the Ship Point pier hosts food trucks, live music, and artisan markets. Check local listings for specific dates and events.

Getting Around

The Inner Harbour is the perfect base for exploring Victoria on foot, but there are many transportation options available.

Walking

The harbour promenade connects all major attractions and continues along the waterfront in both directions. Downtown shopping on Government Street is steps away. The entire downtown core is walkable from the harbour.

Victoria Harbour Ferries

These adorable small ferries provide transportation around the harbour and along the Gorge Waterway. They're part transit, part sightseeing tour, and an affordable way to see the water.

Double-Decker Bus Tours

Hop-on, hop-off bus tours depart from the harbour area, providing transportation to attractions throughout Victoria including Craigdarroch Castle and other neighborhoods.

Cycling

Victoria is very bike-friendly. Rental bikes, including electric bikes, are available near the harbour. The Galloping Goose Trail and other paths are accessible from downtown.

Insider Tips

  • Morning Calm: The harbour is quietest before 10 AM - perfect for photography and peaceful walks
  • Parliament Tours: Free guided tours run every 20-30 minutes in summer. No reservation needed, just arrive at the tour entrance
  • Float Plane Viewing: The best vantage point is near the visitor centre, where you're close to takeoffs and landings
  • Sunset Location: For the best sunset views, head to the north end of the harbour near Ship Point
  • Free Entertainment: Summer evenings often feature free concerts at Ship Point or Cameron Bandshell
  • Hidden Garden: Behind the Empress Hotel, the rose garden is a quiet escape most tourists miss
  • Early Whale Watching: Morning tours (7-8 AM departures) often have calmer seas and fewer boats on the water
  • Night Photography: The Parliament Buildings illumination starts at dusk and stays lit until midnight
