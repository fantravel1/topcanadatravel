About Butchart Gardens

Butchart Gardens is one of the world's most celebrated floral show gardens, attracting over a million visitors annually to its 55 acres of meticulously maintained grounds. Located just 23 kilometres north of downtown Victoria in Brentwood Bay, this botanical paradise has been delighting visitors since 1904.

The gardens began as a reclamation project by Jennie Butchart, whose husband Robert operated a cement plant on the property. When the limestone quarry was exhausted, Jennie transformed the barren pit into the spectacular Sunken Garden that remains the centrepiece today. What started as one woman's vision has grown into five distinct themed gardens, each offering unique beauty throughout the seasons.

Designated a National Historic Site of Canada, Butchart Gardens showcases over 900 bedding plant varieties with more than one million bulbs planted annually. The gardens have been family-owned and operated for over 100 years, now in their fifth generation, maintaining the tradition of horticultural excellence that Jennie Butchart established.

Evening Illuminations

During summer months (mid-June to mid-September), the gardens stay open until 10 PM with thousands of coloured lights illuminating the pathways and gardens. Saturday nights feature spectacular fireworks displays set to music over the water.

The Five Gardens

Sunken Garden

The crown jewel of Butchart Gardens, the Sunken Garden was created in the abandoned limestone quarry. Descending into this former industrial pit, visitors are surrounded by sweeping flower beds, a central pond with fountain, and towering walls covered in greenery. The transformation from barren quarry to this lush paradise took Jennie Butchart years of dedicated work, importing soil by horse and cart.

Japanese Garden

Created in 1906, the Japanese Garden is the second-oldest themed garden on the property. It features traditional elements including a tea house, bamboo groves, maples, ornamental bridges, and carefully placed rocks. The garden was designed with input from landscape designer Isaburo Kishida and offers peaceful contemplation year-round, with spectacular fall colours from the Japanese maples.

Rose Garden

Established in 1929, the Rose Garden blooms magnificently from June through September with hundreds of rose varieties. The formal layout features winding paths through beds of hybrid teas, floribundas, and climbing roses on elegant arbours. At peak bloom in July, the fragrance is intoxicating.

Italian Garden

The former tennis court of the Butchart residence was transformed into the Italian Garden in 1926. This intimate space features a star-shaped Florentine fountain as its centrepiece, surrounded by geometric beds and a reflecting pool. Flowering plants change with the seasons, while Italian cypress and shaped evergreens provide year-round structure.

Mediterranean Garden

The newest addition showcases plants that thrive in Victoria's mild climate, which is similar to the Mediterranean region. Olive trees, lavender, drought-tolerant succulents, and ornamental grasses create a distinctly different atmosphere from the other gardens.

Photography Tips

The best light for photography is early morning (9-10 AM) when the gardens are quieter and light is soft, or during the golden hour before sunset. For the Sunken Garden, the overlook platform offers the most iconic viewpoint.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March-May)

Over 300,000 tulips, daffodils, hyacinths, and flowering trees. Cherry blossoms frame the Japanese Garden. Easter activities for families.

Summer (June-August)

Peak bloom with roses, begonias, and hundreds of annuals. Evening illuminations, Saturday fireworks, outdoor concerts, and extended hours until 10 PM.

Fall (September-October)

Japanese maples display brilliant reds and oranges. Dahlias bloom spectacularly. Fewer crowds and comfortable temperatures for walking.

Winter (November-January)

The Magic of Christmas features thousands of lights, ice skating, live entertainment, and festive displays. A completely different but magical experience.

Dining Options

Butchart Gardens offers several dining venues, from casual cafes to fine dining, all using locally sourced ingredients and produce from the gardens when available.

  • The Dining Room: Elegant fine dining with garden views. Reservations recommended. Features afternoon tea service.
  • Blue Poppy Restaurant: Casual dining with international cuisine. Perfect for lunch between garden explorations.
  • Coffee Shop: Quick bites, coffee, and pastries near the entrance.
  • Benvenuto Gelato: Italian gelato and refreshments in the Italian Garden area.

Afternoon Tea

Butchart Gardens offers traditional afternoon tea service in The Dining Room. Reservations are essential, especially during summer months. The experience includes finger sandwiches, scones with Devonshire cream, and an assortment of pastries.

Getting There

Butchart Gardens is located 23 km north of downtown Victoria in Brentwood Bay on the Saanich Peninsula.

By Car

From downtown Victoria, take Highway 17 (Patricia Bay Highway) north toward the BC Ferries terminal. Exit at Keating Cross Road or Benvenuto Avenue and follow the signs. Free parking is available on-site for cars, RVs, and tour buses.

By Bus

BC Transit Route 75 runs from downtown Victoria to Butchart Gardens seasonally (approximately 75 minutes). During peak season, additional service is available. Check BC Transit schedules for current times.

By Tour

Many tour operators offer transportation packages from downtown Victoria and cruise ship terminals. Some include combination tickets with whale watching or other attractions.

From Vancouver

Take the BC Ferry from Tsawwassen to Swartz Bay (1.5 hours). Butchart Gardens is just 15 minutes from the ferry terminal, making it an ideal first or last stop on your Vancouver Island trip.

Insider Tips

  • Book Online: Pre-purchase tickets online to guarantee entry during peak season and avoid ticket line waits
  • Arrive Early: The gardens open at 9 AM. Early visitors enjoy peaceful gardens and the best photography light
  • Stay for Illuminations: If visiting in summer, plan to stay past sunset for the magical evening light display
  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: You'll walk 2-4 km exploring all the gardens. Paths are paved but hilly in places
  • Bring a Jacket: Even in summer, evenings can be cool. The coastal climate can change quickly
  • Visit Midweek: Weekends and cruise ship days (typically Monday-Wednesday in summer) are busiest
  • Check Bloom Reports: The website posts weekly bloom reports so you know exactly what's flowering
  • Carousel Rides: The antique carousel near the Rose Garden is popular with children (and adults!)
