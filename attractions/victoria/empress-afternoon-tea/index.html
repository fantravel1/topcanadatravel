Empress Afternoon Tea

Elegant Tea Service at the Fairmont Empress Since 1908

Premium Options $100-150 CAD

A Victoria Institution

Afternoon tea at the Fairmont Empress is more than a meal - it's a living piece of Victoria's history. Since the hotel opened in 1908, taking tea in its elegant surroundings has been the quintessential Victoria experience, enjoyed by royalty, celebrities, and generations of visitors who've made it a cherished tradition.

The ritual takes place in the Lobby Lounge, a grand space featuring soaring ceilings, ornate woodwork, and views of the Inner Harbour through tall windows. Soft piano music fills the air as white-gloved servers present tiered stands laden with delicate finger sandwiches, freshly baked scones, and exquisite pastries. It's a step back in time to an era of refined elegance.

While many hotels offer afternoon tea, the Empress experience is in a class of its own. The hotel's pastry team creates new seasonal menus throughout the year, sourcing local ingredients and incorporating BC flavors. The tea selection features 21 premium loose-leaf teas, including the signature Empress Blend created exclusively for the hotel.

Royal Approval

The Empress has hosted numerous royal visitors, including King Charles III and Queen Elizabeth II. The tradition of afternoon tea here carries the same elegance and attention to detail that has impressed royalty for over a century.

The Tea Experience

The Tea Selection

Choose from 21 premium loose-leaf teas, each selected to complement the food offerings. The Empress Blend, a house-exclusive black tea, is the most popular choice. Options range from classic English Breakfast and Earl Grey to specialty white teas, green teas, and herbal infusions. Your server can recommend the perfect pairing based on your preferences.

Savory Course

The experience begins with an assortment of delicate finger sandwiches. Classic offerings include cucumber with cream cheese, smoked salmon, and egg salad on crustless bread. The menu changes seasonally, incorporating local BC ingredients and the chef's creative interpretations of traditional recipes.

Scone Course

Freshly baked scones arrive warm from the oven, served with Devonshire clotted cream, house-made preserves, and lemon curd. The traditional plain scone is always available, alongside a seasonal variety. This is often guests' favorite part of the experience.

Sweet Course

The top tier features an array of miniature pastries, tarts, and sweets crafted by the hotel's pastry team. Each piece is a work of art, beautifully presented and perfectly portioned. Seasonal themes influence the offerings, with special creations for holidays and festivals.

Pacing Your Tea

Don't rush! Afternoon tea is meant to be savoured over 90 minutes to 2 hours. Start with the sandwiches (bottom tier), move to scones (middle), and finish with sweets (top). Feel free to ask for seconds - they're included in the price.

Tea Options

Classic Afternoon Tea

The signature experience featuring finger sandwiches, scones with preserves, pastries, and your choice of premium tea. $85+ per person.

Royal Tea

The classic service enhanced with a glass of champagne or sparkling wine. Perfect for celebrations. $100-120 per person.

Prince & Princess Tea

A specially designed experience for children with kid-friendly sandwiches, treats, and hot chocolate or juice. Available for children under 12.

Seasonal Specialty Teas

Special themed teas during holidays including Christmas, Easter, and Mother's Day feature unique menus and decor. Book well in advance.

Dress Code & Etiquette

The Empress maintains a smart casual dress code to preserve the refined atmosphere of the tea experience.

What to Wear

  • For Women: Dresses, skirts, dress pants, blouses, cardigans. Many guests enjoy wearing hats or fascinators, though they're not required
  • For Men: Dress pants or chinos, collared shirts, blazers welcome. No jeans in casual cuts
  • For Children: Smart casual appropriate for their age. Think Sunday best

What Not to Wear

  • Beachwear, flip-flops, or sandals
  • Athletic wear, yoga pants, or shorts
  • Casual jeans or t-shirts
  • Baseball caps or casual hats (formal hats are welcome)

Afternoon Tea Etiquette

When stirring tea, move the spoon back and forth, not in circles. Place the spoon on the saucer when done. When eating scones, break off bite-sized pieces and add cream and jam to each piece individually rather than spreading the whole scone at once.

Making Reservations

Reservations are essential for afternoon tea at the Empress, especially during peak seasons.

How to Book

  • Online: Through the Fairmont Empress website or OpenTable
  • Phone: Call the hotel directly at +1-250-389-2727
  • Concierge: Hotel guests can book through the front desk

When to Book

  • Summer (June-September): Book 2-3 weeks in advance, earlier for weekends
  • Holidays: Mother's Day, Easter, and Christmas teas should be booked 4-6 weeks ahead
  • Off-Season (November-March): 1 week advance is usually sufficient
  • Last Minute: Call the hotel - cancellations do occur and weekday availability is often better

Special Occasions

Celebrating a birthday, anniversary, or special event? Let the hotel know when booking. They can arrange special touches like custom cakes, champagne, and personalized menus.

Insider Tips

  • Arrive Hungry: The portions are generous. Come with an appetite and skip a heavy breakfast or lunch
  • Request Window Seating: When booking, ask for a table with harbour views. Not guaranteed but they'll try to accommodate
  • Come at Off-Peak Times: The first seating (11 AM) and mid-afternoon (3 PM) are often less crowded than noon sittings
  • Explore the Hotel: Arrive early to wander the historic hotel. The lobby, corridors, and Bengal Lounge are beautiful
  • Ask About Dietary Needs: Gluten-free, vegetarian, and allergy-friendly options are available with advance notice
  • Take Photos: The presentation is stunning. Your server is happy to take group photos
  • Purchase Tea to Take Home: The Empress Blend and other teas are available in the gift shop
  • Consider the Timing: Afternoon tea is substantial - plan it as your main meal for that time of day

Perfect Pairings

Combine afternoon tea with a morning whale watching tour or an afternoon visit to Butchart Gardens. The tea makes an excellent midday break between activities and you'll already be dressed nicely!
