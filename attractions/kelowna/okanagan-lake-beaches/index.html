About Okanagan Lake Beaches

Okanagan Lake stretches 135 kilometres through the heart of the valley, and Kelowna sits right at its midpoint with some of the best beach access in the region. With over 20 public beaches, warm summer water temperatures that reach 22-24¬∞C by late July, and more than 2,000 hours of sunshine annually, Kelowna is genuinely one of Canada's finest beach destinations.

From the buzzing downtown beaches with volleyball courts and food vendors to quiet coves accessible only by trail or boat, there is a beach for every mood. Water sports are hugely popular -- paddleboarding, kayaking, wakeboarding, and jet skiing rentals are available at multiple locations along the waterfront.

The beaches are also home to one of Canada's most enduring legends: the Ogopogo, a mysterious lake creature first described by the Indigenous Syilx people as N'ha-a-itk. Keep your eyes peeled on the water -- you might just spot something unusual.

The Ogopogo Legend

Okanagan Lake's legendary creature, Ogopogo, has been reported for over a century. The Syilx people spoke of N'ha-a-itk long before European settlement. A bronze statue on the downtown waterfront celebrates the legend, and there is even a reward offered for definitive proof of its existence. Canada's answer to the Loch Ness Monster!

Top Beaches

Hot Sands Beach

Located right downtown in City Park, Hot Sands Beach is Kelowna's most popular beach. The fine sandy shoreline stretches along the waterfront with stunning views across the lake. It draws a younger crowd in summer with beach volleyball courts, nearby restaurants and bars, and a lively atmosphere. The Kelowna waterfront boardwalk runs right past it, making it easy to combine beach time with downtown exploring.

Gyro Beach

A family favourite with a large sandy beach, playground equipment, and shallow wading areas perfect for young children. Lifeguards are on duty during peak summer months. The beach has washrooms, picnic tables, and a grassy park area with shade trees. Paddleboard and kayak rentals are available right on the beach.

Rotary Beach

Slightly south of downtown, Rotary Beach offers a quieter atmosphere with excellent swimming. The beach has a gradual sandy entry, making it great for families. There is a playground, washrooms, and ample free parking. The adjacent Rotary Marsh is a bird-watching hotspot where you can spot herons, ospreys, and other wildlife.

Boyce-Gyro Beach Park

One of Kelowna's widest sandy beaches with plenty of space even on busy summer weekends. Excellent for picnics with barbecue facilities, covered shelters, and a large grassy area. The swimming area is well-maintained with a roped-off section for safety.

Okanagan Mountain Provincial Park

For those willing to hike, this park offers remote, uncrowded beaches accessible only by trail or boat. Crystal-clear water and stunning natural surroundings make the effort worthwhile. Pack everything you need as there are no services.

Beach Tips

Arrive before 11 AM on summer weekends to secure parking at popular beaches. The lake is warmest from late July through August. Bring reef-safe sunscreen -- Kelowna averages 300+ sunny days. Water shoes are handy at rockier beach areas south of downtown.

Water Sports & Activities

Stand-Up Paddleboarding

The calm morning waters of Okanagan Lake are perfect for SUP. Rentals available at Gyro Beach and downtown waterfront ($25-40/hour). Guided SUP yoga classes also available.

Kayaking & Canoeing

Explore the shoreline by kayak with rentals at multiple locations. Guided sunset kayak tours are a popular evening activity ($60-80 per person).

Wakeboarding & Tubing

Boat rentals with wakeboard and tube packages available from several marinas. Half-day boat rentals start around $300-500.

Lake Cruises

Scenic lake cruises and dinner cruises depart from the downtown marina. Wine-tasting cruises combine two of Kelowna's best experiences ($80-150).
