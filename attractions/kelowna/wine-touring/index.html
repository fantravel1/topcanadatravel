About Kelowna Wine Touring

The Kelowna area is the beating heart of British Columbia's wine country, home to over 40 wineries that produce some of Canada's finest wines. The Okanagan Valley's unique microclimate -- warm days, cool nights, and low rainfall -- creates ideal conditions for growing premium grapes, and the region now produces 84% of all BC wine.

From boutique family operations to grand estate wineries with panoramic lake views, Kelowna's wine scene offers something for every palate. The West Kelowna wine trail alone features a dozen award-winning producers, while the Lakeshore and downtown corridors add dozens more. Whether you prefer a leisurely self-drive tour or a fully guided experience by bus, bike, or even e-bike, wine touring in Kelowna is an unforgettable experience.

The signature wines of the region include Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Riesling, Merlot, Pinot Gris, and the world-famous Okanagan icewine. Many wineries also feature excellent on-site restaurants, making a day of wine touring a complete culinary adventure.

Harvest Season Magic

Visit during September and October to experience grape harvest (crush) season. Many wineries host special harvest events, grape-stomping activities, and limited-release wines available only at the cellar door. The valley's fall colours add stunning scenery to your wine trail experience.

Top Wineries to Visit

Mission Hill Family Estate

Perched high above Okanagan Lake in West Kelowna, Mission Hill is the Okanagan's most iconic winery. The Tuscan-inspired architecture, 12-storey bell tower, and sweeping vineyard views make it a must-visit. Their Oculus Bordeaux-style blend is legendary, and the Terrace Restaurant offers one of the finest dining experiences in the valley. Tours range from casual tastings to premium vineyard experiences.

Quails' Gate Winery

A family-owned winery on the slopes of Boucherie Mountain with stunning lake views. Known for their exceptional Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, Quails' Gate also features Old Vines Restaurant -- one of Canada's top winery dining experiences. The heritage log cabin tasting room adds rustic charm to every visit.

Summerhill Pyramid Winery

Canada's largest certified organic winery is famous for its on-site pyramid, modeled after the Great Pyramid of Cheops, where sparkling wines are aged. The Sunset Organic Bistro serves outstanding farm-to-table cuisine. Summerhill's commitment to organic and biodynamic farming makes it a leader in sustainable winemaking.

CedarCreek Estate Winery

One of the Okanagan's pioneer wineries, CedarCreek underwent a stunning renovation and now features a modern tasting room and the acclaimed Home Block Restaurant. Their Platinum series wines consistently win national awards.

50th Parallel Estate

Named for its latitude -- the same as Champagne, France -- this winery produces outstanding Pinot Noir. The Block One Restaurant and beautiful modern architecture make it a standout stop on any wine trail.

Wine Touring Tips

Plan to visit 4-6 wineries per day maximum. Many tasting fees are waived with a bottle purchase. Always use a designated driver, hire a tour company, or take a bike tour -- BC has strict impaired driving laws with roadside penalties starting at 0.05% BAC.

Wine Trails & Routes

West Kelowna Wine Trail

12+ wineries along a scenic route including Mission Hill, Quails' Gate, and Volcanic Hills. Stunning lake views and some of the region's most photographed wineries.

Lakeshore Wine Route

Follow the eastern shore of Okanagan Lake through Summerhill, CedarCreek, and St. Hubertus. Flat terrain makes this popular for cycling wine tours.

Downtown Kelowna Wineries

Urban wineries and tasting rooms within walking distance of downtown hotels. Perfect for an evening stroll between tastings without needing a car.

Naramata Bench (Day Trip)

30 minutes south of Kelowna, the Naramata Bench packs 20+ wineries into a single stunning stretch. Often called Canada's finest wine road.

Tour Options

  • Self-Drive: Rent a car and create your own route. Designate a driver or use a sober driving service ($50-80 for the day)
  • Guided Bus Tours: Full-day tours visiting 4-5 wineries with lunch included ($150-200 per person)
  • Bike Tours: Pedal between wineries on guided cycling tours -- e-bikes available for hills ($120-180 per person)
  • Private Tours: Customized experiences in luxury vehicles with a personal wine guide ($300+ per group)
  • Helicopter Tours: Arrive at select wineries by helicopter for the ultimate VIP experience ($500+)

Seasonal Wine Experiences

Spring (May-June)

New vintage releases, spring wine festivals, and patio season begins. Fewer crowds and lower prices make this an excellent time for wine touring.

Summer (July-August)

Peak season with outdoor concerts, al fresco dining, and long sunny days. Book tastings and restaurants in advance. Many wineries host summer events.

Fall Harvest (September-October)

The most magical time! Watch grapes being harvested and crushed. Special harvest dinners, grape-stomping events, and limited releases abound.

Winter (November-April)

Cozy tasting room experiences, icewine season, and excellent value. Pair with skiing at Big White for the ultimate winter wine weekend.
