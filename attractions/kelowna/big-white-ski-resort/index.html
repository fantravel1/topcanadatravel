About Big White Ski Resort

Big White Ski Resort is British Columbia's largest ski-in/ski-out village and one of Canada's top family ski destinations. Located just 56 kilometres east of Kelowna in the Monashee Mountains, Big White receives an average of 750 centimetres of dry, light "champagne powder" snow each season -- the kind of snow that skiers and snowboarders dream about.

With 118 marked runs spread across 2,765 acres and a vertical drop of 777 metres, Big White offers terrain for every ability level. The resort is particularly renowned for its extensive beginner and intermediate terrain, making it ideal for families and those learning to ski. Advanced skiers will find plenty of challenge in the steeps, glades, and alpine bowls.

The ski-in/ski-out village is the largest in Canada, meaning you can walk out your door and onto the slopes. The village features hotels, condos, restaurants, shops, and an ice-skating rink -- everything you need without ever getting in a car. Big White also boasts Western Canada's largest night skiing operation, with 26 illuminated runs and terrain park features.

Snow Ghosts

Big White is famous for its "snow ghosts" -- subalpine fir trees completely encased in rime ice and snow, creating otherworldly frozen sculptures across the upper mountain. These ghostly figures are unique to Big White's climate and are one of the resort's most photographed features.

Terrain Breakdown

  • Beginner (18%): Wide, gentle groomers perfect for first-timers and children. The Happy Valley beginner area is separate from main traffic
  • Intermediate (54%): The majority of terrain with well-groomed cruisers, rolling terrain, and tree-lined runs
  • Advanced (18%): Steep groomers, mogul runs, and challenging tree skiing through the powder-filled glades
  • Expert (10%): Alpine bowls, cliff bands, and extreme terrain for those seeking the ultimate challenge

Night Skiing

Big White operates the largest night skiing program in Western Canada. Over 26 runs and terrain park features are illuminated from mid-December through mid-March, typically from 5 PM to 8 PM. Night skiing passes are available separately and are significantly cheaper than day passes -- a great option for those arriving in the afternoon.

Ski Tips

Buy lift tickets online in advance for the best prices -- walk-up rates are significantly higher. The resort operates a free shuttle from several Kelowna hotels during ski season. Winter tires or chains are legally required on Highway 33 from October 1 to March 31.

Beyond Skiing

Snowshoeing

Explore 25 km of marked snowshoe trails through snow ghost forests. Guided twilight snowshoe tours with fondue are a memorable evening activity.

Tubing & Skating

The Happy Valley tubing park is a hit with families. The village ice-skating rink offers free skating under the stars (bring your own or rent skates).

Dog Sledding

Guided dog sled tours through the backcountry offer a uniquely Canadian winter experience. Available for all ages and no experience needed.

Village Dining

Over 15 restaurants and bars in the village ranging from casual pub fare to upscale dining. Apres-ski at Snowshoe Sam's is a Big White tradition.

Getting There

From Kelowna, take Highway 33 East for approximately 56 km (about 1 hour). The road is well-maintained in winter with regular plowing, but winter tires or chains are legally required. The resort operates shuttle buses from downtown Kelowna and the airport during ski season.

From Kelowna Airport

Kelowna International Airport (YLW) receives direct flights from Vancouver, Calgary, Toronto, and seasonal service from other cities. The airport is about 50 minutes from the resort. Shuttle services and car rentals are available at the terminal.

Champagne powder is ultra-dry, light, fluffy snow with very low moisture content. Big White's high elevation and cold interior climate produce snow with just 4-6% water content (compared to 12%+ at coastal resorts). This creates a skiing sensation of floating on clouds -- it's the most coveted snow type in the ski world.
