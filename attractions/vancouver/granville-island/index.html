About Granville Island

Granville Island is Vancouver's cultural heart - a vibrant peninsula transformed from industrial wasteland into one of the world's most successful urban redevelopment projects. Located beneath the Granville Street Bridge on the shores of False Creek, this 35-acre enclave attracts over 10 million visitors annually.

Originally a sandbar, the island was created in 1915 by dredging False Creek and became an industrial hub of machine shops, sawmills, and factories. By the 1970s, the industries had declined and the area was derelict. The federal government purchased the land and began the transformation that would make Granville Island a model for urban renewal worldwide.

Today, the island buzzes with activity: the famous Public Market, artisan studios, theatres, galleries, craft breweries, and waterfront restaurants. Despite the name, Granville Island isn't actually an island - it's a peninsula connected by road to the city, though arriving by ferry across False Creek is the quintessential Vancouver experience.

Industrial Heritage

Look up and you'll see the rustic character of Granville Island's industrial past. The original corrugated metal buildings and rail tracks have been preserved, creating a unique atmosphere where working studios and food vendors occupy former machine shops.

Granville Island Public Market

The Public Market is the crown jewel of Granville Island - a 50,000-square-foot marketplace housing over 50 permanent vendors selling everything from fresh produce to artisan crafts. Open since 1979, it's one of North America's premier public markets.

Market Must-Visits

  • Lee's Donuts: Legendary fresh donuts made throughout the day - arrive early for warm honey-dip
  • Longliner Seafoods: BC salmon, halibut, and Dungeness crab - they'll pack seafood for travel
  • Benton Brothers Fine Cheese: Over 300 artisan cheeses with knowledgeable staff
  • Granville Island Tea Company: Hundreds of loose-leaf teas with free samples
  • Oyama Sausage Company: European-style charcuterie and deli meats
  • Stuart's Bakery: Fresh bread, pastries, and the famous raisin bread
  • Siegel's Bagels: Montreal-style bagels baked in a wood-fired oven

The market also features prepared food stalls offering cuisines from around the world - perfect for grabbing lunch and eating at the waterfront seating area outside.

Market Tips

The market is busiest Saturday and Sunday 11 AM - 3 PM. For the best experience, visit on a weekday morning when vendors have time to chat and you can move freely. Bring cash for smaller vendors, though most accept cards.

Artisan Studios

Granville Island is home to dozens of working artisan studios where you can watch craftspeople at work and purchase directly from the artists. The island has more than 280 resident artists, making it one of North America's densest concentrations of creative talent.

Net Loft

A collection of unique shops in a converted net storage building. Find handmade jewelry, paper arts, First Nations crafts, and one-of-a-kind gifts. The eclectic mix of vendors makes it perfect for browsing.

Railspur Alley

A hidden laneway of artist studios including glass blowing, pottery, leather goods, and artisan distilleries. Watch artists at work and commission custom pieces. Less crowded than the main areas.

Craft Council Gallery

Showcases the finest work from BC artisans - ceramics, textiles, jewelry, and woodwork. Rotating exhibitions highlight different craft traditions. Everything is handmade in British Columbia.

Circle Craft

A cooperative gallery featuring works from over 200 BC artisans. High-quality handmade goods including pottery, glass art, wood items, and textiles. Great for unique gifts.

Kids Market

The Kids Market is a two-storey wonderland dedicated entirely to children. Located in a colorful building near the water park, it houses over 25 shops and activities designed for kids of all ages.

Kids Market Highlights

  • Toy Stores: Science toys, puzzles, art supplies, and classic wooden toys
  • Adventure Zone: Indoor play area with ball pit, slides, and climbing structures
  • Kids Only Market: Mini marketplace where children can buy affordable items themselves
  • Face Painting: Available most weekends and holidays
  • Costume Corner: Dress-up costumes for imaginative play

Outside the Kids Market, the free water park operates during summer months (late May to September) with spray features perfect for cooling off on hot days. Adjacent is an adventure playground with structures for all ages.

Granville Island Brewing

Canada's first modern microbrewery, Granville Island Brewing has been crafting beer on the island since 1984 - before the craft beer revolution swept North America. Take a brewery tour, sample their flagship beers, and enjoy the taproom with views of False Creek.

Brewery Tours

Tours run daily and last about 45 minutes, including the history of the brewery, the brewing process, and samples of 4-5 beers. Cost is approximately $15 per person. Reservations recommended on weekends. Must be 19+ (BC legal drinking age).

The taproom offers the full range of Granville Island beers including seasonal and limited releases you won't find elsewhere. Pair your flight with snacks and enjoy the industrial-chic atmosphere.

Waterfront Dining

Granville Island offers some of Vancouver's best waterfront dining experiences. From casual fish and chips to upscale Pacific Northwest cuisine, there's something for every taste and budget.

Restaurant Highlights

  • Sandbar: Upscale seafood with stunning water views and rooftop patio
  • The Keg: Classic steakhouse with waterfront terrace seating
  • Dockside Restaurant: Contemporary West Coast cuisine at the Granville Island Hotel
  • Go Fish: Famous fish and chips in a casual waterfront shack - cash only, worth the line
  • Bridges: Three dining venues in one - bistro, pub, and fine dining with patios
  • Tony's Fish & Oyster Cafe: Casual market-style seafood in the Public Market

Don't Miss: Go Fish

Go Fish serves what many consider Vancouver's best fish and chips. Located in a small shack at Fisherman's Wharf, expect a line - but it moves quickly. Cash only. Try the halibut and chips or the legendary fish tacos.

Emily Carr University of Art + Design

While the main campus moved to a new location in 2017, Emily Carr University maintains a presence on Granville Island with continuing education programs and public galleries. The university community contributes to the island's creative energy.

Public galleries on the island display student and faculty work, often pushing boundaries in contemporary art and design. Check their calendar for opening receptions and special events.

Getting There

Granville Island is located on the south shore of False Creek, directly beneath the Granville Street Bridge. While you can drive, the most enjoyable ways to arrive are by water or on foot.

By Ferry (Recommended)

Two ferry services connect Granville Island to downtown Vancouver:

  • Aquabus: Departs from the foot of Hornby Street (downtown) and from the north end of Davie Street. About $4-6 one-way.
  • False Creek Ferries: Departs from the Aquatic Centre near Sunset Beach. About $4-6 one-way.

Both services run every 5-10 minutes and the crossing takes about 5 minutes. It's a delightful mini-cruise with city skyline views.

By Bus

Take bus #50 False Creek South from downtown Vancouver. The bus stops at the entrance to Granville Island. Trip takes about 15-20 minutes from Waterfront Station.

By Car

Access is via Anderson Street from the south end of the Granville Street Bridge. Follow signs for Granville Island. Parking is available but limited - lots fill quickly on weekends. First 3 hours free with merchant validation.

Walking or Cycling

The False Creek seawall connects Granville Island to Science World, Olympic Village, and Yaletown. Walking from Yaletown takes about 15 minutes along the scenic waterfront path.

Insider Tips

  • Visit Early: Arrive when the market opens at 9 AM to avoid crowds and get the freshest products
  • Take the Ferry: The Aquabus ride is half the experience - don't miss it
  • Explore Beyond the Market: Many visitors miss Railspur Alley and the quieter eastern side of the island
  • Summer Evenings: The island is magical at dusk when crowds thin and patio lights glow
  • Buskers Welcome: Street performers play near the market entrance - bring small bills to tip
  • Rainy Days: Most attractions are covered or indoors, making it perfect for Vancouver's rainy days
  • Combine with Seawall: Walk or bike the False Creek seawall to Olympic Village or Yaletown
