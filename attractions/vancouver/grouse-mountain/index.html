About Grouse Mountain

Grouse Mountain is Vancouver's year-round mountain playground, offering spectacular views and adventures just 15 minutes from downtown. Rising 1,200 metres above sea level, "The Peak of Vancouver" provides everything from skiing and snowshoeing in winter to hiking, wildlife viewing, and ziplines in summer.

The mountain got its name in 1894 when hikers shot a blue grouse near the summit. Today, it's one of North America's most accessible alpine experiences - take the Skyride gondola and you're transported from sea level to mountaintop in just 8 minutes, with stunning views of Vancouver, the ocean, and surrounding peaks.

Grouse Mountain operates year-round with different seasonal attractions. Summer brings wildlife encounters, lumberjack shows, and hiking, while winter transforms the mountain into a ski resort with western Canada's longest night skiing hours. The views of Vancouver's city lights from the summit at night are unforgettable.

Record-Breaking Views

On a clear day, you can see Vancouver Island 100 km to the west, Mount Baker in Washington 120 km to the south, and the peaks of Garibaldi Provincial Park to the north. The Eye of the Wind turbine offers 360-degree views from its viewing pod 65 metres above the summit.

The Skyride Gondola

The Super Skyride is one of the largest aerial tramways in North America, carrying 100 passengers in each of its two cars. The 8-minute journey climbs 1,100 vertical metres, offering increasingly spectacular views of Vancouver, English Bay, and the surrounding mountains.

On the ride up, watch for the famous Grouse Grind trail snaking through the forest below - you might spot determined hikers making their way up "Mother Nature's Stairmaster." At the top, step out onto the deck for one of the most photographed viewpoints in British Columbia.

The Skyride operates year-round, with extended hours during ski season and special events. Your ticket includes unlimited same-day rides, so you can go up and down as many times as you like.

Summer Activities

Grizzly Bear Refuge

Meet Grinder and Coola, two orphaned grizzly bears rescued as cubs in 2001. Watch them play, swim, and forage in their 5-acre alpine habitat. Educational presentations explain their story and grizzly conservation efforts.

Lumberjack Show

World-champion lumberjacks demonstrate log rolling, axe throwing, springboard chopping, and chainsaw skills in this family-friendly show. Audience participation and humor make it a highlight for all ages.

Birds in Motion

Spectacular free-flying demonstrations featuring eagles, owls, hawks, and falcons. Learn about BC's raptors as they soar above the crowd and return to their handlers. Truly magical in the alpine setting.

Zipline Adventure

Soar across the mountaintop on parallel ziplines reaching speeds up to 80 km/h. The course includes multiple lines totaling over 1.5 km with stunning views of Vancouver below. Additional cost: $49.

More Summer Activities

  • Peak Chairlift: Ride higher for even more expansive views (included with Skyride)
  • Eye of the Wind: Climb inside the wind turbine to a viewing pod 65m above the summit
  • Mountain Ropes Adventure: Aerial obstacle course through the treetops ($39)
  • Hiking Trails: Multiple trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes
  • Disc Golf: 18-hole course across the alpine terrain

The Grouse Grind

The Grouse Grind is Vancouver's legendary hiking trail - 2.9 kilometres of steep climbing through coastal rainforest, ascending 853 metres with approximately 2,830 stairs. Known as "Mother Nature's Stairmaster," it attracts over 100,000 hikers each season.

Grouse Grind Facts

  • Distance: 2.9 km (1.8 miles)
  • Elevation Gain: 853 m (2,800 ft)
  • Average Time: 1.5 - 2 hours
  • Record Time: 23 minutes 48 seconds
  • Season: May - October (weather permitting)
  • Download Required: Skyride ticket ($20) - trail is one-way up only

Grouse Grind Tips

Start early to avoid afternoon heat and crowds. Bring water (1-2 litres minimum), wear sturdy footwear, and go at your own pace. The trail is extremely steep - if you're not used to hiking, consider taking the Skyride up and exploring the easier summit trails instead.

Winter Activities

When snow blankets the mountain, Grouse transforms into a ski resort with 33 runs, terrain parks, and the longest night skiing hours in western Canada. Just 15 minutes from downtown, it's where Vancouver learns to ski.

Skiing and Snowboarding

  • Terrain: 33 runs - 20% beginner, 50% intermediate, 30% advanced
  • Lifts: 5 lifts including the Skyride
  • Night Skiing: Until 10 PM, the longest hours in western Canada
  • Terrain Parks: Multiple parks for all skill levels
  • Ski School: Lessons for all ages and abilities
  • Rentals: Full equipment rental available at the base

Other Winter Activities

  • Mountaintop Skating: Ice skating pond with mountain views
  • Snowshoeing: Guided and self-guided snowshoe trails
  • Sleigh Rides: Horse-drawn sleigh rides through the winter wonderland
  • Light Walk: Thousands of lights illuminate trails during the holiday season

Dining on the Mountain

Multiple dining options cater to every taste and budget, from quick cafeteria meals to fine dining with spectacular views.

Restaurant Options

  • The Observatory: Fine dining with panoramic windows overlooking Vancouver. Reservations recommended for dinner.
  • Altitudes Bistro: Casual West Coast cuisine with outdoor patio seating in summer
  • Lupins Cafe: Quick service cafe with sandwiches, soups, and coffee
  • Rusty Rail BBQ: Summer outdoor BBQ near the lumberjack show

Dinner and Views

The Observatory restaurant offers a special dinner package that includes Skyride tickets. Time your reservation for sunset to watch Vancouver's lights twinkle on while enjoying Pacific Northwest cuisine.

Getting There

Grouse Mountain is located in North Vancouver, just 15 minutes from downtown Vancouver. Multiple transportation options make it easy to visit without a car.

Free Shuttle Bus

A free shuttle bus operates from Canada Place in downtown Vancouver, with additional stops at select hotels. The shuttle runs year-round with increased frequency during ski season. Check the Grouse Mountain website for current schedules and pickup locations.

By Transit

Take the SeaBus from Waterfront Station to Lonsdale Quay in North Vancouver. Transfer to bus #236 (Grouse Mountain) which goes directly to the base of the Skyride. Total journey time is approximately 45-60 minutes from downtown.

By Car

From downtown Vancouver, cross the Lions Gate Bridge and follow Highway 1 (Trans-Canada) east to exit 14 (Capilano Road). Turn left and follow signs to Grouse Mountain. Free parking is available at the base, though it fills quickly on busy days - arrive early or take transit.

By Rideshare

Uber and Lyft operate throughout Vancouver. The ride from downtown takes 15-25 minutes and typically costs $25-40 one way. Drop-off is at the base of the Skyride.

Insider Tips

  • Beat the Crowds: Arrive when the Skyride opens at 9 AM for the smallest crowds and best wildlife viewing
  • Sunset Magic: Go up in late afternoon and stay for sunset - watching the city lights come on is magical
  • Layer Up: The summit is 10-15 degrees cooler than sea level; bring layers even in summer
  • Annual Pass Value: If you plan to ski 3+ days or visit twice in summer, the annual pass pays for itself
  • Combine Attractions: Capilano Suspension Bridge is just 5 minutes away - combine for a full day
  • Check the Webcam: The mountain website has live webcams - check conditions before you go
  • Night Skiing Deal: Night skiing tickets after 4 PM are significantly cheaper than full-day passes
  • Grind Training: If attempting the Grouse Grind, train beforehand - it's extremely challenging
