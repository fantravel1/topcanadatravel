About the CN Tower

Soaring 553 metres into the Toronto skyline, the CN Tower is Canada's most recognizable landmark and one of the most iconic structures in the world. Completed in 1976, it held the record as the world's tallest free-standing structure for an incredible 32 years until 2007, and it remains the tallest free-standing structure in the Western Hemisphere.

The tower was originally built by the Canadian National Railway (hence "CN") as a communications and observation tower to demonstrate the strength of Canadian industry. Today, it's a defining feature of Toronto's skyline and welcomes over 1.5 million visitors annually who come to experience its multiple observation levels, world-famous Glass Floor, thrilling EdgeWalk, and award-winning 360 Restaurant.

From the observation deck, visitors enjoy unobstructed 360-degree views spanning up to 160 kilometres on a clear day. You can see across Lake Ontario to Niagara Falls, spot the mist rising from the falls, and on exceptionally clear days, glimpse the faint outline of Rochester, New York across the lake.

Engineering Marvel

The CN Tower contains 40,524 cubic metres of concrete and weighs 130,000 tonnes. It sways only 1.07 metres at the SkyPod level in 190 km/h winds. The tower took 40 months to build, with crews working 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

The Glass Floor Experience

The CN Tower's Glass Floor, located at 342 metres, was the world's first when it opened in 1994. The five panels of glass, each 2.5 inches thick, offer a stomach-dropping view straight down to the ground below. Walking on air 342 metres above the streets of Toronto is an unforgettable experience.

The glass panels can withstand the weight of 35 moose‚Äîthat's over 14,000 kilograms. Despite knowing the statistics, most visitors feel a thrill of adrenaline when stepping onto the transparent surface for the first time. Some visitors sit, lie down, or even do yoga poses on the glass for memorable photos.

Glass Floor Photo Tips

For the best photos, visit on a clear day and wear colourful clothing that contrasts with the cityscape below. Lying on the glass and having someone photograph you from above creates a dramatic perspective. Early morning means fewer crowds and better lighting for photography.

EdgeWalk - Walk on the Edge

For the ultimate adrenaline rush, EdgeWalk takes brave visitors outside the tower to walk hands-free on a 1.5-metre wide ledge encircling the main pod at 356 metres‚Äîthe world's highest external walk on a building.

Secured by a harness attached to an overhead rail system, participants lean back over Toronto with nothing but air beneath them, walk around the tower's circumference, and experience views that no regular observation deck can match. The 30-minute outdoor experience includes photos and videos of your walk.

EdgeWalk Requirements

  • Age: Must be 13 years or older
  • Weight: Between 75-300 lbs (34-136 kg)
  • Weather: Operates in most conditions; cancelled during lightning, high winds, or freezing rain
  • Season: April through October (weather permitting)
  • Booking: Advance reservations required; sells out quickly in summer

Not for the Faint of Heart

EdgeWalk participants report it as a life-changing experience. You'll lean back at 356 metres with nothing below you but the Toronto skyline. The experience has a 100% safety record since opening in 2011.

360 Restaurant

Perched at 351 metres, 360 is Canada's highest and most famous revolving restaurant. The dining room completes one full rotation every 72 minutes, offering guests an ever-changing panoramic view of Toronto, Lake Ontario, and beyond.

The restaurant features contemporary Canadian cuisine highlighting regional ingredients and seasonal menus. Award-winning dishes include Ontario lamb, Atlantic lobster, and AAA Canadian beef. The wine cellar houses over 550 labels, with an emphasis on Canadian vintages.

Dining at 360

  • Reservations: Essential; book 2-4 weeks ahead for weekend dinners
  • Free Access: Dinner reservation includes complimentary LookOut admission
  • Sunset Tables: Request 45 minutes before sunset for the best light show
  • Dress Code: Smart casual; no shorts or athletic wear
  • Budget: $80-150 per person for dinner with wine

Tower Observation Levels

LookOut Level (346m)

The main observation deck with floor-to-ceiling windows, interactive displays, and 360-degree views. Included with general admission.

Glass Floor Level (342m)

Features the famous Glass Floor and outdoor SkyTerrace. Look straight down through 2.5 inches of glass to the ground below.

SkyPod (447m)

The highest observation level, offering views up to 160km on clear days. Requires additional ticket upgrade.

EdgeWalk Level (356m)

The outdoor ledge where EdgeWalk participants walk hands-free around the tower's circumference. Separate ticket required.

Best Times to Visit

Sunrise

The tower opens at 9 AM, making early morning ideal for avoiding crowds. Watch the city wake up with soft morning light illuminating the skyline.

Golden Hour (Sunset)

The most popular time for photography. Arrive 1 hour before sunset and stay for twilight as the city lights come alive. Book ahead for this time slot.

Clear Winter Days

Cold, crisp days offer the best visibility‚Äîsometimes over 160km. The shorter days mean earlier sunsets and dramatic evening light.

Night Views

Experience Toronto's glittering skyline after dark. The tower is open until 10:30 PM, perfect for seeing the city illuminated.

Getting There

The CN Tower is located in downtown Toronto's Entertainment District, adjacent to the Rogers Centre (formerly SkyDome) and Ripley's Aquarium of Canada.

By Transit (TTC)

Take the subway to Union Station (all lines connect here). Exit via the SkyWalk and follow signs to the CN Tower‚Äîit's a 10-minute covered walk through the underground PATH system. The 509/510 Harbourfront streetcar stops at Rees Street, a 5-minute walk away.

By Train (GO Transit/VIA Rail)

Both GO Transit and VIA Rail arrive at Union Station. The CN Tower is directly accessible via the SkyWalk from the station.

By Car

Parking is available at the Metro Toronto Convention Centre ($25-35) and various lots along Bremner Boulevard. The tower itself does not have dedicated parking.

By Foot

From the Toronto waterfront, walk north along Rees Street or York Street. From King Street, walk south along John Street past the Rogers Centre.

Insider Tips

Save Money with CityPASS

Toronto CityPASS includes CN Tower admission plus four other attractions at a significant discount. If visiting multiple attractions, this is the best value option.

  • Book Online: Skip the ticket line by purchasing timed-entry tickets online in advance
  • Avoid Weekends: Weekday mornings are the least crowded times to visit
  • Combine Attractions: Ripley's Aquarium is next door‚Äîdo both in one day
  • Weather Check: Check visibility conditions before visiting; foggy days mean limited views
  • Photography: Wear dark clothing to reduce reflections when photographing through glass
  • Glass Floor: If nervous, start at the edge and gradually move to the centre
