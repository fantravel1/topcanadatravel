About the Distillery District

The Distillery District is Toronto's most celebrated pedestrian-only neighbourhood, a stunning transformation of Victorian-era industrial architecture into a vibrant hub of arts, culture, dining, and shopping. The 13-acre site preserves the largest and best-preserved collection of Victorian industrial architecture in North America.

The site was originally home to the Gooderham and Worts Distillery, founded in 1832 and once the largest distillery in the world. At its peak in the 1860s, it produced over two million gallons of whisky annually. The distillery closed in 1990, and after a decade of planning, the district reopened in 2003 as a heritage arts and entertainment destination.

Today, the cobblestone streets wind past beautifully restored red-brick buildings housing over 80 tenants: contemporary art galleries, designer boutiques, craft breweries, artisan cafes, performance venues, and some of Toronto's most acclaimed restaurants. The car-free environment makes it perfect for leisurely exploration.

Film Location Famous

The Distillery District's atmospheric Victorian architecture has made it a favourite film location. It has appeared in dozens of movies and TV shows, standing in for 19th-century London, Boston, and Chicago. Keep an eye out for film crews during your visit!

What to See & Do

Art Galleries

Over a dozen contemporary art galleries showcase Canadian and international artists. Highlights include the Thompson Landry Gallery (Quebec art), Corkin Gallery (photography), and Sandra Fauza Gallery (contemporary painting).

Craft Beverages

Sample craft spirits at Spirit of York Distillery or Mill Street Brewery's original brewpub. The district has come full circle, once again producing award-winning alcoholic beverages.

Artisan Shopping

Discover unique boutiques featuring handcrafted jewelry, designer fashion, home decor, specialty foods, and Canadian artisan products you won't find anywhere else.

Public Art

Outdoor sculptures and art installations dot the district, including the famous "Love" sign (a popular Instagram spot) and various rotating contemporary pieces.

Dining & Drinks

The Distillery District has become one of Toronto's premier dining destinations, with options ranging from casual cafes to destination restaurants.

Restaurant Highlights

  • Cluny Bistro: Elegant French bistro in a stunning restored space with soaring ceilings and a wraparound terrace
  • El Catrin: Upscale Mexican with spectacular Day of the Dead decor and one of the city's best patios
  • Archeo: Italian cuisine in the original boiler house, featuring an open kitchen and wood-burning oven
  • Pure Spirits Oyster Bar: Seafood-focused menu with an extensive oyster selection and craft cocktails
  • Madrina Bar y Tapas: Spanish small plates and an impressive sherry collection

Cafes & Quick Bites

  • Balzac's Coffee: Toronto's beloved local roaster in a dramatic heritage space
  • SOMA Chocolatemaker: Bean-to-bar chocolate, drinking chocolate, and truffles
  • Brick Street Bakery: Fresh-baked breads, pastries, and sandwiches
  • Arvo: Australian-inspired brunch and all-day cafe

Patio Season

Summer is patio season in the Distillery District. Most restaurants open spacious outdoor seating areas amid the cobblestones and heritage buildings. El Catrin's patio is particularly spectacular, seating over 200 guests under string lights.

Toronto Christmas Market

The Distillery District transforms into a magical winter wonderland each year for the Toronto Christmas Market, one of the world's best Christmas markets and the largest in Canada. Running from mid-November through late December, it draws over 600,000 visitors annually.

What to Expect

  • Massive Christmas Tree: A towering tree (often 50+ feet) dominates the central square, surrounded by lights
  • Artisan Vendors: Over 75 vendors selling handcrafted gifts, decorations, and specialty foods
  • Festive Food: European-style treats including raclette, chimney cakes, mulled wine, and hot chocolate
  • Live Entertainment: Carol singers, musicians, and performers create a soundtrack of holiday cheer
  • Holiday Decorations: The entire district is elaborately decorated with millions of lights

Christmas Market Tips

The Christmas Market requires timed-entry tickets (around $10-15). Weekday afternoons are less crowded than weekends. Dress warmly‚Äîyou'll be outdoors most of the time. Book restaurant reservations well in advance if you want to dine during your visit.

Events & Entertainment

Beyond the Christmas Market, the Distillery District hosts events throughout the year that bring the heritage spaces to life.

Annual Events

  • ArtFest Toronto (May): Outdoor art fair featuring 100+ Canadian artists
  • Jazz Festival Events (June): The district hosts stages during the TD Toronto Jazz Festival
  • Pumpkin Path (October): Family-friendly Halloween event with carved pumpkins and activities
  • Light Festival (Winter): Outdoor light art installations from local and international artists

Performance Venues

The Young Centre for the Performing Arts, home to Soulpepper Theatre Company, presents world-class drama, musicals, and concerts year-round. Smaller venues host comedy nights, live music, and special events throughout the district.

Getting There

The Distillery District is located in Toronto's east end, southeast of downtown, bounded by Parliament Street, Mill Street, and the Don River.

By Streetcar (TTC)

The 504 King streetcar runs along King Street‚Äîget off at Parliament Street and walk south for 5 minutes. Alternatively, the 503 Kingston Road streetcar terminates at the Distillery Loop, right at the entrance.

By Subway & Walking

From King Station (Line 1), take the 504 streetcar east. From Union Station, it's a 25-minute walk east along the waterfront, or take the 504 streetcar.

By Car

Enter from Mill Street (off Parliament Street). Underground parking is available beneath the district for approximately $3 per half hour or $15-20 for the day. During the Christmas Market, parking fills up quickly‚Äîarrive early or use transit.

By Bike

Bike Share Toronto stations are located nearby. The Martin Goodman Trail along the waterfront connects to paths leading to the district. Bike parking is available on-site.

Insider Tips

Best Photo Spots

The "Love" sign in Trinity Square, the Pure Spirits building's iron fire escapes, Tank House Lane's string lights, and the cobblestone streets lined with heritage buildings are all Instagram favourites.

  • Weekday Visits: For a quieter experience, visit on a weekday; weekends draw larger crowds
  • Evening Ambiance: The district is particularly atmospheric after dark when the string lights and building lights create a romantic glow
  • Art Walk: Pick up a gallery map at the visitor centre and create your own self-guided art tour
  • Combine with Corktown: The adjacent Corktown neighbourhood has additional restaurants and the charming Corktown Common park
  • Weather Prep: Cobblestones can be slippery when wet; wear appropriate footwear
  • Dog-Friendly: The pedestrian streets are great for dogs, and many patios welcome well-behaved pets
