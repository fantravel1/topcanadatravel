About the Royal Ontario Museum

The Royal Ontario Museum‚Äîuniversally known as the ROM‚Äîis Canada's largest and most comprehensive museum, combining world cultures and natural history under one remarkable roof. Founded in 1912, the museum houses over 13 million artifacts and specimens across more than 40 galleries, making it one of the largest museums in North America.

The ROM's collections span virtually every continent and era‚Äîfrom the age of dinosaurs to contemporary Canadian art. Its encyclopedic approach means you can explore ancient Egypt, Chinese temple art, Canadian Indigenous heritage, African textiles, and Ice Age mammals all in a single visit.

The museum's striking architecture tells two stories: the original 1914 Romanesque building with its iconic totem poles and heritage galleries, and the bold 2007 Michael Lee-Chin Crystal addition that juts dramatically over Bloor Street. Together, they represent over a century of Toronto's commitment to preserving and sharing human knowledge.

The Crystal

The Michael Lee-Chin Crystal, designed by Daniel Libeskind, features five interlocking prismatic structures clad in 25% glass and 75% aluminum. Love it or debate it, the Crystal has become one of Toronto's most photographed architectural landmarks since opening in 2007.

Must-See Collections

Dinosaur Gallery (Level 2)

Home to over 25 complete dinosaur skeletons including a T. rex named Gordo, a massive Futalognkosaurus, and the world's most complete Barosaurus. Kids and adults alike are captivated by these prehistoric giants.

Egyptian Collection (Level 3)

One of the best Egyptian collections outside Europe, featuring mummies, sarcophagi, ancient jewelry, and everyday objects from 5,000 years ago. The gallery includes a reconstructed Middle Kingdom tomb.

Chinese Architecture (Level 1)

The Bishop White Gallery houses spectacular Chinese Buddhist temple art, including massive wall paintings, wooden sculptures, and the impressive Yuan Dynasty mural from the Shanxi Province.

Indigenous Peoples (Level 1)

Daphne Chicken Hall showcases First Nations, Metis, and Inuit art and culture. Highlights include the iconic totem poles, ceremonial masks, and contemporary Indigenous artworks.

Natural History Highlights

The ROM's natural history collections are among the most important in the world for scientific research, with millions of specimens spanning geology, paleontology, and biodiversity.

Bat Cave

A family favourite since 1987, the Bat Cave recreates a Jamaican cave ecosystem complete with 800 freeze-dried bats, acoustic soundscape, and the unique scent of guano. It's educational, immersive, and slightly spooky‚Äîperfect for curious minds of all ages.

Biodiversity Gallery

Explore the incredible diversity of life on Earth through thousands of specimens, from tiny insects to the massive blue whale heart. Interactive displays help visitors understand evolution, ecosystems, and conservation challenges.

Gems & Minerals

The Teck Suite of Galleries houses stunning specimens of minerals, gems, and meteorites. Highlights include the Light of the Desert‚Äîone of the world's largest cerussite crystals‚Äîand a collection of Canadian gold specimens.

For Families

The CIBC Discovery Gallery on Level 2 is designed for children ages 0-7, with hands-on activities, dress-up stations, and touchable specimens. It's free with museum admission and a perfect first museum experience.

World Cultures Collections

The ROM's world cultures collections represent human creativity and heritage from every inhabited continent, spanning thousands of years of history.

Gallery Highlights

  • Ancient Greece & Rome: Classical sculptures, pottery, jewelry, and everyday objects from Mediterranean civilizations
  • Japanese Art: Samurai armour, woodblock prints, ceramics, and a traditional tea room setting
  • Korean Gallery: Celadon ceramics, Buddhist art, and contemporary Korean culture
  • South Asian Art: Hindu and Buddhist sculptures, textiles, and temple architecture
  • African Galleries: Textiles, sculptures, ceremonial objects, and contemporary art
  • European Decorative Arts: Period rooms, armour, and objects from medieval to modern Europe

Special Exhibitions

The ROM hosts world-class travelling exhibitions throughout the year, from ancient treasures to contemporary art and natural wonders. These special exhibitions often require timed-entry tickets and may have additional admission fees.

Past exhibitions have featured Pompeii artifacts, Viking treasures, wildlife photography awards, and immersive experiences exploring biodiversity. Check the ROM website for current and upcoming exhibitions during your visit.

ROM Friday Night Live

On select Friday evenings, the ROM transforms into an adults-only event with DJs, cash bars, special programming, and after-hours gallery access. It's one of Toronto's most unique nightlife experiences‚Äîart, science, and socializing combined.

Getting There

The Royal Ontario Museum is located in the heart of Toronto's museum district, at the intersection of Bloor Street and Avenue Road, adjacent to the University of Toronto campus.

By Subway (TTC)

Museum Station on Line 1 (Yonge-University) exits directly into the ROM's lower level. It's the most convenient option. The station itself features fossil replicas in its design.

By Bus

Multiple bus routes stop at Bloor Street and Avenue Road, including the 5 Avenue Road, 94 Wellesley, and 6 Bay routes. The 300 Bloor-Danforth night bus serves the area after subway hours.

By Car

Paid parking is available at the municipal lot on Bedford Road, as well as various Green P lots in the area. Street parking is limited and expensive. Consider transit as parking can be challenging.

By Foot

The ROM is a pleasant 15-minute walk north from Bloor-Yonge Station through the Yorkville neighbourhood, or 20 minutes from Queen's Park Station through the university campus.

Insider Tips

Third Tuesday Discount

On the third Tuesday of each month from 4:30-8:30 PM, the ROM offers discounted admission ($15 for adults). It's a popular time, so expect crowds but great value.

  • Start at the Top: Begin on Level 3 and work your way down‚Äîyou'll be fresher for the upper galleries and can take your time descending
  • Pick Your Focus: You can't see everything in one visit; choose 10-15 galleries that interest you most
  • Use the App: Download the ROM app for self-guided tours, gallery maps, and artifact information
  • Coat Check: Free coat check is available and highly recommended‚Äîthe galleries require lots of walking
  • Photography: Photos without flash are permitted in most galleries; check signage for restrictions
  • Dining: The C5 Restaurant on Level 5 offers upscale dining with Crystal views; the cafeteria provides quicker, budget-friendly options
