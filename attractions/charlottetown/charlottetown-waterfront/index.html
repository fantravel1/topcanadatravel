About the Waterfront

The Charlottetown Waterfront has been the lifeblood of this city since its founding in 1764. For over 250 years, this harbour has welcomed ships from around the world ‚Äî including the vessels that brought the Fathers of Confederation to Charlottetown in 1864 for the historic conference that would create Canada.

Today, the waterfront has been beautifully revitalized as a vibrant gathering place. Peake's Wharf is the centrepiece, a collection of colourful shops, restaurants, and tour boat docks built on the historic wharf. The boardwalk stretches along the harbour, offering scenic views and connecting to trails that extend beyond the city.

Whether you are looking for a harbour cruise, a fresh lobster dinner with a view, or simply a sunset stroll along the water, the Charlottetown Waterfront delivers one of the most pleasant maritime experiences in Atlantic Canada.

Peake's Wharf

Named after James Peake, a prominent 19th-century merchant, Peake's Wharf is a lively complex of shops, eateries, and boat tour departures right on the harbour. Browse local crafts, sample PEI ice cream, watch the boats come and go, and enjoy the energy of this working waterfront. In 1864, the Fathers of Confederation arrived at this very wharf to attend the conference that would create Canada.

What to Experience

Harbour Boat Tours

Cruise the harbour and surrounding waters. Options include sightseeing cruises, seal watching tours, deep-sea fishing, and lobster boat experiences. Most depart from Peake's Wharf.

Founders' Hall

An interactive museum telling the story of Canadian Confederation through multimedia exhibits. Walk through the history of how Canada was built, province by province.

Seafood Restaurants

Fresh PEI lobster, oysters, mussels, and fish & chips at harbourside restaurants. Dining with a view of the boats and harbour activity.

Boardwalk & Trails

A scenic waterfront boardwalk perfect for walking, jogging, or cycling. Connects to longer trails extending along the coast. Beautiful at sunset.

Visiting Tips

  • Sunset: The waterfront faces west ‚Äî perfect for spectacular sunset views over the harbour
  • Boat Tours: Book in advance during July and August. Morning tours often have calmer waters
  • Cruise Ships: When cruise ships dock (summer), the waterfront gets busy ‚Äî plan accordingly
  • Cow's Ice Cream: PEI's famous ice cream shop has a location on the waterfront ‚Äî the lineups are worth it
  • Farmers' Market: Check for the waterfront farmers' market on Saturday mornings for local produce and crafts

Insider Tip

Walk the boardwalk at sunset, then grab a table at one of the harbour restaurants for dinner. The combination of golden light on the water, boats returning to harbour, and fresh PEI seafood is an unforgettable Maritime experience.
