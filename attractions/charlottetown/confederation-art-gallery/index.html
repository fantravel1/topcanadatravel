Confederation Centre Art Gallery

PEI's Largest Art Gallery with Free Admission

Admission Free

About the Gallery

The Confederation Centre Art Gallery is Prince Edward Island's largest and most important visual arts institution. Located inside the Confederation Centre of the Arts in the heart of downtown Charlottetown, the gallery houses a significant permanent collection of Canadian art and presents rotating exhibitions that change throughout the year.

The gallery's permanent collection spans the breadth of Canadian artistic expression, from historical works to contemporary pieces. With a particular strength in Maritime and Atlantic Canadian art, the collection tells the visual story of this region's landscapes, people, and culture. Works in painting, sculpture, photography, printmaking, and mixed media are all represented.

As part of Canada's national memorial to Confederation, the gallery occupies a special place in the country's cultural landscape. It serves as both a repository of Canadian artistic heritage and a platform for emerging and established artists from across the nation.

Free Admission

The Confederation Centre Art Gallery offers free admission year-round, making it one of the best free cultural experiences in Atlantic Canada. Whether you have 30 minutes between other activities or an entire afternoon to explore, the gallery welcomes all visitors at no cost.

What to See

Permanent Collection

An impressive collection of Canadian art spanning historical to contemporary works. Strong representation of Maritime artists and landscapes that define Atlantic Canada.

Rotating Exhibitions

New exhibitions throughout the year featuring Canadian and international artists. Themes range from traditional to cutting-edge contemporary art. Always something new to see.

Photography & Mixed Media

Beyond traditional painting and sculpture, the gallery showcases photography, printmaking, installation art, and mixed media works reflecting the diversity of contemporary art practice.

Gallery Shop

Browse art books, prints, PEI-made crafts, and unique gifts inspired by the collection. A wonderful place to find meaningful souvenirs with an artistic connection to the island.

Insider Tip

The gallery is busiest when cruise ships are in port. For the most peaceful experience, visit in the morning or late afternoon. Summer weekday mornings tend to be the quietest times, giving you space to appreciate the art at your own pace.
