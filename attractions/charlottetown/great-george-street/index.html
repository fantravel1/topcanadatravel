About Great George Street

Great George Street holds the distinction of being the most historically significant street in Canada. On September 1, 1864, the delegates who would become known as the Fathers of Confederation walked up this very street from the harbour to Province House, where they would begin the discussions that led to the creation of Canada.

The street runs from the waterfront uphill to Province House, and nearly every building along its length has a story to tell. Beautifully preserved Victorian-era homes, churches, and commercial buildings create one of the finest heritage streetscapes in the country. Many of these buildings have been lovingly converted into charming bed and breakfasts, allowing visitors to sleep in the same neighbourhood where Canada was born.

Walking Great George Street today, you follow in the literal footsteps of John A. Macdonald, George-Etienne Cartier, Charles Tupper, Samuel Leonard Tilley, and the other delegates who shaped the destiny of a nation. Interpretive plaques along the route mark key sites and tell the story of those transformative September days.

The Walk That Changed History

On September 1, 1864, the delegates from the Province of Canada arrived by steamship at the Charlottetown harbour. They walked up Great George Street to Province House, carrying with them a bold vision: to unite all of British North America into a single nation. By the end of the conference, the Maritime delegates were convinced. Three years later, on July 1, 1867, the Dominion of Canada was born.

What to See

Heritage Buildings

Victorian-era architecture in excellent condition, with colourful facades, decorative trim, and the charm of 19th-century craftsmanship. Many buildings date from the 1860s Confederation era.

St. Dunstan's Basilica

One of the most impressive churches in Atlantic Canada, with Gothic Revival architecture and stunning stained glass windows. Visible from much of Great George Street.

Confederation Plaques

Interpretive markers along the street tell the story of the 1864 conference and the delegates who walked this route. A self-guided history tour at your own pace.

Heritage B&Bs

Several beautifully restored Victorian homes operate as bed and breakfasts. Stay overnight on the street where Canada was born for a truly immersive heritage experience.

Walking Tour Tips

  • Start at the Waterfront: Begin at the harbour (where the delegates arrived) and walk uphill to Province House for the full historical experience
  • Read the Plaques: Take time to read the interpretive markers ‚Äî they bring the Confederation story to life
  • Photography: The street is most photogenic in late afternoon light when the Victorian buildings glow warmly
  • Combine Attractions: Great George Street connects the waterfront to Province House and Victoria Row ‚Äî walk it as part of a longer downtown tour
  • Stay Overnight: Book a B&B on Great George Street for the most immersive heritage experience in Charlottetown

Insider Tip

The Great George Hotel, a collection of restored heritage buildings on the street, offers boutique accommodation right in the heart of the Confederation story. It is one of the most atmospheric places to stay in all of Atlantic Canada.
