About the Confederation Centre

The Confederation Centre of the Arts is Canada's national memorial to the Fathers of Confederation. Opened on October 6, 1964 ‚Äî exactly 100 years after the Charlottetown Conference ‚Äî the Centre was a gift from every province and the federal government to honour the birth of Canada.

The brutalist-style building occupies an entire city block in the heart of downtown Charlottetown, directly adjacent to Province House where Confederation was born. It houses a 1,100-seat main stage theatre, multiple smaller performance spaces, PEI's largest art gallery, a public library, and a memorial hall.

Today, the Centre is the cultural heart of Prince Edward Island, presenting world-class theatre, visual arts, and musical performances year-round. Its flagship production ‚Äî Anne of Green Gables - The Musical ‚Äî has been enchanting audiences every summer since 1965.

Anne of Green Gables - The Musical

Running every summer since 1965, this is the longest-running annual musical theatre production in Canada. Based on L.M. Montgomery's beloved novel, the show brings Anne Shirley's story to life with memorable songs, vibrant costumes, and the charm of PEI. Over 2 million people have seen it. Book early for July and August ‚Äî performances frequently sell out.

What to Experience

Mainstage Theatre

The 1,100-seat Homburg Theatre hosts the Anne musical and other major productions throughout the season. Excellent sightlines from every seat.

Art Gallery

PEI's largest art gallery features an outstanding permanent collection of Canadian art plus rotating exhibitions. Free admission year-round.

Second Stage Theatre

More intimate productions, new Canadian works, and experimental theatre in the smaller Mack theatre space.

Young Company

Family-friendly performances and programming designed for younger audiences. A wonderful introduction to live theatre for children.

Planning Your Visit

  • Book Early: Anne of Green Gables musical tickets sell out quickly in July and August ‚Äî book weeks or months ahead
  • Evening Shows: Most mainstage performances begin at 7:30 PM. Arrive early to explore the lobby and gallery
  • Gallery Hours: The art gallery is open daily during summer and has reduced hours in the off-season
  • Gift Shop: Excellent selection of Anne of Green Gables memorabilia, PEI art, and Canadian crafts
  • Dining: Enjoy dinner at Victoria Row restaurants before the show ‚Äî they're just steps away
  • Accessibility: The Centre is fully wheelchair accessible with elevator access to all levels

Insider Tip

If you cannot get tickets for the Anne musical, check for the Centre's other productions ‚Äî they often feature outstanding Canadian plays and musicals that are just as entertaining, and tickets are easier to get.
