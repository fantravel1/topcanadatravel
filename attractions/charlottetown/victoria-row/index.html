Charlottetown's Charming Pedestrian Street with Patios & Live Music

About Victoria Row

Victoria Row is Charlottetown's most charming street and the social heart of the city in summer. This short stretch of Richmond Street, near the intersection with Great George Street, is lined with beautifully restored Victorian-era buildings painted in soft pastels and bright colours, creating one of the most picturesque streetscapes in Atlantic Canada.

From June through September, the street closes to vehicle traffic and transforms into a vibrant pedestrian promenade. Restaurants spill onto the sidewalks with outdoor patios, street musicians fill the air with Maritime folk music, and art galleries open their doors to browsers. The atmosphere is reminiscent of a European piazza, with the warmth and friendliness that PEI is famous for.

The Victorian heritage buildings date from the mid-to-late 1800s, when Charlottetown was a prosperous colonial capital. Today they house a delightful mix of restaurants, cafes, boutiques, and galleries that make Victoria Row the perfect place to spend a summer evening.

Summer Evenings on the Row

The best time to experience Victoria Row is on a warm summer evening. Grab a patio seat, order fresh PEI seafood or a craft cocktail, and enjoy live music drifting through the street. As the sun sets and the heritage buildings glow in warm light, you'll understand why this is considered one of the most romantic spots in the Maritimes.

What to Experience

Outdoor Dining

Multiple restaurants offer patio seating right on the pedestrian street. Fresh PEI lobster, oysters, craft cocktails, and international cuisine in an al fresco setting.

Live Music

Street musicians and scheduled performances throughout summer evenings. Maritime folk, Celtic music, and contemporary artists create the soundtrack of Charlottetown.

Art Galleries

Browse galleries featuring PEI and Maritime artists. Paintings, photography, pottery, and crafts inspired by the island's landscapes and culture.

Boutique Shopping

Unique local shops offering PEI-made crafts, jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs. Perfect for finding one-of-a-kind gifts and mementos.

Insider Tip

For the best people-watching and atmosphere, get a patio table at one of the middle restaurants where you can see both ends of the Row. Lunchtime is less crowded if you prefer a quieter experience, but the real magic happens after 7 PM on summer evenings.
