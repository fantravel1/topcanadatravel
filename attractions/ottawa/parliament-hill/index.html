About Parliament Hill

Parliament Hill, known affectionately as "The Hill," is the political and cultural heart of Canada. Perched dramatically above the Ottawa River, this stunning complex of Gothic Revival buildings has been the seat of Canada's federal government since 1866. The iconic Peace Tower, with its 53-bell carillon and observation deck, dominates the Ottawa skyline and has become one of Canada's most recognizable symbols.

The original Centre Block was destroyed by fire in 1916, with only the Library of Parliament surviving. The current Centre Block, completed in 1927, is now undergoing the most extensive renovation in its history, expected to continue into the early 2030s. During this time, Parliament continues to function in the beautifully restored West Block and the former Ottawa Train Station.

Beyond politics, Parliament Hill is a gathering place for Canadians. It hosts Canada Day celebrations, protests, vigils, and cultural events throughout the year. The manicured lawns, the eternal Centennial Flame, and the dramatic views of the Ottawa River and Gatineau Hills make it a must-visit destination for any trip to Ottawa.

Centre Block Renovation

The Centre Block is currently closed for a major renovation project that will restore and modernize this heritage building. The House of Commons now meets in the West Block, and the Senate in the former Ottawa Train Station. Both locations offer free tours.

The Peace Tower

The Peace Tower is the centrepiece of Parliament Hill, rising 92.2 metres above the main entrance to Centre Block. Built as a memorial to Canadians who served in the First World War, it houses the Memorial Chamber, which contains the Books of Remembrance listing the names of all Canadians who died in military service.

The tower features a 53-bell carillon that plays daily concerts, the four-faced clock that has kept time since 1927, and an observation deck offering panoramic views of Ottawa, Gatineau, and the surrounding region. The observation deck is typically open to visitors when Parliament is in session.

Peace Tower Tip

Visit the Memorial Chamber inside the Peace Tower to pay respects to fallen Canadians. The Books of Remembrance are turned daily to a new page, ensuring each name is displayed at least once per year. Admission is free but hours are limited.

Tours and Experiences

Guided Building Tours

Free tours of the Senate (train station) and House of Commons (West Block) run daily. Book online in advance as spots fill quickly, especially in summer.

Changing of the Guard

Daily at 10 AM from late June to late August. Watch the ceremonial guards in red tunics and bearskin hats perform this 30-minute military tradition.

Sound & Light Show

Free nightly shows in summer project stunning visuals and stories onto the Parliament buildings. One of Canada's most spectacular free attractions.

Canada Day Celebrations

July 1st brings the country's largest celebration to Parliament Hill with concerts, ceremonies, and fireworks. Arrive early for the best viewing spots.

What to See

  • Centennial Flame: Lit in 1967 for Canada's 100th birthday, this eternal flame is surrounded by the shields of all provinces and territories. Coins tossed in the fountain are donated to disability research.
  • Library of Parliament: The only part of the original Centre Block to survive the 1916 fire. This stunning circular library with its Gothic architecture is included on some tours.
  • East Block: This beautifully preserved building contains the original offices of Canada's founding Prime Ministers, restored to their 1870s appearance.
  • Queen Victoria Statue: An iconic bronze statue on the front lawn, a popular photo spot with Parliament as the backdrop.
  • Cat Sanctuary: Behind the Library of Parliament, a colony of cats has been cared for by volunteers since the 1970s.

Getting There

Parliament Hill is located in downtown Ottawa, within walking distance of most major hotels, ByWard Market, and the Rideau Canal. It's easily accessible by foot, transit, or car.

By Transit

Take OC Transpo buses to the Wellington/Metcalfe or Wellington/Bank stops. The Confederation Line LRT stops at Parliament Station, just a short walk from the Hill.

By Car

There is no public parking on Parliament Hill. Use nearby parking garages at World Exchange Plaza (111 Albert St) or the Westin Hotel. Street parking is limited and metered.

On Foot

Parliament Hill is a 10-minute walk from ByWard Market, 5 minutes from the Rideau Centre, and connected to the Rideau Canal pathways. The surrounding area is very walkable.

Visitor Tips

  • Book Tours Early: Free tours fill up quickly, especially in summer. Book online at least 2-3 days in advance.
  • Security Screening: All visitors entering buildings must pass through airport-style security. Allow extra time and avoid bringing large bags.
  • Dress Appropriately: Parliament buildings are air-conditioned. Bring a light layer even in summer.
  • Photo ID Required: Bring government-issued ID for building tours.
  • Arrive Early for Ceremonies: For the Changing of the Guard or Sound & Light Show, arrive 30-45 minutes early for the best viewing spots.
  • Combine with Nearby Attractions: The National War Memorial, Rideau Canal locks, and ByWard Market are all within a 10-minute walk.
