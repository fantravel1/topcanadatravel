About ByWard Market

ByWard Market is the pulsing heart of Ottawa - a vibrant neighborhood that has served as the city's primary public market since 1826. Named after Lieutenant Colonel John By, the British engineer who oversaw the construction of the Rideau Canal, this is one of Canada's oldest and largest public markets.

Today, ByWard Market is much more than a farmers' market. It's a complete urban experience encompassing four square blocks of heritage buildings, cobblestone courtyards, and lively streets packed with restaurants, bars, boutiques, galleries, and entertainment venues. From dawn when farmers arrive with fresh produce to late night when the clubs are in full swing, there's always something happening.

The neighborhood seamlessly blends historic charm with contemporary energy. Nineteenth-century stone buildings house modern restaurants and trendy shops. Street performers entertain crowds on summer evenings. The aroma of fresh-baked goods mingles with the sizzle of shawarma. It's quintessentially Ottawa.

BeaverTails Birthplace

The original BeaverTails pastry stand opened in ByWard Market in 1978 and remains a must-visit. This iconic Canadian treat - fried dough stretched to resemble a beaver's tail and topped with sweet toppings - was invented right here.

What to Experience

Farmers Market

Fresh produce, maple syrup, artisan cheeses, baked goods, and flowers from local farms. The outdoor vendors (May-October) surround the market building with colorful stalls.

Dining Scene

Over 100 restaurants ranging from quick bites to fine dining. Find cuisines from around the world including Lebanese, Italian, Mexican, Japanese, and classic Canadian fare.

Nightlife

When the sun sets, the market transforms. Patio bars, live music venues, dance clubs, and cocktail lounges make this Ottawa's premier entertainment district.

Shopping

Boutique clothing stores, Canadian-made gifts, Indigenous art, vintage finds, and specialty food shops line the streets and fill the market building.

Must-Try Foods

  • BeaverTails: The iconic fried dough pastry. Try the classic cinnamon sugar or chocolate hazelnut.
  • Shawarma: Ottawa has one of the highest concentrations of shawarma shops in North America. ByWard Market has several excellent options.
  • Poutine: French fries, cheese curds, and gravy - find it at numerous restaurants throughout the market.
  • Maple Treats: Maple syrup, maple candy, maple cookies - stock up on Canada's sweetest export.
  • Fresh Bagels: Montreal-style bagels baked fresh daily at several bakeries.
  • Craft Beer: Local breweries and craft beer bars offer the best of Ottawa's brewing scene.
  • Farm-Fresh Produce: In season, sample Ontario strawberries, peaches, apples, and corn from local farms.

Foodie Tip

Come hungry and graze! The best way to experience ByWard Market is to sample from multiple vendors. Get a BeaverTail for breakfast, shawarma for lunch, browse the cheese shops, and save room for dinner at a sit-down restaurant.

Market Building

The ByWard Market Building is the heart of the neighborhood, housing permanent vendors selling fresh produce, meats, cheeses, baked goods, and specialty items. The two-storey building has operated continuously since 1926, though markets have existed on this site since 1826.

Inside You'll Find:

  • Fresh produce from local farms
  • Artisan cheese shops
  • Butcher shops with local meats
  • Bakeries with fresh bread and pastries
  • Specialty food importers
  • Maple syrup and Canadian food products
  • Quick-service food counters

The building is open daily from 6 AM to 6 PM. Early morning (before 9 AM) is when you'll find the freshest selection and interact with farmers directly. It gets busier around lunch time.

Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring (May-June): Outdoor vendors return. Fresh asparagus, rhubarb, and the first strawberries appear. Patios open up.
  • Summer (July-August): Peak season with the fullest selection of produce. Street performers entertain crowds. Extended patio hours.
  • Fall (September-October): Apple season, pumpkins, and fall vegetables. Oktoberfest celebrations at local pubs.
  • Winter (November-April): Indoor market continues. Christmas market and holiday events. Nearby Rideau Canal skating.

Getting There

ByWard Market is located in downtown Ottawa, a short walk from Parliament Hill and the Rideau Canal. The neighborhood is bounded roughly by St. Patrick Street to the north, Rideau Street to the south, Sussex Drive to the west, and King Edward Avenue to the east.

By Transit

Take the Confederation Line LRT to Rideau Station. The market is a 5-minute walk north. Multiple OC Transpo bus routes stop at Rideau Centre and along Rideau Street.

By Car

Several parking garages serve the market area, including at the Rideau Centre and municipal lots on Clarence Street. Street parking is limited and metered. Consider parking elsewhere and walking, as market streets can be congested.

On Foot

The market is a 10-minute walk from Parliament Hill, 5 minutes from the Rideau Canal locks, and 15 minutes from the National Gallery. The entire area is highly walkable.

Visitor Tips

  • Cash is Useful: While most restaurants take cards, some outdoor vendors and small stalls are cash-only.
  • Arrive Early for Produce: For the freshest farmers' market selection, arrive before 10 AM.
  • Summer Evenings: The market comes alive on warm summer evenings. Plan for dinner and stay for the nightlife.
  • Winter Warmth: In winter, the market building and indoor food halls offer warm refuge while exploring.
  • Pub Crawl Friendly: The concentration of bars makes ByWard Market ideal for a night out.
  • Combine with Nearby Attractions: Easily walkable to Parliament Hill, the National Gallery, and the Rideau Canal.
  • Weekend Brunch: Many restaurants offer excellent weekend brunch - reserve ahead for popular spots.
  • Safety at Night: Like any entertainment district, stay aware late at night, particularly on weekends.
