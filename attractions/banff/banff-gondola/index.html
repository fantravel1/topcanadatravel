About the Banff Gondola

The Banff Gondola is one of Canada's most spectacular mountain experiences, transporting visitors on an 8-minute journey from the base station at 1,583 metres to the summit of Sulphur Mountain at 2,281 metres. Since opening in 1959, this iconic attraction has welcomed millions of visitors seeking panoramic views of the Canadian Rockies.

The modern four-passenger gondola cabins travel along a 2.3-kilometre cable, climbing 698 metres through pristine mountain wilderness. Large windows on all sides provide unobstructed views throughout the ascent, often including glimpses of bighorn sheep, mountain goats, and other wildlife on the rocky slopes.

At the summit, the state-of-the-art interpretive centre features multiple observation decks, interactive exhibits about the region's geology and wildlife, the historic Cosmic Ray Station, gift shops, and dining options ranging from casual fare to the acclaimed Sky Bistro restaurant.

Six Mountain Ranges in One View

From the summit observation decks, you can see the Bow Valley, Mount Rundle, Cascade Mountain, Lake Minnewanka, and on clear days, views extend over 100 kilometres to reveal six distinct mountain ranges of the Canadian Rockies.

The Sulphur Mountain Boardwalk

One of the highlights of the Banff Gondola experience is the Sulphur Mountain Boardwalk, a 1-kilometre interpretive trail that follows the mountain's ridgeline from the upper terminal to Sanson's Peak. This fully accessible boardwalk offers stunning views in every direction and connects to the historic Cosmic Ray Station.

The boardwalk was designed to minimize environmental impact while providing safe access to the alpine environment. Interpretive panels along the route explain the geology, flora, fauna, and human history of the area. The walk takes approximately 20-30 minutes each way at a leisurely pace.

Boardwalk Tips

The summit is significantly cooler than the valley floor‚Äîbring an extra layer even in summer. The boardwalk can be windy, so secure loose items. Early morning and late evening visits offer the best light for photography and fewer crowds.

Cosmic Ray Station National Historic Site

At the far end of the boardwalk sits the Cosmic Ray Station, a small stone building that played a significant role in scientific history. Built in 1956, this research facility was part of an international network of stations studying cosmic rays‚Äîhigh-energy particles from outer space.

Norman Bethune Sanson, the first meteorologist to take regular readings on Sulphur Mountain, climbed to this summit over 1,000 times between 1903 and 1931, even in winter. The peak is now named Sanson's Peak in his honour. Today, the station serves as a small interpretive museum where visitors can learn about this fascinating scientific legacy.

Summit Dining Options

Sky Bistro

Perched at 2,281 metres, Sky Bistro is one of Canada's highest-elevation restaurants and one of Banff's finest dining experiences. The restaurant features floor-to-ceiling windows with panoramic mountain views, Canadian-inspired cuisine featuring Alberta beef and locally sourced ingredients, and an impressive wine list.

Northern Lights Alpine Kitchen

For a more casual dining experience, the Northern Lights Alpine Kitchen offers quick-service options including gourmet sandwiches, soups, salads, and Canadian comfort food. The cafeteria-style seating areas provide excellent views while accommodating families and groups.

  • Sky Bistro Reservations: Highly recommended, especially for dinner and sunset dining
  • Sunset Dinner: Request a window table about 45 minutes before sunset for the best experience
  • Budget Option: The Alpine Kitchen offers satisfying meals at lower prices
  • Dress Code: Smart casual for Sky Bistro; casual attire acceptable at Alpine Kitchen

Best Times to Visit

Sunrise (Summer)

Special early access tickets let you watch the sunrise paint the peaks pink and gold. Enjoy breakfast at the summit with virtually no crowds. Book well in advance.

Golden Hour (Sunset)

The most popular time for photographers. The mountains glow in warm light as the sun sets. Stay for twilight when the Bow Valley lights begin to twinkle.

Winter Magic

Snow-covered peaks and crisp clear air offer unmatched visibility. The summit is a winter wonderland. Dress warmly‚Äîsummit temperatures can reach -30¬∞C.

Stargazing (Evening)

Extended summer hours allow for stargazing above most light pollution. Special astronomy events are held throughout the year. The Milky Way is spectacular from here.

Getting There

The Banff Gondola base station is located at 100 Mountain Avenue, about a 5-minute drive from downtown Banff. The facility sits at the base of Sulphur Mountain, adjacent to the Upper Hot Springs.

By Car

From downtown Banff, head south on Banff Avenue, then turn left onto Mountain Avenue. The gondola has a large parking lot, but it fills up quickly in peak season. Arrive early or consider the shuttle service.

By Shuttle

Pursuit offers a complimentary shuttle service from downtown Banff to the gondola base station. Shuttles run regularly throughout the day from various pickup points including major hotels.

By Roam Transit

Roam Transit Route 1 (Sulphur Mountain) connects downtown Banff to the gondola base station. This is an affordable and eco-friendly option that runs frequently during peak season.
