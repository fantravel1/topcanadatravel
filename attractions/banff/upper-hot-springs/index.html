About Banff Upper Hot Springs

Perched on the slopes of Sulphur Mountain at 1,585 metres elevation, the Banff Upper Hot Springs is one of Canada's highest and most scenic hot spring pools. For over a century, visitors have come to soak in the naturally heated mineral waters while enjoying breathtaking views of Mount Rundle and the Bow Valley.

The hot springs were first discovered by railway workers in the 1880s and quickly became one of the main attractions that led to the establishment of Banff National Park. The original bathhouse was built in 1932 and has been carefully restored to maintain its historic charm while providing modern amenities.

The mineral-rich water emerges from deep within Sulphur Mountain at temperatures up to 47¬∞C (117¬∞F) and is cooled to a comfortable 37-40¬∞C (98-104¬∞F) in the pool. The water contains sulphate, calcium, bicarbonate, magnesium, and sodium‚Äîminerals believed to have therapeutic benefits for skin and muscles.

Year-Round Mountain Wellness

The Upper Hot Springs is open every day of the year, making it perfect for a relaxing soak after skiing in winter or hiking in summer. There's something magical about soaking in steaming water while snowflakes fall around you and the mountains are dusted in white.

What to Expect

The outdoor pool is spacious enough to accommodate many visitors yet maintains an intimate, relaxed atmosphere. The pool's edge offers views of Mount Rundle, Sulphur Mountain, and the Bow Valley spread out below. Steam rises from the water, creating a mystical atmosphere, especially in colder months.

Facilities Available

  • Change Rooms: Separate facilities for men and women with showers, changing areas, and hair dryers
  • Lockers: Available for rent to secure your belongings while you soak
  • Swimsuit Rental: Forgot your suit? Rentals available for $2 CAD
  • Towel Rental: Large towels available if you didn't bring your own
  • Locker Combos: Package deals include locker, towel, and swimsuit rental
  • Gift Shop: Souvenirs, swimwear, and hot springs merchandise

What to Bring

Bring your swimsuit, a towel, and flip-flops for the deck. Leave jewelry and electronics in a locker as the mineral water can tarnish metal. Bring a waterproof phone case if you want photos. In winter, bring a warm robe or coat to stay comfortable between the change room and pool.

Health Benefits of Hot Springs

For thousands of years, people have sought out hot springs for their healing properties. The mineral-rich waters of the Banff Upper Hot Springs are believed to offer several wellness benefits:

  • Muscle Relaxation: The warm water helps relax tight muscles and can relieve soreness after hiking or skiing
  • Joint Relief: Buoyancy reduces pressure on joints, providing relief for arthritis and joint pain
  • Improved Circulation: Heat causes blood vessels to dilate, improving blood flow throughout the body
  • Skin Health: Minerals in the water may help with certain skin conditions and leave skin feeling soft
  • Stress Relief: The combination of warm water, mountain views, and fresh air promotes deep relaxation
  • Better Sleep: Soaking before bed can help promote more restful sleep

Note: If you have heart conditions, are pregnant, or have other health concerns, consult your doctor before using hot springs. Don't stay in the hot water too long‚Äî15-20 minute sessions with breaks are recommended.

Best Times to Visit

Winter Wonderland

Soaking in hot water while surrounded by snow is an unforgettable experience. Steam rises dramatically in the cold air. Perfect after a day of skiing at nearby resorts.

Evening Soak

Visit after 7 PM for a magical experience under the stars. Watch the mountains turn pink at sunset, then see stars emerge in the dark sky above the steaming pool.

Early Morning

Arrive when doors open at 10 AM for the smallest crowds. Morning light on Mount Rundle is spectacular. Start your day relaxed before exploring the park.

Summer Evenings

Long summer days mean extended golden hours. The pool stays open until 10 PM, allowing you to enjoy the extended twilight of the Canadian Rockies.

Getting There

The Banff Upper Hot Springs is located at 1 Mountain Avenue, about a 5-minute drive from downtown Banff on the road to the Banff Gondola.

By Car

From downtown Banff, head south on Banff Avenue, then turn left onto Mountain Avenue. The hot springs are on your right, just before the Banff Gondola base station. Free parking is available, but the lot fills up quickly on busy days.

By Roam Transit

Roam Transit Route 1 (Sulphur Mountain) stops directly at the Upper Hot Springs. The bus runs regularly from downtown Banff and is an excellent option during peak times when parking is limited.

On Foot

The hot springs are approximately 3.5 km from downtown Banff. The walk takes about 45 minutes and is uphill, but it's a pleasant way to reach the springs, especially if you're comfortable with a moderate hike.

Frequently Asked Questions

Experts recommend soaking for 15-20 minutes at a time, then taking a break to cool down before returning. This prevents overheating and dehydration. Most visitors spend 1-2 hours total at the facility, including time to change and relax between soaks. Stay hydrated by drinking water before and after your visit.

It's recommended that pregnant women consult their doctor before visiting hot springs. The high water temperature can be potentially harmful, especially during the first trimester. If your doctor approves, limit your time in the water to short periods and keep your body temperature from rising too high.

There is a mild sulphur smell from the natural mineral water, but it's not overwhelming. Most visitors find it adds to the authentic hot spring experience. The smell dissipates quickly after you leave the pool, and showering after your soak removes any residual odour from your skin and hair.

The pool can get busy during peak times, especially weekends, holidays, and summer afternoons. For a more relaxed experience, visit early in the morning (10-11 AM), on weekday evenings, or during shoulder seasons (April-May or October-November). Winter visits are generally less crowded except during ski season holidays.

Outside food and beverages are not permitted in the pool area. Water bottles (plastic only, no glass) are allowed to stay hydrated. The facility does not have a cafe, so plan to eat before or after your visit. There are several restaurants nearby in downtown Banff.

Yes, the Banff Upper Hot Springs is wheelchair accessible. The facility has accessible change rooms, and a lift is available to help visitors enter and exit the pool. Staff are available to assist visitors with mobility needs. It's recommended to call ahead to arrange any special accommodations.
