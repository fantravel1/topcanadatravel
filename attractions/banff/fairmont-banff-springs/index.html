About the Fairmont Banff Springs

Rising majestically from the confluence of the Bow and Spray Rivers, the Fairmont Banff Springs is one of the world's most iconic hotels. Often called the "Castle in the Rockies," this grand railway hotel has been welcoming guests since 1888 and remains one of Canada's most photographed landmarks.

The hotel was built by the Canadian Pacific Railway as part of a strategy to attract tourists to ride the newly completed transcontinental railway. Architect Bruce Price designed the original structure in a Scottish Baronial style, inspired by the great castles of Scotland. The current building, completed in 1928 after a fire destroyed much of the original structure, expanded on Price's vision to create the castle we see today.

With 764 guest rooms, twelve restaurants and lounges, the world-renowned Willow Stream Spa, and a 27-hole championship golf course, the Banff Springs is a destination in itself. However, you don't need to be a hotel guest to experience its magic‚Äîday visitors are welcome to explore the public areas, dine, spa, or golf.

A Living Piece of History

The Fairmont Banff Springs has hosted royalty, celebrities, and millions of travellers over its 135+ year history. Every corridor tells a story, from the hand-painted murals in the Mount Stephen Hall to the ghost tales that have made it one of Canada's most haunted hotels.

Things to Do for Day Visitors

Heritage Tours

The best way to experience the hotel's rich history is on a guided heritage tour. Knowledgeable guides share stories of the hotel's construction, famous guests, architectural details, and yes‚Äîthe ghost stories. Tours typically last 60-90 minutes and take you through areas not normally accessible to the public.

Willow Stream Spa

The Willow Stream Spa at the Fairmont Banff Springs is one of the world's top destination spas. Spanning 38,000 square feet, this sanctuary offers mineral pools, waterfalls, a Hungarian mineral pool, steam rooms, and a full menu of treatments inspired by the mountain environment. Day spa packages are available for non-guests.

Fine Dining

The hotel features twelve dining venues ranging from casual to world-class fine dining. Eden is the signature restaurant, offering contemporary Canadian cuisine in an elegant setting. The Vermillion Room serves classic French dishes, while the Waldhaus serves traditional Bavarian fare in a charming alpine setting. Reservations are strongly recommended.

Golf

The Fairmont Banff Springs Golf Course is consistently ranked among the world's most scenic courses. The 27-hole championship layout winds through the Bow Valley with mountain views at every turn. The original course was designed by Stanley Thompson in 1928 and has hosted numerous prestigious tournaments.

Visiting Tips

Parking is available for day visitors for a fee. Arrive with time to explore the public spaces, gardens, and viewpoints. The hotel is busiest during summer‚Äîconsider visiting in spring or fall for a more relaxed experience. Dress code varies by venue, but smart casual is appropriate for most areas.

Dining Highlights

The Fairmont Banff Springs offers an exceptional culinary journey with twelve distinct dining venues:

  • Eden: Contemporary Canadian cuisine with mountain views‚Äîthe hotel's signature fine dining experience
  • The Vermillion Room: Classic French cuisine in an elegant setting reminiscent of a Parisian salon
  • Waldhaus: Authentic Bavarian cuisine served in a charming alpine chalet setting
  • Rundle Lounge & Hall: Afternoon tea, cocktails, and light fare in the hotel's grand social space
  • Stock Food & Drink: Farm-to-table Canadian cuisine featuring Alberta's finest ingredients
  • Castello Ristorante: Italian cuisine in a wine cellar atmosphere
  • Grapes Wine Bar: Extensive wine selection with small plates perfect for sharing
  • The Juniper Bistro: Casual dining with panoramic mountain and golf course views

Reservations are strongly recommended for all dining venues, especially during peak season. The hotel's concierge can assist with bookings.

Ghost Stories and Legends

The Fairmont Banff Springs has earned a reputation as one of Canada's most haunted hotels, with ghost stories dating back decades. The most famous is the ghost of a bride who is said to have died on the main staircase on her wedding night. Guests and staff have reported seeing her dancing alone in the ballroom or descending the stairs in her wedding gown.

Another frequently reported apparition is Sam the Bellman, a friendly ghost who helps guests find their rooms and even returns to assist with their luggage, only to disappear before they can tip him. Sam McAuley worked at the hotel for decades and reportedly continues his duties from beyond.

Whether you believe in ghosts or not, these tales add to the hotel's mystique and are shared on heritage tours. Some visitors specifically choose to stay in the reportedly haunted rooms for a chance to have their own supernatural experience.

Photography Spots

The Fairmont Banff Springs is one of Canada's most photographed buildings. Here are the best spots to capture its grandeur:

  • Surprise Corner: The classic viewpoint on the road to the hotel, offering the iconic angle of the castle with Sulphur Mountain behind
  • Bow Falls Viewpoint: Capture the hotel with Bow Falls in the foreground
  • Golf Course: The golf course offers stunning angles of the hotel (accessible during open season)
  • Interior Corridors: The grand hallways, Mount Stephen Hall, and Rundle Lounge offer beautiful interior shots
  • Gardens: The hotel's gardens provide beautiful foreground elements in summer
  • Spray River Trail: Views from the trail behind the hotel capture its full scale

Getting There

The Fairmont Banff Springs is located at 405 Spray Avenue, about a 10-minute drive from downtown Banff.

By Car

From Banff Avenue, turn east onto Spray Avenue and follow it to the hotel. Day visitor parking is available in the hotel's lot for a fee. During peak times, parking can be limited.

By Shuttle

The hotel operates a complimentary shuttle service from downtown Banff. Check with the concierge for current schedules. Roam Transit Route 6 also serves the hotel.

On Foot

A scenic 2 km walk from downtown Banff follows the Spray River Trail. You can also walk via Bow Falls, adding beautiful scenery to your journey.
