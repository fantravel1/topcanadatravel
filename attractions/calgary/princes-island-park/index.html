About Prince's Island Park

Prince's Island Park is a 20-hectare urban oasis situated on an island in the Bow River, just steps from downtown Calgary. Named after Peter Anthony Prince, a lumber baron who used the island for his sawmill in the late 1800s, the park has been a beloved public green space since 1950.

The island is connected to the mainland by pedestrian bridges at each end, making it easily accessible from the Eau Claire district, Kensington, and the downtown core. The park is part of Calgary's extensive pathway system, which stretches over 1,000 km throughout the city.

With its weeping willows, wetlands, walking paths, and stunning views of the Calgary skyline and Bow River, Prince's Island is the perfect escape from city life ‚Äî while being right in the middle of it.

Calgary Folk Music Festival

Every July, Prince's Island transforms into the venue for the Calgary Folk Music Festival, one of Canada's premier music events since 1980. The 4-day festival attracts over 50,000 attendees with folk, roots, and world music on multiple stages.

Things to Do

Walking & Jogging

The island's perimeter path is a 2.5 km loop, perfect for a morning jog or leisurely stroll with river views the entire way.

Picnicking

Bring a blanket and enjoy the large lawns. The Eau Claire area has cafes and restaurants for takeout if you forgot lunch.

Bird Watching

The wetlands on the island's west end attract diverse bird species including herons, ducks, and songbirds. Bring binoculars!

Cycling

The island connects to Calgary's pathway network. Rent a bike nearby and explore along the Bow River in either direction.

Festival Season

Beyond the Folk Festival, the park hosts Shakespeare in the Park, outdoor yoga, food festivals, and special events throughout summer.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (April-May)

Watch the park come alive with blooming flowers and trees. The geese and ducks return, and the pathways are perfect for exploring as the weather warms.

Summer (June-August)

Peak season with festivals, outdoor dining, and long summer evenings. The Folk Festival in late July is the highlight. Expect crowds on sunny weekends.

Fall (September-October)

The trees turn golden and the crowds thin out. Perfect weather for walking, and the River Caf√©'s patio stays open into October.

Winter (November-March)

The park is quieter but beautiful with snow. Cross-country skiing and winter walking are popular. Bundle up as wind off the river can be cold.

Getting There

Prince's Island Park is accessible from multiple points:

  • From Eau Claire: Walk across the bridge from 2nd Street SW (5-minute walk from downtown)
  • From Kensington: Cross the bridge at 10th Street NW (10-minute walk from Kensington shops)
  • By C-Train: Take any line to 3rd Street SW station, then walk north to the river (10 minutes)
  • By Bike: The Bow River Pathway connects the island to the entire city pathway network

Street parking is available along Memorial Drive and in the Eau Claire area. Expect to pay for parking on weekdays.
