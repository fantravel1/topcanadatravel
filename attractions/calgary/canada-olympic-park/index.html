About Canada Olympic Park

Canada Olympic Park, now operated as WinSport, was built for the 1988 Winter Olympics in Calgary. The ski jumps, bobsled track, and freestyle ski hills that hosted Olympic competitions are now open to the public for year-round recreation and adventure.

Located just 15 minutes west of downtown Calgary, the park offers something for everyone ‚Äî from gentle beginner ski slopes to heart-pounding bobsled rides down the actual Olympic track. It's one of only a handful of places in the world where everyday visitors can experience these once-in-a-lifetime thrills.

The venue continues its Olympic legacy as a training centre for Canadian winter sport athletes. You might spot national team members training on the ski jumps, half-pipes, or sliding tracks during your visit.

1988 Winter Olympics

Calgary hosted the XV Olympic Winter Games in February 1988. This venue was home to ski jumping, bobsled, luge, freestyle skiing, and Nordic combined events. The Olympic torch relay and opening ceremonies kicked off what Canadians remember as the "Friendly Games."

Must-Try Experiences

Zipline

Soar off the top of the ski jump tower ‚Äî the same spot Olympic athletes launched from in 1988. Reach speeds of 140 km/h on this thrilling 500-metre ride.

Bobsled Experience

Hurtle down the actual 1988 Olympic track with a professional pilot. Experience up to 4G forces and speeds over 100 km/h. Bucket-list worthy!

Skyline Luge

Ride a three-wheeled cart down the ski slopes on a 1.8 km track. Easy to learn, incredibly fun, and suitable for ages 6+. Multiple ride packages available.

Learn to Ski

Calgary's most convenient ski hill with excellent beginner terrain and ski school. Perfect for first-timers who want to try skiing before heading to the Rockies.

Sports Hall of Fame

Canada's Sports Hall of Fame is located at WinSport, celebrating the country's greatest athletes across all sports. Interactive exhibits, Olympic memorabilia, and inspiring stories make this a worthwhile stop, especially on inclement weather days.

  • Admission: $15 adult, $12 senior, $10 youth
  • Hours: 10 AM - 5 PM daily
  • Highlights: Olympic torch collection, interactive sports challenges, athlete profile exhibits

Getting There

Canada Olympic Park is located at the western edge of Calgary, easily accessible from both downtown and the Trans-Canada Highway.

By Car

From downtown Calgary, take Bow Trail west, which becomes the Trans-Canada Highway (Highway 1). Take the Canada Olympic Road exit (about 15 minutes from downtown). Free parking is available.

By Transit

Calgary Transit Route 408 runs from Sirocco C-Train Station to WinSport. The journey takes about 20 minutes from the station.

Visitor Tips

  • Book ahead for bobsled: The Bobsled Experience sells out weeks in advance, especially in summer
  • Combine activities: Multi-activity packages offer better value than individual purchases
  • Check conditions: Weather affects outdoor activities. Winter sports need snow; some summer activities pause in rain
  • Arrive early for skiing: The ski hill can get crowded on weekends, especially during ski school hours
  • Watch the ski jumpers: National team training is free to watch and incredibly impressive
