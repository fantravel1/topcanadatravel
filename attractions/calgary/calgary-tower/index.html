About the Calgary Tower

Rising 190.8 metres above downtown Calgary, the Calgary Tower has been the city's most iconic landmark since opening on June 30, 1968. Originally called the Husky Tower after its primary sponsor, it was renamed in 1971 and has since welcomed millions of visitors seeking the best views in the city.

The tower was built to mark Canada's centennial year (1967) and took 15 months to construct at a cost of $3.5 million. When completed, it was the tallest structure in Calgary and all of western Canada. While it's no longer the city's tallest building (that honour now goes to Brookfield Place at 247m), it remains the most beloved vantage point for panoramic views.

On a clear day, you can see the Rocky Mountains 80 kilometres to the west, the endless prairies stretching east, and the entire Calgary skyline spread beneath you. The observation deck offers 360-degree views through floor-to-ceiling windows, with informational displays identifying landmarks in every direction.

Olympic Flame Tradition

The Calgary Tower's torch was lit for the first time during the 1988 Winter Olympics and has been lit for special occasions ever since. The natural gas-fueled cauldron burns at the top of the tower during major events, Canadian holidays, and to honour significant achievements.

The Glass Floor Experience

The most thrilling feature of the Calgary Tower is the glass floor observation area, installed during a major renovation in 2005. Standing on 1.5 inches of reinforced tempered glass, you'll look straight down 160 metres to the street below.

The glass is incredibly strong‚Äîit can support over 3,000 kilograms, roughly the weight of a hippo or 45 adults standing on it at once. Despite knowing the statistics, many visitors feel a rush of adrenaline stepping onto the transparent surface for the first time.

Glass Floor Tips

For the best experience, visit on a sunny day when the streets below are busy. Watching tiny cars and pedestrians from directly above is mesmerizing. If you're nervous, start at the edge where the glass meets solid floor and gradually work your way to the centre.

SKY 360 Restaurant

Located at the observation level, SKY 360 is Calgary's only revolving restaurant. The dining room completes one full rotation every 45 minutes, giving guests an ever-changing panoramic view throughout their meal.

The menu features upscale Canadian cuisine with Alberta beef as the star attraction. Prices are higher than ground-level restaurants, but the experience is unique‚Äîand dining guests receive complimentary observation deck access.

Dining Tips

  • Reservations Required: Book at least 2-3 days in advance, especially for weekend dinners and sunset times
  • Sunset Timing: Request a table 30-45 minutes before sunset for the best light show
  • Dress Code: Smart casual; no shorts or athletic wear
  • Budget: Expect $60-100 per person for dinner with drinks
  • Free Observation Access: Your dinner reservation includes unlimited observation deck access

Best Times to Visit

Sunrise (Early Birds)

Special sunrise tickets available seasonally. Watch the sun rise over the prairies and light up the Rocky Mountains. Smallest crowds of the day.

Golden Hour (Sunset)

The most popular time. The Rockies turn pink and orange as the sun sets. Arrive 30-45 minutes before sunset and stay for twilight.

Clear Winter Days

Cold, clear winter days offer the best visibility. After a chinook clears the air, you can see mountains over 100km away. The city lights sparkle in early darkness.

Stampede (July)

During the Calgary Stampede, see the fairgrounds lit up at night. The tower offers unique aerial views of the midway and fireworks shows.

Photography Tips

  • Reflections: To minimize window reflections, wear dark clothing and press your lens close to the glass
  • Tripods: Small tabletop tripods are permitted; full-size tripods may not be allowed during busy times
  • Glass Floor Shots: Shoot straight down for the most dramatic effect. A wide-angle lens captures more of the scene
  • Panoramas: The observation deck is perfect for stitched panorama photos. Take overlapping shots as you walk the circle
  • Night Photography: Friday and Saturday nights stay open until 10 PM‚Äîideal for city lights and long exposures

Getting There

The Calgary Tower is located in the heart of downtown Calgary, steps from the Calgary City Hall C-Train Station. It's within the Free Fare Zone, so you can ride the C-Train downtown at no charge.

By Transit

Take any C-Train (Red or Blue Line) to City Hall Station. The tower is a 2-minute walk east. All downtown C-Train travel is free.

By Car

The tower has no dedicated parking, but there are several parkades nearby. The Palliser Parkade (119 9th Ave SW) and Telus Convention Centre Parkade are within a 5-minute walk. Expect to pay $5-15 depending on duration.

Walking from Stephen Avenue

If you're exploring the Stephen Avenue pedestrian mall, the tower is 2 blocks south at the corner of 9th Avenue and Centre Street.
