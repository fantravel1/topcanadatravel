About Calgary Zoo

The Calgary Zoo is one of Canada's largest and most visited zoos, welcoming over 1.3 million visitors annually. Located on St. George's Island in the Bow River, just east of downtown, the zoo has been a Calgary institution since 1929.

Home to over 1,000 animals representing nearly 120 species, the zoo is organized into distinct zones that recreate natural habitats from around the world. From Canadian wildlife to African savannah animals, there's something for every nature enthusiast.

Beyond being an attraction, the Calgary Zoo is a leader in conservation and wildlife research. Their breeding programs have helped save endangered species, and educational programs reach thousands of students each year.

Conservation Success

The Calgary Zoo has been instrumental in saving species from extinction. Their whooping crane breeding program helped bring the species back from fewer than 20 birds in the 1940s, and they continue vital work with Vancouver Island marmots, burrowing owls, and greater sage-grouse.

Zoo Zones

Canadian Wilds

Meet the animals of the Great White North: grizzly bears, wolves, cougars, bison, moose, and more. Beautifully designed habitats recreate Rocky Mountain and prairie ecosystems.

Penguin Plunge

Walk through an underwater viewing tunnel as penguins swim overhead. Four species call this exhibit home, including gentoo, king, and rockhopper penguins.

Destination Africa

Explore African ecosystems from the savannah to the rainforest. Giraffes, hippos, gorillas, and lions are among the incredible animals in this immersive zone.

Land of Lemurs

Madagascar-inspired exhibit featuring ring-tailed lemurs, red-ruffed lemurs, and other unique species from the island continent.

Dorothy Harvie Gardens

Six acres of beautiful botanical gardens surrounding the zoo. Butterflies, flowers, and peaceful pathways offer a tranquil break.

Prehistoric Park

Life-size dinosaur statues set in natural badlands terrain. Learn about Alberta's prehistoric past while kids climb and explore.

Special Events

ZOOLIGHTS (November-January)

The zoo transforms into a winter wonderland with over 2 million lights. ZOOLIGHTS is one of Calgary's most beloved holiday traditions, featuring light displays, hot chocolate, and festive activities.

Boo at the Zoo (October)

Family-friendly Halloween fun with costume contests, trick-or-treating stations, and spooky (but not too scary) decorations throughout the zoo.

Summer Nights (July-August)

Extended summer hours with live entertainment, food festivals, and special animal programs. A great way to experience the zoo in cooler evening temperatures.

Getting There

The Calgary Zoo is located on St. George's Island, accessible from Memorial Drive NE or the Zoo C-Train Station (Blue Line).

By C-Train

Take the Blue Line (69 Street or Saddletowne direction) to Zoo Station. The station is right at the zoo entrance ‚Äî it's the easiest way to visit.

By Car

Exit Memorial Drive at 12 Street NE. Parking is $15/day in the main lot, or $10 in the overflow lot (longer walk). Free parking is available along Memorial Drive on weekends and after 6 PM weekdays.

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive early: Animals are most active in the morning, especially in summer before it gets hot
  • Check feeding times: The daily schedule includes talks and feeding demonstrations ‚Äî plan around them
  • Wear comfortable shoes: The zoo covers 120 acres with lots of walking
  • Bring snacks: Food options are available but can get busy at lunch; packing snacks saves time
  • Visit in winter: Smaller crowds and some animals (like the snow leopard) are more active in cold weather
  • Don't skip Penguin Plunge: The underwater tunnel is worth waiting for ‚Äî pure magic
