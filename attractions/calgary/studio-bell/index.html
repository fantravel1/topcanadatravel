About Studio Bell

Studio Bell is a 160,000-square-foot architectural marvel in Calgary's revitalized East Village. Opened in 2016, this striking terracotta-clad building houses the National Music Centre ‚Äî Canada's only museum dedicated entirely to music and sound.

The museum's collection spans five floors of interactive exhibits, rare instruments, recording studios, and performance spaces. Unlike traditional museums where you look but don't touch, Studio Bell invites visitors to play instruments, mix tracks, and experience music firsthand.

The building itself is an attraction. Designed by Allied Works Architecture, it features nine "vessels" ‚Äî tower-like structures ‚Äî connected by bridges with panoramic views of the Bow River and downtown Calgary.

The Rolling Stones Mobile Studio

One of Studio Bell's crown jewels is the Rolling Stones Mobile Studio ‚Äî the actual recording truck used by the Stones, Led Zeppelin, Deep Purple, and other legendary artists. "Smoke on the Water" and "Stairway to Heaven" were recorded using this equipment.

Must-See Exhibits

Canadian Music Hall of Fame

Explore the history of Canadian music legends from Joni Mitchell to Drake. Artifacts, memorabilia, and interactive displays honour the nation's greatest artists.

Instrument Collection

Over 2,000 rare instruments including Elton John's piano, the Tragically Hip's guitars, and vintage synthesizers. Many are playable by visitors.

Recording Studios

Actual working recording studios where you can try mixing tracks. The facility hosts real recording sessions with Canadian artists.

Sound & Science

Interactive exhibits explaining the physics of sound, acoustics, and how instruments create music. Perfect for curious minds of all ages.

  • Check for concerts: The Performance Hall hosts regular shows; some are included with admission

Getting There

Studio Bell is in the East Village, a 15-minute walk from downtown Calgary or the Calgary Tower. Take the C-Train to City Hall Station and walk east along the RiverWalk, or to Victoria Park/Stampede Station.

Limited street parking is available, but the East Village Parkade on 4th Street SE offers convenient underground parking.
