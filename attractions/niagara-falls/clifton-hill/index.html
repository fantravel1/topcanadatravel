About Clifton Hill

Welcome to "The Street of Fun by the Falls" - Niagara Falls' famous entertainment district that has been delighting visitors since 1925. Clifton Hill is a vibrant, neon-lit strip packed with family attractions, haunted houses, arcades, wax museums, restaurants, and the iconic Niagara SkyWheel.

Located just steps from Horseshoe Falls, Clifton Hill provides the perfect complement to the natural wonder of Niagara. After experiencing the majesty of the waterfalls, visitors flock to this colourful street for food, fun, and entertainment. The hill runs from Victoria Avenue down to the Niagara Parkway, putting the falls just a 5-minute walk from the action.

The strip comes alive especially after dark, when millions of lights illuminate the facades of the various attractions. The SkyWheel dominates the skyline, offering elevated views of both the entertainment district and the illuminated falls. Whether you're traveling with kids, on a date, or celebrating with friends, Clifton Hill has something for everyone.

Niagara's Entertainment Heart

Clifton Hill draws millions of visitors annually with its unique blend of classic tourist attractions and modern entertainment. It's touristy by design - and proudly so! Embrace the neon, the novelty, and the nostalgia of this uniquely Niagara experience.

Major Attractions

Niagara SkyWheel

Niagara's landmark Ferris wheel standing 53 metres tall. Climate-controlled gondolas offer 360-degree views of the falls, gorge, and city. Spectacular at night when the falls are illuminated. $15-18 CAD.

Nightmares Fear Factory

Canada's scariest haunted house - a darkened labyrinth of terror. Famous for its "chicken photo gallery" of visitors who couldn't finish. Not recommended for young children. ~$15 CAD.

Movieland Wax Museum

Over 100 lifelike celebrity wax figures including movie stars, musicians, and historical figures. Great for photos with your favourite celebrities. ~$15 CAD.

Ripley's Believe It or Not!

Museum of the weird and wonderful featuring oddities from around the world. Interactive exhibits, optical illusions, and strange artifacts. ~$18 CAD.

Dinosaur Adventure Golf

Two 18-hole mini golf courses with animatronic dinosaurs, waterfalls, and volcano. Fun for all ages, especially dramatic after dark. ~$12 CAD.

Great Canadian Midway

Massive indoor arcade with hundreds of games, rides, and redemption games. WIN prizes, play classics, and enjoy modern VR experiences. Pay-per-game or unlimited passes.

Wizard's Golf

Indoor blacklight mini golf with magical wizard theming. Glow-in-the-dark course perfect for a rainy day or hot afternoon. ~$10 CAD.

Haunted House

Classic haunted house experience with live actors and special effects. Different theme than Nightmares Fear Factory - both are worthwhile for horror fans. ~$15 CAD.

Saving Money with Fun Passes

If you plan to visit multiple attractions, Clifton Hill's combo passes offer significant savings over individual tickets. Several pass options are available depending on your interests.

Popular Pass Options

  • 6 Attraction Fun Pass (~$35 CAD): Includes SkyWheel, Dinosaur Golf, Movieland Wax Museum, and more. Best value for families.
  • Ultimate Fun Pass (~$60 CAD): Includes nearly everything on the hill including haunted houses and premium attractions.
  • Couples Night Pass: Romantic package with SkyWheel and select attractions. Great for date night.
  • Kids Pass: Tailored for younger visitors with age-appropriate attractions.

Money-Saving Tip

Purchase passes online before your visit for additional discounts. Passes are valid for one day, so plan to spend several hours on the hill. If you're only interested in 1-2 attractions, individual tickets may be more economical.

Dining Options

Clifton Hill offers numerous dining options from quick bites to sit-down restaurants. Most are casual, family-friendly establishments with something for every taste and budget.

Popular Restaurants

  • IHOP: All-day breakfast favourite with pancakes and American classics
  • Boston Pizza: Canadian chain with pizza, pasta, and sports bar atmosphere
  • Rainforest Cafe: Themed dining with animatronic animals and jungle atmosphere. Great for kids.
  • Kelsey's: Casual dining with burgers, ribs, and Canadian pub fare
  • Hard Rock Cafe: Rock music memorabilia and American comfort food
  • Wendy's, McDonald's, Burger King: Fast food options for quick, budget-friendly meals

Quick Bites

  • Pizza slices and hot dogs from street vendors
  • Ice cream and frozen treats
  • Fudge shops with homemade candies
  • Tim Hortons for coffee and donuts
  • Popcorn, cotton candy, and carnival treats

Best Times to Visit

Time of Day

  • Daytime: Good for families with young children. Attractions are less crowded, and you can see everything in natural light before evening falls illumination.
  • Evening: The hill truly comes alive after dark! Neon lights, illuminated attractions, and the SkyWheel against the night sky create a magical atmosphere.
  • Late Night: Weekend nights see the most activity with attractions open until 1-2 AM. Great energy but can be very crowded.

Day of Week

  • Weekdays: Smaller crowds, shorter lines, easier parking
  • Weekends: More energetic atmosphere but longer wait times for popular attractions
  • Holidays: Very busy but often feature special events and extended hours

Season

  • Summer (June-August): Peak season with longest hours and most attractions open. Busiest crowds.
  • Fall (September-October): Halloween season brings special haunted events. Beautiful weather for exploring.
  • Winter (November-March): Some attractions have reduced hours, but the Winter Festival of Lights adds millions of additional lights throughout the area. SkyWheel operates year-round.
  • Spring (April-May): Attractions begin extending hours. Crowds build as summer approaches.

Halloween on Clifton Hill

October is haunted house heaven! The attractions enhance their scare factor, and special Halloween events run throughout the month. If you enjoy being frightened, this is the time to visit.

Getting There and Parking

Walking

If you're staying in the Fallsview or tourist district, Clifton Hill is likely within walking distance. From the falls viewing area, walk up the hill - you can't miss the neon lights and Ferris wheel.

By Car

Several parking lots surround Clifton Hill with varying rates. Expect to pay $15-30 CAD per day during peak season. The Clifton Hill municipal lot is convenient. Street parking is limited and fills quickly.

By WEGO Bus

WEGO Green Line and Red Line both stop near Clifton Hill. The Falls Incline Railway connects the Table Rock area directly to the top of Clifton Hill (included with WEGO pass).

Falls Incline Railway

This historic funicular connects the Fallsview area (at Portage Road) with the bottom of Clifton Hill near Table Rock. A convenient way to travel between the falls and the hill. Included with WEGO pass or small fee.
