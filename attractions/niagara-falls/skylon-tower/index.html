About Skylon Tower

Rising 160 metres above the Niagara landscape, Skylon Tower has been providing visitors with the definitive aerial view of Niagara Falls since 1965. The tower's distinctive Y-shaped design has become an iconic part of the Niagara skyline and offers unparalleled 360-degree views of all three waterfalls, the Niagara Gorge, and the surrounding landscape of both Canada and the United States.

From the observation deck, located 236 metres above the base of the falls, you can see Horseshoe Falls (the Canadian falls), American Falls, and the delicate Bridal Veil Falls laid out before you. On clear days, visibility extends to Toronto's CN Tower 100 kilometres to the north and Buffalo, New York to the south.

The tower features both indoor and outdoor observation levels, a revolving dining room that completes one rotation per hour, and "The Summit Suite" buffet restaurant. The "Yellow Bug" elevators on the exterior of the tower are themselves a unique experience, offering views during the 52-second ride to the top.

The Best Overall View

While other attractions get you closer to the falls, Skylon Tower is the only place to see all three waterfalls at once, plus the entire gorge, both cities, and the layout of the Niagara River. It's perfect for understanding the geography and planning what to visit.

Observation Deck Experience

The observation experience begins with a ride in one of Skylon's famous "Yellow Bug" exterior elevators. These glass-enclosed elevators travel along the outside of the tower, giving you expanding views as you rise 52 seconds to the top.

Indoor Observation Deck

The indoor level offers floor-to-ceiling windows with climate-controlled comfort year-round. Informational displays identify landmarks in every direction, and binocular viewers are available for closer looks at the falls and surrounding areas. This level is perfect for rainy days, extreme temperatures, or when you want to take your time studying the views.

Outdoor Observation Deck

One level above, the outdoor deck provides open-air viewing with no glass between you and the panorama. Feel the breeze and hear the distant thunder of the falls while enjoying unobstructed photography opportunities. The outdoor deck may close in severe weather or high winds.

Photography Tip

For the best photos, visit the outdoor deck at golden hour (1-2 hours before sunset). The warm light beautifully illuminates the falls and landscape. Stay past sunset to capture the falls illumination from above - a truly unique perspective.

Dining Options

Revolving Dining Room

Fine dining 160m above Niagara. The restaurant completes one full rotation every hour, ensuring every table eventually gets every view. Reservations include observation deck access.

The Summit Suite Buffet

Family-friendly buffet dining with panoramic views. More casual atmosphere and pricing than the Revolving Dining Room. Popular for Sunday brunch with falls views.

Sunset Dinner

Book a window table at sunset for an unforgettable experience. Watch the falls transform from daylight views to illuminated colours as you dine.

Free Observation with Dining

Restaurant reservations include complimentary observation deck access. If you're planning to dine, this offers excellent value compared to separate tickets.

Dining Tips

  • Reservations: Highly recommended for both restaurants, especially for dinner and weekend brunch. Book 1-2 weeks ahead in summer.
  • Sunset Tables: Request a sunset time when booking. Aim for 30-45 minutes before sunset for optimal light.
  • Dress Code: The Revolving Dining Room has smart casual dress code. Summit Suite Buffet is more casual.
  • Budget: Expect $50-80 per person for Revolving Dining Room dinner, $35-50 for Summit Suite Buffet.

What You'll See

  • Horseshoe Falls: The massive Canadian horseshoe-shaped waterfall, 670 metres wide and 57 metres high. See the entire cascade from above.
  • American Falls: The 260-metre wide U.S. waterfall with its massive talus rock pile at the base.
  • Bridal Veil Falls: The smallest and most delicate of the three falls, separated from American Falls by Luna Island.
  • Niagara Gorge: The 11-kilometre canyon carved by the retreating falls over 12,000 years.
  • Rainbow Bridge: The international border crossing connecting Canada and the U.S.
  • Niagara River: Trace the river from Lake Erie to Lake Ontario, seeing its dramatic change at the falls.
  • Both Cities: Canadian Niagara Falls to the north and west, American Niagara Falls to the east.
  • Toronto Skyline: On clear days, spot the CN Tower 100 km to the north.

Best Times to Visit

Time of Day

  • Morning (10-11 AM): Clearest visibility, smallest crowds, best for photography with sun behind you
  • Midday: Good light on all three falls, but busiest crowds
  • Golden Hour (1-2 hours before sunset): Warm light creates stunning photos, moderate crowds
  • Evening/Night: See the falls illumination from above. The coloured lights on the water viewed from this height is magical.

Season

  • Summer: Longest hours, warmest weather for outdoor deck. Most crowded but most convenient.
  • Fall: Beautiful autumn colours frame the falls. Fewer crowds. Clear air often provides excellent visibility.
  • Winter: Ice formations on the falls create a spectacular winter wonderland view. Indoor deck perfect for cold days.
  • Spring: Highest water flow after snowmelt. Weather can be variable but crowds are smaller.

Fireworks from Above

During summer weekends and holidays, Niagara Falls hosts fireworks shows. Watching from Skylon Tower puts you at eye level with the pyrotechnics, with the illuminated falls as a backdrop. Book dinner reservations well in advance for fireworks nights.

Getting There

Skylon Tower is located on Robinson Street, a short distance from Clifton Hill and the Fallsview tourist district.

By Foot

A 10-minute walk from Clifton Hill or the Fallsview hotels. Follow Robinson Street - the tower is visible from most of the tourist area.

By WEGO Bus

WEGO Green Line and Blue Line both have stops near Skylon Tower. The "Skylon" stop puts you right at the base.

By Car

Skylon Tower has its own free parking lot for visitors. Enter from Robinson Street. This is one of the only free parking options in the Niagara tourist district.

Accessibility

The tower is fully wheelchair accessible. Elevators accommodate wheelchairs, and both observation levels are accessible. Restaurant accessibility available with advance notice.
