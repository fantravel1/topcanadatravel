About Hornblower Niagara Cruises

There is no experience at Niagara Falls more iconic than boarding a boat and sailing directly into the thundering mist of Horseshoe Falls. Hornblower Niagara Cruises operates Canada's only boat tour to the base of the falls, offering visitors the closest possible view of one of the world's most powerful waterfalls.

The "Voyage to the Falls" takes passengers on a 20-minute journey that begins with panoramic views of the American Falls and Bridal Veil Falls, then continues past the massive rock pile of the Niagara Gorge before entering the legendary Horseshoe Falls basin. Here, 2,800 cubic metres of water crashes down every second from 57 metres above - creating a wall of mist, sound, and raw natural power that surrounds the boat.

Hornblower's fleet of catamarans are modern, eco-friendly vessels that replaced the older boats in 2014. Each ship can carry up to 700 passengers across multiple open-air decks, giving everyone ample space for viewing and photography. The catamarans feature zero-emission electric propulsion when departing and returning to the dock.

The Mist Experience

Getting wet is part of the adventure! As the boat enters the Horseshoe Falls basin, you'll be engulfed in spray from the crashing water. Complimentary recyclable ponchos are provided, but expect your face, hair, and shoes to get thoroughly soaked. Most visitors describe it as refreshing and exhilarating.

What to Expect on Your Voyage

Your experience begins at the departure plaza at the foot of Clifton Hill, where you'll descend via a 54-metre enclosed funicular to the boarding area at water level. After receiving your signature red poncho, you'll board the catamaran on the lower deck.

As the boat departs, you'll cruise along the gorge with views of the American Falls (260 metres wide) and the delicate Bridal Veil Falls separated by Luna Island. The captain provides commentary throughout the journey, sharing facts about the falls' geology, history, and famous daredevils.

The climax comes when the boat enters the Horseshoe Falls basin. The roar becomes deafening, the mist completely envelops the vessel, and you'll feel the raw power of 168,000 cubic metres of water falling per minute. The boat remains in the basin for several minutes before returning to calmer waters.

Photo Tips

Take photos on the way out when conditions are drier. Use a waterproof phone case or camera. The best shots come from the upper deck on the starboard (right) side when facing the falls. Keep a microfiber cloth to wipe your lens between shots.

Cruise Options

Voyage to the Falls (Daytime)

The classic 20-minute journey into the mist basin. Departs every 15 minutes during peak season. Experience the full power of Horseshoe Falls in daylight.

Falls Illumination Cruise

Evening cruise when the falls are lit with colourful LED lights. A magical experience as the boat glides toward glowing water. Available May-October after dusk.

Falls Fireworks Cruise

Special cruises during holiday fireworks shows. Watch the pyrotechnics reflect off the falls and water from the best vantage point. Limited seasonal availability.

Sunrise Breakfast Cruise

Limited seasonal offering featuring early morning departure with continental breakfast. Smallest crowds and beautiful morning light on the falls.

Best Times to Visit

Time of Day

  • Early Morning (9-10 AM): Smallest crowds, beautiful morning light, cooler temperatures mean less mist evaporation
  • Midday (12-3 PM): Busiest times, longer queues, but rainbows often form in the mist on sunny days
  • Golden Hour (1-2 hours before sunset): Warm light on the falls, moderate crowds, leads into illumination cruise option
  • Evening: Illumination cruises offer a completely different experience with the falls lit in vibrant colours

Season

  • May-June: Spring water levels are highest, creating maximum flow. Weather can be unpredictable.
  • July-August: Peak season with longest hours and warmest weather. Most crowded but most convenient.
  • September-October: Beautiful fall colours along the gorge. Smaller crowds than summer. Water flow may be slightly reduced.
  • November: Final weeks of the season. Coldest weather but dramatic atmosphere and minimal crowds.

What to Bring and Wear

  • Footwear: Wear shoes that can get wet - sandals with straps or water shoes are ideal. Avoid flip-flops that could slip.
  • Clothing: Dress in layers you don't mind getting damp. Quick-dry fabrics are best. The poncho covers your body but not your legs or feet.
  • Electronics: Waterproof phone case highly recommended. Consider leaving valuable electronics in a locker on shore.
  • Glasses: Bring a strap for eyeglasses - the mist is intense. Contact lens wearers may prefer glasses.
  • Camera: Waterproof camera or waterproof housing for regular camera. GoPro-style action cameras work well.
  • Sun Protection: Sunscreen and sunglasses (with retainer strap) for sunny days.

Lockers Available

Secure lockers are available at the departure plaza. Consider storing valuables, extra layers, and items you want to keep dry. Small lockers are $2 CAD, large lockers $4 CAD.

Getting There

The Hornblower departure plaza is located at 5920 Niagara Parkway, at the bottom of Clifton Hill. The funicular entrance is clearly marked and accessible from the Table Rock area.

By Foot from Clifton Hill

Walk down Clifton Hill toward the falls. The Hornblower entrance is at the bottom on your left, near the foot of the hill. Look for the distinctive glass funicular building.

By WEGO Bus

The WEGO Green Line and Red Line both stop near Hornblower. The "Table Rock" and "Hornblower Landing" stops are both within a 3-minute walk.

By Car

Use the Niagara Parks parking lots at Table Rock or the Floral Showhouse. Rates are $30+ CAD per day in peak season. The Clifton Hill area also has private lots at varying rates.

Accessibility

The attraction is wheelchair accessible. The funicular accommodates wheelchairs, and the catamarans have accessible viewing areas on the lower deck. Notify staff when purchasing tickets to arrange assistance.
