About the Falls Illumination

Every night after dusk, Niagara Falls transforms from a daytime wonder into a magical nighttime spectacle. The Niagara Falls Illumination bathes both the Canadian Horseshoe Falls and the American Falls in vibrant colours, creating one of the most photographed night scenes in the world - and it's completely free to view.

The current illumination system, upgraded in 2016, uses LED technology to produce over 1.4 billion colour combinations. Twenty-one powerful LED fixtures positioned along the Canadian shoreline project millions of lumens of light onto the cascading water. The lights cycle through colours including brilliant blues, vivid greens, passionate reds, regal purples, and countless gradient combinations.

The tradition of illuminating Niagara Falls began in 1860 for a visit by the Prince of Wales. The first permanent lighting system was installed in 1925, and the technology has been upgraded several times since. Today's system is more energy-efficient than its predecessors while being dramatically more colourful and powerful.

A Nightly Wonder

Unlike many attractions that require tickets or reservations, the illumination is simply there for everyone to enjoy. Whether you're watching from a restaurant patio, a hotel room, or standing at the brink of the falls, the light show is yours to experience at no cost.

Best Viewing Locations

Table Rock

The closest viewpoint to Horseshoe Falls. Stand at the brink and watch the illuminated water cascade over the edge. Feel the mist as you watch the colours change. Parking available.

Queen Victoria Park

Spacious parkland directly facing the falls with benches and walking paths. Perfect for a leisurely evening stroll while enjoying the lights. Less crowded than Table Rock.

Skylon Tower

See the illumination from 160 metres above. The aerial perspective shows the coloured water against the dark gorge. Observation deck or Revolving Dining Room offer unique views.

Hornblower Illumination Cruise

Special evening cruises put you on the water surrounded by illuminated falls. A magical experience as coloured light reflects off the mist. Seasonal availability.

Other Great Viewpoints

  • Fallsview Hotels: Many hotels have falls-view rooms where you can watch the illumination from your window or balcony.
  • Clifton Hill: The lower end of Clifton Hill offers views of American Falls illumination while you enjoy the entertainment district.
  • Rainbow Bridge: Views from the international bridge (pedestrian access) offer a unique perspective between the two countries.
  • Niagara SkyWheel: Ride the Ferris wheel for elevated, 360-degree views of the illuminated falls and surrounding city.
  • Restaurant Patios: Many restaurants along the parkway and in Fallsview have outdoor seating with illumination views.

Illumination Schedule

The illumination runs every night of the year, starting at dusk and continuing until at least 1:00 AM (often until 2:00 AM on weekends). Because it begins at dusk, the start time varies dramatically by season.

Approximate Start Times

  • January-February: 5:00 PM - 5:30 PM
  • March: 6:30 PM - 7:30 PM (DST transition)
  • April: 8:00 PM - 8:30 PM
  • May-June: 8:45 PM - 9:15 PM
  • July: 9:15 PM - 9:30 PM
  • August: 8:45 PM - 9:00 PM
  • September: 7:30 PM - 8:00 PM
  • October: 6:30 PM - 7:30 PM (DST transition)
  • November-December: 5:00 PM - 5:30 PM

Best Viewing Time

Arrive 15-30 minutes after official sunset for the best experience. At this point, it's dark enough to see the colours vividly, but there may still be some twilight glow on the horizon adding atmosphere to photos.

Special Colour Displays

Throughout the year, the illumination features special colour schemes to mark holidays, awareness campaigns, and celebrations. These displays show the falls bathed in specific colours that have meaning beyond their beauty.

Notable Special Illuminations

  • Valentine's Day (February 14): Red and pink for romance
  • St. Patrick's Day (March 17): Green
  • Canada Day (July 1): Red and white
  • Independence Day (July 4): Red, white, and blue
  • Pride Month (June): Rainbow colours
  • Breast Cancer Awareness (October): Pink
  • Remembrance Day (November 11): Red
  • Christmas/Holidays: Red and green
  • New Year's Eve: Special countdown displays

Winter Festival of Lights

From November through January, the Niagara Falls Winter Festival of Lights adds millions of additional lights throughout the parks and along the parkway. Combined with the falls illumination, the entire area becomes a wonderland of light displays - all free to view.

Fireworks Shows

During summer months (typically mid-May through mid-October), spectacular fireworks displays accompany the illumination on select nights. The pyrotechnics are launched from the Canadian side and viewed against the backdrop of the illuminated falls.

Fireworks Schedule (Typical)

  • Fridays: 10:00 PM (May-October)
  • Sundays: 10:00 PM (May-October)
  • Holiday Weekends: Friday, Saturday, Sunday and holiday Monday
  • Canada Day (July 1): Enhanced display at 10:00 PM
  • Victoria Day: Special display
  • Labour Day: Season finale display

Fireworks shows typically last 10-15 minutes. For the best views, position yourself at Table Rock, Queen Victoria Park, or any of the Fallsview restaurants with outdoor patios. The Hornblower Fireworks Cruise puts you on the water for a truly memorable perspective.

Fireworks Viewing Tip

Arrive at your chosen viewpoint at least 30-45 minutes before the scheduled fireworks on summer weekends. Popular spots fill up quickly. Bring a blanket for sitting on the grass in Queen Victoria Park.

Photography Tips

  • Tripod Essential: Night photography requires long exposures. A sturdy tripod is crucial for sharp images.
  • Low ISO: Use ISO 100-400 to minimize noise in your night shots.
  • Long Exposure: Try 2-10 second exposures to capture the flowing water with smooth, silky effects.
  • Remote Trigger: Use a remote shutter release or your camera's timer to avoid shake.
  • Multiple Exposures: Take many shots as the colours change. Each frame will be unique.
  • Include Context: Frame shots to include the falls and surrounding illuminated cityscape for context.
  • Smartphone Photography: Modern smartphones have excellent night modes. Use night mode and hold very still, or prop against a railing.
  • Arrive Early: Scout your location and set up before darkness falls completely.
