About the Broadway District

The Broadway District is the beating heart of Saskatoon's arts and culture scene. Located in the historic Nutana neighbourhood just across the Broadway Bridge from downtown, this walkable stretch of Broadway Avenue is lined with independent boutiques, eclectic cafes, cozy restaurants, vintage shops, and live music venues.

Unlike chain-dominated commercial strips, Broadway has maintained its independent character. Locally owned businesses give the district its unique personality, from bookshops and record stores to artisan bakeries and craft cocktail bars. It is the kind of place where you can spend an entire afternoon browsing, eating, and soaking up the local creative energy.

The district is anchored by the historic Broadway Theatre, a beloved 1946 cinema that now serves as a community hub for independent film, live performances, and cultural events. Its marquee is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Saskatoon.

Broadway Theatre

The historic Broadway Theatre, built in 1946, is the cultural anchor of the district. Today it screens independent and international films, hosts live music performances, comedy shows, and community events. Check their schedule for current programming during your visit.

What to Do

Independent Shopping

Browse unique boutiques, vintage clothing stores, bookshops, record stores, and artisan gift shops. The district is a treasure trove for one-of-a-kind finds you won't find anywhere else.

Cafes & Restaurants

From specialty coffee roasters to farm-to-table restaurants, Broadway offers diverse dining options. Try local favourites for brunch, craft beer, or a leisurely dinner.

Live Music & Entertainment

Multiple venues host live music, comedy, and performing arts throughout the week. The Broadway Theatre and local pubs feature emerging and established artists in intimate settings.

Street Life & People Watching

In summer, patios line the avenue and the street comes alive with buskers, street art, and festivals. Grab a coffee, find a patio seat, and enjoy Saskatoon's most vibrant streetscape.

Getting There & Around

The Broadway District is easily accessible on foot from downtown Saskatoon. Simply walk across the Broadway Bridge‚Äîone of the city's most scenic crossings‚Äîand you'll arrive directly on Broadway Avenue. The walk from downtown takes about 10 minutes.

Walking Tips

  • From Downtown: Cross the Broadway Bridge for a scenic 10-minute walk with river views
  • From River Landing: Walk south along the Meewasin Trail and cross at Broadway Bridge
  • Parking: Street parking is available along Broadway Avenue and side streets. Most spots are metered during business hours
  • Best Route: Start at the north end of Broadway (near the bridge) and walk south to explore the full district

Evening Visit

The Broadway District really comes alive in the evening. Plan dinner at one of the local restaurants, catch a show at the Broadway Theatre, and finish with drinks at a neighbourhood pub. Summer evenings are especially magical with long daylight hours and bustling patios.
