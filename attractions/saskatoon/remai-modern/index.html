About Remai Modern

Remai Modern is Saskatoon's crown jewel of contemporary art, a striking architectural landmark situated on the banks of the South Saskatchewan River at River Landing. Opened in 2017, the museum has quickly established itself as one of Canada's most important modern art institutions.

The museum's collection spans over 8,000 works of modern and contemporary art, with a particular strength in Canadian and Indigenous art. However, its most celebrated holding is the world's largest collection of Picasso linocuts‚Äîover 400 prints that showcase the master's bold experimentation with this printmaking technique.

The building itself is a work of art, designed to frame views of the river and city through its expansive windows. The galleries are bright, spacious, and thoughtfully arranged to create an immersive experience that connects visitors with both the art and the prairie landscape beyond.

The Picasso Linocut Collection

Remai Modern holds the world's largest collection of Picasso linocuts, featuring over 400 works created primarily between the late 1950s and 1960s. These vibrant, bold prints demonstrate Picasso's mastery of yet another medium and are a must-see for any art lover visiting Saskatoon.

What to See

Picasso Gallery

The permanent Picasso linocut gallery showcases rotating selections from the museum's unparalleled collection. Bold colours and striking compositions reveal Picasso's printmaking genius.

Contemporary Exhibitions

Rotating exhibitions feature Canadian and international contemporary artists, with a strong emphasis on Indigenous art and perspectives from the prairies and beyond.

River Views

The museum's architecture frames stunning views of the South Saskatchewan River. The rooftop terrace and river-facing galleries offer some of the best vantage points in the city.

Studio & Workshops

Hands-on art-making studios welcome visitors of all ages. Drop-in programs, workshops, and family activities make art accessible and engaging for everyone.

Planning Your Visit

Remai Modern is centrally located at River Landing, making it easy to combine with a stroll along the Meewasin Valley Trail or exploration of the surrounding riverfront area.

Tips for Your Visit

  • Free Thursday Evenings: The museum often offers free or reduced admission on Thursday evenings‚Äîcheck the website for current programming
  • Free for Youth: All visitors under 18 enter free, making this an excellent family destination
  • Cafe & Gift Shop: The on-site cafe serves excellent coffee and light meals with river views. The gift shop features art books and locally made items
  • Guided Tours: Free docent-led tours are available on select days‚Äîcheck the schedule on arrival
  • Photography: Photography for personal use is generally permitted in the permanent collection galleries, but flash and tripods are not allowed

Combine with River Landing

After visiting the museum, explore the River Landing area. Walk along the Meewasin Trail, visit the nearby farmers' market (seasonal), and enjoy sunset views from the riverbank. The Broadway Bridge is a short walk away for stunning photo opportunities.
