About Beaver Creek Conservation Area

Beaver Creek Conservation Area is a 12-hectare natural oasis in south Saskatoon, preserving a pocket of native prairie grassland and wetland habitat within the city. Managed by the Meewasin Valley Authority, it offers visitors a chance to experience the Saskatchewan prairie landscape as it existed before European settlement.

The conservation area is home to a diverse array of native plants and wildlife, including deer, beavers (of course), foxes, and over 100 species of birds. The interpretive centre provides educational exhibits about prairie ecology, native plants, and the wildlife that calls this area home.

Connected to the broader Meewasin Valley Trail system, Beaver Creek is an easy addition to any exploration of Saskatoon's riverfront. Whether you are a nature enthusiast, birdwatcher, or family looking for an outdoor adventure, this conservation area offers a peaceful escape from the urban environment.

Native Prairie Preservation

Less than 20% of Saskatchewan's original native prairie grassland remains. Beaver Creek Conservation Area protects a valuable remnant of this endangered ecosystem, allowing visitors to see the plants, grasses, and wildflowers that once covered the vast Canadian prairies.

Things to Do

Nature Walks

Well-maintained trails wind through native prairie grasslands and along creek beds. Interpretive signage explains the ecology, plants, and wildlife you may encounter along the way.

Birdwatching

Over 100 bird species have been recorded at Beaver Creek. Spring and fall migrations bring warblers, waterfowl, and raptors. Year-round residents include chickadees, woodpeckers, and owls.

Interpretive Centre

The on-site interpretive centre features exhibits on prairie ecology, native wildlife, and conservation. Educational programs for children and families are offered seasonally.

Cross-Country Skiing

In winter, the trails transform into cross-country skiing routes through a snow-covered prairie landscape. The gentle terrain is suitable for beginners and offers a peaceful winter outing.

Wildlife & Nature

Beaver Creek Conservation Area supports a rich diversity of prairie wildlife. The wetland and grassland habitats provide food and shelter for numerous species throughout the year.

Wildlife You May See

  • Beavers: The area's namesake residents are most active at dawn and dusk along the creek
  • White-tailed Deer: Commonly seen grazing in the grassland areas, especially in early morning
  • Red Foxes: Occasionally spotted hunting in the prairie grass
  • Great Horned Owls: Year-round residents that nest in the mature trees
  • Pelicans & Waterfowl: Visit the wetland areas during spring and summer migration
  • Prairie Wildflowers: In summer, the grasslands bloom with native wildflowers including prairie crocus, wild rose, and goldenrod

Best Wildlife Viewing

Early morning and late afternoon offer the best chances to see wildlife. Bring binoculars and move quietly along the trails. Spring (April-May) is the best season for birdwatching as migratory species return to the prairies.
