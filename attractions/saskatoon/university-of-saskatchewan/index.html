About the Campus

The University of Saskatchewan campus is one of the most beautiful in Canada, perched on the banks of the South Saskatchewan River with stunning views of the city and its bridges. Founded in 1907, the campus is renowned for its distinctive greystone Gothic Revival architecture, which gives it a timeless, collegiate atmosphere.

The original campus plan called for buildings constructed from locally quarried greystone (Tyndall stone), creating a cohesive architectural identity that has been maintained for over a century. Walking through the campus feels like stepping into a heritage postcard, with elegant stone buildings, tree-lined pathways, and manicured gardens.

Beyond its architectural beauty, the campus houses several attractions open to the public, including the Museum of Antiquities, an observatory for stargazing, and beautiful gardens that bloom throughout the growing season.

Greystone Architecture

The University of Saskatchewan's greystone buildings are among the finest examples of Gothic Revival collegiate architecture in western Canada. The College Building (1912) and other early structures set the standard that continues to define the campus character today.

What to See & Do

Museum of Antiquities

This unique museum houses plaster cast replicas of classical Greek and Roman sculptures, along with genuine archaeological artifacts. A fascinating collection that brings ancient history to the Canadian prairies.

Observatory

The campus observatory hosts public stargazing events, offering views of planets, stars, and celestial objects through research-grade telescopes. Check the schedule for open nights, especially popular during clear prairie skies.

Campus Gardens

Beautiful gardens and green spaces dot the campus, including the Patterson Garden and various landscaped areas. Spring and summer bring colourful blooms, while autumn transforms the tree-lined paths into golden corridors.

River Walk

The campus connects directly to the Meewasin Valley Trail along the riverbank. Walk or cycle along the river with views of the University Bridge and downtown skyline. One of the most scenic stretches of the trail.

Visitor Tips

  • Self-Guided Walk: Start at the Bowl (the central green) and explore the original greystone buildings radiating outward. The College Building and Convocation Hall are architectural highlights
  • River Views: Walk to the campus edge overlooking the river for panoramic views of downtown Saskatoon and the bridges
  • Diefenbaker Centre: Visit this centre dedicated to Canada's 13th Prime Minister, John Diefenbaker, a U of S alumnus
  • Parking: Visitor parking is available on campus. On weekends and evenings, many lots are free
  • Seasonal Beauty: Visit in autumn for spectacular fall colours among the campus trees, or in winter for a peaceful snow-covered landscape

Combine with Meewasin Trail

The campus sits directly on the Meewasin Valley Trail. Walk south along the river to the Broadway Bridge and continue into the Broadway District for shops and dining, making a perfect half-day outing.
