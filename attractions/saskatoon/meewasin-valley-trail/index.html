About the Meewasin Valley Trail

The Meewasin Valley Trail is the green ribbon that ties Saskatoon together. Stretching over 80 kilometres along both banks of the South Saskatchewan River, this multi-use trail system is the city's most beloved outdoor amenity and a year-round destination for walkers, cyclists, runners, and cross-country skiers.

"Meewasin" comes from the Cree word meaning "beautiful," and the trail lives up to its name. It winds through urban parks, past heritage sites, beneath the city's seven iconic bridges, and into natural areas where you might spot deer, foxes, pelicans, and beavers along the riverbank.

The trail is maintained by the Meewasin Valley Authority, established in 1979 to conserve the South Saskatchewan River valley. Thanks to their stewardship, the trail system has grown into one of the finest urban trail networks in Canada.

Free for Everyone

The Meewasin Valley Trail is completely free and open to the public year-round. Multiple access points throughout the city make it easy to join the trail from virtually any neighbourhood. No registration or permits are required.

Seasonal Activities

Walking & Running

The paved sections are perfect for walking and running year-round. Enjoy river views, cross all seven bridges, and discover parks and gardens along the way. Popular with locals for daily exercise.

Cycling

Excellent cycling on paved and gravel paths. Rent bikes at several locations along the trail. The flat terrain makes it accessible for all skill levels, with scenic stops throughout.

Cross-Country Skiing

In winter, groomed sections of the trail become cross-country ski tracks. The snow-covered river valley is stunning, and the cold, dry prairie air makes for excellent skiing conditions.

Nature Watching

The river corridor is rich with wildlife. Watch for white pelicans in summer, migrating birds in spring and fall, and deer year-round. The riverbanks are home to beavers, foxes, and countless bird species.

Trail Highlights

  • River Landing to Broadway Bridge: The most popular section, passing Remai Modern and offering stunning views of downtown and the river
  • University Bridge to College Drive: Walk along the riverbank below the University of Saskatchewan's greystone campus
  • Beaver Creek Conservation Area: Nature trails through native prairie with wildlife viewing opportunities
  • Seven Bridges Loop: An ambitious walk or ride crossing all seven of Saskatoon's bridges for a complete city tour
  • Rotary Park to Chief Whitecap Park: Southern extension with quieter paths and natural riverbank scenery

Sunset Walk

For the most spectacular experience, walk the trail section between the Broadway Bridge and the University Bridge during golden hour. The prairie sunset reflecting off the river with the city skyline as a backdrop is unforgettable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, most of the 80+ km trail system is suitable for cycling. The core urban sections are paved, while some outlying areas have gravel surfaces. The trail is relatively flat, making it accessible for casual cyclists. Allow a full day to ride the entire system.

Popular access points include River Landing (near Remai Modern), Rotary Park, Kiwanis Memorial Park, and the university campus. Most bridges also provide trail access. There are parking areas at several points along the trail.

Key sections are maintained year-round, including snow clearing on major paved paths. Some sections are groomed for cross-country skiing. However, more remote areas may not be cleared after heavy snowfall. Check the Meewasin Valley Authority website for current conditions.

Yes, dogs are welcome on the Meewasin Valley Trail but must be kept on leash. There are several off-leash dog parks adjacent to the trail system where dogs can run free. Please clean up after your pet.
