About the Chateau Frontenac

Rising majestically above Quebec City's skyline, the Fairmont Le Chateau Frontenac is more than a hotel - it's a symbol of Quebec, a witness to history, and the most photographed hotel in the world. Its distinctive copper roof, castle-like turrets, and commanding position overlooking the St. Lawrence River have made it one of the most recognizable buildings in North America.

The hotel opened its doors on December 18, 1893, as the crown jewel of the Canadian Pacific Railway's chain of grand railway hotels. Designed by American architect Bruce Price in the Chateauesque style, it was named after Louis de Buade, Count of Frontenac, who served as Governor of New France from 1672 to 1698.

Over its 130+ year history, the Chateau Frontenac has hosted royalty, world leaders, celebrities, and millions of travellers. It witnessed the historic Quebec Conferences of 1943 and 1944, where Winston Churchill, Franklin D. Roosevelt, and William Lyon Mackenzie King planned the D-Day invasion and shaped the post-war world.

World Record Holder

Guinness World Records has officially recognized the Chateau Frontenac as the most photographed hotel in the world. Its distinctive silhouette, set against the dramatic backdrop of Cap Diamant and the St. Lawrence River, has been captured billions of times by visitors from around the globe.

Guided Tours

Even if you're not staying at the hotel, you can explore the Chateau Frontenac on a fascinating guided tour. Led by knowledgeable guides, these 50-minute tours take you through the hotel's grand public spaces while sharing stories of its remarkable history, famous guests, and architectural secrets.

Tour Highlights

  • The Grand Hall: Admire the stunning lobby with its original 1893 fireplace and period furnishings
  • Historic Photographs: See images of royalty, celebrities, and world leaders who've stayed here
  • Quebec Conferences Room: Learn about the secret meetings that helped win World War II
  • Architectural Details: Discover the Chateauesque style and the hotel's multiple expansions
  • Ghost Stories: Hear tales of the hotel's resident spirits (it's rumored to be haunted!)

Booking Tips

Tours fill up quickly in summer and during major events. Book online at least 24-48 hours in advance. English and French tours are available daily. The tour desk is located in the main lobby near the concierge.

Afternoon Tea Experience

The Chateau Frontenac's afternoon tea is a quintessential Quebec City experience. Served in the elegant Place Dufferin restaurant with panoramic river views, this refined tradition features premium teas, delicate finger sandwiches, freshly baked scones with clotted cream, and exquisite pastries.

What to Expect

  • Setting: Elegant salon with St. Lawrence River views
  • Tea Selection: Over 20 premium loose-leaf teas
  • Menu: Finger sandwiches, scones, pastries, and petit fours
  • Duration: Plan for 90 minutes to 2 hours
  • Dress Code: Smart casual (no shorts or flip-flops)
  • Price: $75-95 CAD per person, more for champagne options
  • Reservations: Essential, especially on weekends

Special Occasion Perfect

Afternoon tea at the Chateau makes a memorable celebration for birthdays, anniversaries, bridal showers, or simply treating yourself to something extraordinary. Request a window table for the best views when booking.

Famous Guests

The Chateau Frontenac's guest book reads like a who's who of the 20th and 21st centuries. The hotel has welcomed an extraordinary array of distinguished visitors.

Royalty

King George VI, Queen Elizabeth II, Prince Charles, Princess Diana, Prince William, and many more members of royal families worldwide.

World Leaders

Winston Churchill, Franklin D. Roosevelt, Charles de Gaulle, Ronald Reagan, and numerous heads of state during diplomatic visits.

Hollywood Stars

Alfred Hitchcock (who filmed "I Confess" here), Paul McCartney, Leonardo DiCaprio, Celine Dion, and countless film productions.

Literary Figures

The hotel inspired and hosted numerous writers, becoming a character in many books, films, and television shows over the decades.

Architecture & History

The Chateau Frontenac's distinctive Chateauesque architecture was designed to evoke the grand chateaux of France's Loire Valley. Architect Bruce Price created a building that would be unmistakably Canadian while drawing on European traditions of castle-building.

Key Architectural Features

  • Central Tower: The iconic 75-meter tower added in 1924 became the hotel's defining feature
  • Copper Roof: The distinctive green patina develops naturally over decades of weathering
  • Stone Walls: Built from locally quarried limestone and brick
  • Multiple Wings: Expanded numerous times since 1893, each addition respecting the original style
  • 611 Unique Rooms: No two rooms are exactly alike due to the castle's organic growth

Best Photo Spots

The most iconic views of the Chateau are from the Levis ferry crossing the St. Lawrence River, from Dufferin Terrace looking up, and from Battlefields Park. For interior shots, the lobby and grand staircase are stunning during golden hour light.

Dining at the Chateau

The Chateau Frontenac offers multiple dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining, all within its historic walls.

Restaurant Options

  • Champlain Restaurant: Fine dining with French-inspired Quebec cuisine and river views
  • Place Dufferin: Elegant setting for afternoon tea and casual meals
  • 1608 Wine & Cheese Bar: Quebec artisanal cheeses paired with local wines
  • Le Sam Bistro: Contemporary Quebec cuisine in a lively atmosphere
  • Starbucks: Quick coffee in a historic setting (yes, there's a Starbucks in the castle!)

Practical Tips

  • Free to explore: The lobby and ground floor public areas are open to all visitors
  • Photography: Photos are welcome in public spaces; guests appreciate discretion
  • Best views of the hotel: Step back to Dufferin Terrace or take the Levis ferry
  • Parking: Valet parking available; self-park options nearby
  • Gift shop: Find Chateau-branded souvenirs and Quebec artisan goods
  • Accessibility: The hotel is wheelchair accessible with elevators to all floors
  • Staying overnight: Rooms start around $300-400 CAD/night; book early for best rates
