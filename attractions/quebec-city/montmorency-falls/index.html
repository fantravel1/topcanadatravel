About Montmorency Falls

Montmorency Falls is one of Quebec's most spectacular natural wonders. At 83 metres (272 feet), this thundering waterfall is a full 30 metres taller than Niagara Falls, making it one of the highest waterfalls in eastern North America. The Montmorency River plunges dramatically over a cliff into the St. Lawrence River, creating a breathtaking display of natural power.

Named after Henri II, Duke of Montmorency, the falls were first mapped by Samuel de Champlain in 1613. The area has been a popular tourist destination since the 1800s, when wealthy Quebecois built grand homes nearby to enjoy the spectacular scenery and cooling mist during summer months.

Today, Parc de la Chute-Montmorency offers multiple ways to experience the falls: a cable car whisks visitors to the top, 487 panoramic stairs climb the cliff face, a suspension bridge spans directly over the cascade, and adventure activities like ziplines and via ferrata let thrill-seekers get even closer to the action.

Niagara vs. Montmorency

While Niagara Falls is wider and moves vastly more water, Montmorency Falls wins the height competition by 30 metres. Montmorency's narrow, single-plunge cascade creates a completely different but equally dramatic experience - and without the crowds and commercialism of its more famous southern cousin.

Ways to Experience the Falls

Cable Car (Teleferico)

The glass-enclosed cable car glides from the base to the top in minutes, offering stunning aerial views of the falls. Round trip $18.75 CAD. Wheelchair accessible.

Panoramic Staircase

487 steps zigzag up the cliff alongside the falls. Free to climb, with viewing platforms at multiple levels. Budget 15-20 minutes for the ascent.

Suspension Bridge

Walk directly over the falls on a 98-metre suspension bridge at the top. Feel the mist and hear the roar. Included with cable car ticket or stair access.

Zipline

Soar 300 metres across the gorge at speeds up to 100 km/h with the falls thundering below. Double zipline available for friends. Approximately $25 CAD.

Via Ferrata Adventure

For the ultimate Montmorency Falls experience, tackle the via ferrata - a thrilling cliff-face climbing route that combines hiking, climbing, and mountain engineering. Secured by steel cables and equipped with iron rungs, you'll traverse the rock face directly beside the crashing waterfall.

Via Ferrata Options

  • Traverse Route (2.5 hrs): A horizontal crossing with stunning views - perfect for beginners
  • Ascent Route (3 hrs): Climb up the cliff face beside the falls for a more challenging adventure
  • Complete Circuit (4 hrs): Combines both routes for the full experience
  • Equipment: All safety gear provided including harness, helmet, and gloves
  • Requirements: Minimum age 8 years, moderate physical fitness required

Booking Tip

Via ferrata experiences are popular and have limited capacity. Book online at least 24-48 hours in advance, especially during summer weekends. Wear sturdy closed-toe shoes and dress in layers - the mist can make things cool even on warm days.

Climbing the 487 Stairs

The panoramic staircase is one of the most rewarding free activities in the Quebec City area. This engineering marvel zigzags up the cliff face, with viewing platforms positioned at optimal spots to photograph the falls from different angles.

What to Expect

  • Difficulty: Moderate - equivalent to climbing a 25+ story building
  • Time: 15-20 minutes to climb, longer if you stop at viewpoints
  • Best Direction: Most people climb up and take the cable car down
  • Conditions: Steps can be slippery when wet; handrails available throughout
  • Rest Points: Multiple viewing platforms let you catch your breath
  • Reward: Spectacular close-up views of the cascading water and misty spray

Pro Tip

For the best photos, climb the stairs around mid-morning when the sun illuminates the falls. The rainbow created by the mist is often visible from the lower platforms. Bring a waterproof bag for your camera - you will get misted!

The Suspension Bridge

At the top of the falls, a 98-metre suspension bridge spans directly over the cataract, offering the most dramatic perspective of the plunging water. Standing on the bridge, you'll feel the vibrations from the millions of litres cascading beneath your feet.

The bridge connects the main park area to Manoir Montmorency, a historic building housing a restaurant with spectacular views. On busy days, there may be a short queue to cross, but the experience is worth the wait.

Bridge Access

The suspension bridge is accessible from the top of the falls only. You can reach it via the cable car or by climbing the 487 stairs. Bridge access is included with your cable car ticket or free if you climb the stairs.

Winter at Montmorency Falls

Montmorency Falls transforms into a winter wonderland from December through March. The spray from the falls freezes into a massive cone of ice called the "Sugar Loaf" (Pain de Sucre), sometimes reaching 30 metres in height. This frozen formation has been a Quebec attraction since the 1700s.

Winter Activities

  • Ice Climbing: The frozen falls and Sugar Loaf attract ice climbers from around the world
  • Snowshoeing: Trails through the snow-covered park offer winter views
  • Cable Car: Operates year-round for dramatic winter panoramas
  • Photography: The ice-covered falls are incredibly photogenic
  • Winter Carnival: Special events during Quebec's famous Carnaval

The Sugar Loaf Tradition

In the 1800s, climbing and sliding down the Sugar Loaf ice cone was a beloved winter tradition for Quebec families. Today, while you can't climb the cone, you can walk around its base and marvel at this natural ice sculpture that forms fresh each winter.

Manoir Montmorency

At the top of the falls sits Manoir Montmorency, an elegant building housing a restaurant with spectacular views. The current manor was rebuilt in 1994 after the original 1780s structure burned down, but it maintains the historic charm of the region.

Dining Options

  • Restaurant: Fine Quebec cuisine with floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the falls
  • Terrace: Outdoor dining in summer with unobstructed waterfall views
  • Cafe: Quick snacks and drinks for a lighter stop
  • Gift Shop: Quebec souvenirs and local products

Getting There

Montmorency Falls is located 12 km northeast of Old Quebec, an easy 15-minute drive along scenic Boulevard Sainte-Anne (Route 138).

By Car

Take Highway 440 East to the Route 138 (Boulevard Sainte-Anne) exit. The falls are well-signposted. Parking costs $6.25 CAD at either the upper or lower lot.

By Bus

RTC bus route 800 (Le Metrobus) runs from downtown Quebec City to near the falls. Get off at the Montmorency Falls stop. Journey time is approximately 30 minutes.

By Tour

Many Quebec City tour operators include Montmorency Falls in half-day excursions, often combined with visits to Ile d'Orleans or Sainte-Anne-de-Beaupre.

Two Entrances

The park has two entrances - upper and lower. The lower entrance (base of falls) offers easier access to the stairs and cable car base station. The upper entrance accesses the suspension bridge and Manoir Montmorency directly. Both have parking facilities.
