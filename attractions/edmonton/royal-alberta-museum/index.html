About the Royal Alberta Museum

The Royal Alberta Museum (RAM) is Alberta's largest museum and one of Western Canada's premier cultural institutions. Located in a striking purpose-built facility in downtown Edmonton since 2018, it tells the story of Alberta's natural and human history through immersive galleries spanning over 82,000 square feet of exhibition space.

Originally founded in 1967 as the Provincial Museum of Alberta, it was renamed the Royal Alberta Museum in 2005 following a visit by Queen Elizabeth II. The museum's move from its original Glenora location to its current downtown home represented a transformative moment, resulting in a modern, state-of-the-art facility that dramatically expanded its exhibition capabilities.

The museum houses a collection of over 2 million objects encompassing natural history specimens, archaeological artifacts, ethnographic materials, and historical objects. Its galleries cover everything from Alberta's ancient geological past through its diverse ecosystems to the stories of the Indigenous peoples who have called this land home for millennia.

Royal Designation

The museum received its "Royal" designation from Queen Elizabeth II during her visit to Alberta in 2005, making it one of only a handful of museums in Canada to carry this distinction. The honour recognized the museum's significant contribution to preserving Alberta's heritage.

Major Galleries

Natural History Hall

Explore Alberta's diverse ecosystems from the Rocky Mountains to the prairies to the boreal forest. Life-size dioramas, fossils including dinosaur specimens, and interactive displays bring the province's natural world to life. See ice-age mammals and Alberta's rich paleontological heritage.

Human History Hall

Journey through Alberta's human story from the earliest inhabitants over 13,000 years ago through the fur trade era, settlement, and into the modern age. Artifacts, personal stories, and immersive environments bring Alberta's diverse cultural heritage to life.

Bug Gallery

The museum's most popular exhibit features live arthropods from around the world -- giant beetles, tarantulas, scorpions, leaf-cutter ants, and hundreds more. Interactive stations let visitors observe these fascinating creatures up close. A favourite with children and adults alike.

Indigenous Peoples Gallery

A respectful and comprehensive exploration of the cultures, histories, and contemporary lives of Alberta's First Nations and Metis peoples. Features traditional artifacts, oral histories, and contemporary Indigenous art, developed in close collaboration with Indigenous communities.

Special Exhibitions

In addition to its permanent galleries, the Royal Alberta Museum hosts rotating special exhibitions throughout the year. These travelling and in-house exhibitions cover diverse topics from art and culture to science and technology. Past exhibitions have included dinosaur showcases, Egyptian artifacts, and contemporary art installations.

Exhibition Tips

Special exhibitions sometimes require a separate ticket or timed entry. Check the museum website before visiting for current exhibitions and any additional admission requirements. Thursday evenings (5-9 PM) often offer reduced admission for special exhibitions as well.

Visiting with Children

The Royal Alberta Museum is one of Edmonton's best family attractions. The Bug Gallery is a guaranteed hit with kids of all ages, and the Natural History Hall's life-size animal dioramas captivate young visitors.

  • Bug Gallery: Live insect handling sessions are offered at scheduled times -- check the daily schedule upon arrival
  • Discovery Room: Hands-on exploration space designed for younger children with touchable specimens and activity stations
  • Scavenger hunts: Free activity sheets available at the front desk to guide kids through the galleries
  • Stroller-friendly: The museum is fully accessible with elevators and wide corridors
  • Nursing room: Dedicated quiet space available for parents with infants

Insider Tips

  • Thursday evenings: Extended hours until 9 PM with reduced admission -- the best-value time to visit
  • Start with the Bug Gallery: It gets busiest in the afternoon, so visit first thing in the morning
  • Allow enough time: The museum is larger than it appears. Most visitors underestimate and wish they had more time
  • Gift shop: Excellent selection of books, educational toys, and Alberta-made gifts. One of Edmonton's best museum shops
  • Combine visits: The Art Gallery of Alberta is a short walk away, making a great museum-hopping day
  • Photography: Photos are allowed in most permanent galleries (no flash). Check signage for restrictions in special exhibitions
  • Cafe: The museum cafe offers light meals, coffee, and snacks. It is a good spot to recharge mid-visit

Getting There

The Royal Alberta Museum is centrally located in downtown Edmonton, easily accessible by transit, car, or on foot from most downtown hotels.

By Transit

The museum is a short walk from the Churchill LRT station on both the Capital and Metro lines. Multiple bus routes stop within a block of the museum on Jasper Avenue and 103 Street.

By Car

Several public parking lots and parkades are available nearby. Street parking is metered on surrounding streets. The City Centre Parkade is within a 5-minute walk.

Walking

The museum is in the heart of downtown Edmonton, within walking distance of the Legislature Grounds, Art Gallery of Alberta, Rogers Place arena, and most downtown hotels.
