About West Edmonton Mall

West Edmonton Mall (WEM) is far more than a shopping centre -- it is one of Canada's most visited tourist attractions and a destination unto itself. When it opened in 1981, it was the world's largest mall, a title it held until 2004. Today it remains North America's largest shopping and entertainment complex, spanning over 5.3 million square feet with more than 800 stores and services.

What sets WEM apart from every other mall in the world is its extraordinary collection of indoor attractions. Where else can you ride a roller coaster, surf an indoor wave pool, watch sea lions perform, skate on an NHL-size rink, and shop at hundreds of stores -- all under one roof? The mall attracts over 32 million visitors annually and is Alberta's number one tourist attraction.

The mall was developed by the Ghermezian family and opened in three phases between 1981 and 1998. Its sheer scale is staggering: it covers 48 city blocks, has over 20,000 parking spaces, employs more than 24,000 people, and contains the equivalent of 115 football fields of floor space.

World Record History

West Edmonton Mall held the Guinness World Record for largest shopping mall from 1981 to 2004, when it was surpassed by malls in China and Dubai. However, WEM still holds records for the world's largest indoor amusement park, largest indoor wave pool, and largest parking lot.

Major Attractions Inside WEM

Galaxyland Indoor Amusement Park

The world's largest indoor amusement park features 27 rides and attractions, including the Mindbender triple-loop roller coaster, the Galaxy Orbiter Ferris wheel, and numerous family-friendly rides. Thrill seekers and young children alike will find something to enjoy.

World Waterpark

Home to the world's largest indoor wave pool, World Waterpark features 17 waterslides, a surfing simulator (FlowRider), a lazy river, hot tubs, and a massive beach area. The Blue Thunder wave pool generates 5-foot waves every 10 minutes.

Ice Palace

An NHL-regulation-size ice rink in the centre of the mall. Open for public skating sessions, hockey games, and figure skating. Skate rentals are available. Many Edmonton Oilers players have been spotted practising here.

Sea Lions' Rock

Watch California sea lions in daily shows and presentations. Learn about marine life conservation while being entertained by these charismatic animals. VIP encounters allow you to meet the sea lions up close.

More Entertainment

  • Professor WEM's Adventure Golf: Two 18-hole indoor mini golf courses with themed environments
  • Escape City: Immersive escape room experiences for groups
  • Ed's Recreation Centre: Bowling, billiards, and arcade games
  • Cineplex Cinemas: Multi-screen movie theatre with IMAX
  • VR experiences: Virtual reality gaming and attractions
  • Bourbon Street: Themed dining area inspired by New Orleans
  • Europa Boulevard: European-themed shopping street with replica landmarks

Shopping Highlights

With over 800 stores, WEM features virtually every major Canadian and international retail brand. Key shopping areas include:

  • Luxury Wing: High-end brands including Coach, Michael Kors, and Swarovski
  • Europa Boulevard: Specialty boutiques in a charming European streetscape setting
  • Phase I-III: Major anchors including Hudson's Bay, Simons, and T&T Supermarket
  • Chinatown: Asian specialty stores, restaurants, and grocers
  • Tech corridor: Apple, Samsung, Microsoft, and specialty electronics stores

Shopping Tips

Grab a free mall map at any information kiosk -- you will need it. The mall is divided into phases and themed areas. Download the WEM app for wayfinding, deals, and attraction wait times. The best sales happen during Boxing Week (Dec 26+) and Black Friday.

Dining at WEM

With over 100 restaurants and eateries, you could eat at a different spot every day for more than three months. Highlights include:

  • Bourbon Street: New Orleans-themed dining strip with seafood, Cajun, and international cuisine
  • Food Courts: Multiple food courts with dozens of quick-service options
  • Sit-Down Restaurants: The Keg, Earls, Original Joe's, and dozens more
  • International Cuisine: Japanese, Korean, Indian, Vietnamese, Middle Eastern, and more
  • T&T Supermarket: Asian supermarket with incredible prepared foods and bakery

Insider Tips for Visiting WEM

  • Wear comfortable shoes: You will walk 10,000+ steps easily. The mall is nearly 1 km from end to end
  • Arrive early: Attractions are least crowded when the mall opens, especially on weekdays
  • Multi-attraction passes: If you plan to do Galaxyland and World Waterpark, combo passes save 20-30%
  • Weekday visits: Far fewer crowds Monday through Thursday; weekends and holidays are packed
  • Bring swimwear: If you want to visit World Waterpark, bring your own suit and towel to save on rentals
  • Stay on-site: The Fantasyland Hotel is inside the mall with themed rooms -- convenient for multi-day visits
  • Holiday events: WEM hosts spectacular seasonal events including a massive Christmas display and Santa experience

Getting There

West Edmonton Mall is located in west Edmonton, about 20 minutes from downtown by car or transit.

By Transit

Edmonton Transit (ETS) routes 1, 2, 100, 109, and others serve WEM. The future Valley Line West LRT will connect the mall directly to downtown. Bus service runs frequently from most parts of the city.

By Car

The mall is accessible from Whitemud Drive, 170 Street, and 87 Avenue. With over 20,000 free parking spaces, finding parking is rarely an issue. Use the mall app to remember where you parked -- the lot is enormous.

From Downtown

Drive west on Stony Plain Road or take Whitemud Drive west to 170 Street. Allow 15-25 minutes depending on traffic.
