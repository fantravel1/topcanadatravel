About Rainforest Hiking in Tofino

The temperate rainforest surrounding Tofino is one of the most extraordinary ecosystems on Earth. Towering western red cedars and Sitka spruce, some over 800 years old and rising more than 60 metres into the misty canopy, create a cathedral-like atmosphere that feels impossibly ancient and alive. Every surface is draped in moss, ferns cascade from branches, and the air is thick with the scent of cedar and damp earth.

Pacific Rim National Park Reserve protects a significant portion of this old-growth forest and offers well-maintained trails that make it accessible to visitors of all abilities. The Rainforest Trail is the signature experience: a beautifully constructed 2.4-kilometre boardwalk loop that winds through the heart of the ancient forest, elevated above the sensitive forest floor.

Beyond the national park trails, the Wild Pacific Trail near Ucluelet offers dramatic coastal hiking with ocean viewpoints, and the Big Tree Trail leads to some of the largest individual trees in the region. Together, these trails showcase the incredible biodiversity of Vancouver Island's west coast rainforest.

A Living Cathedral

Tofino's old-growth rainforest receives over 3 metres of rainfall annually, creating one of the most productive ecosystems on the planet. A single old-growth tree can support hundreds of species of moss, lichen, ferns, and insects, making each ancient cedar a living ecosystem in itself.

Top Trails

Rainforest Trail

The must-do trail. Two 1.2 km boardwalk loops (A and B) wind through towering old-growth forest. Interpretive signs explain the ecosystem. Flat and accessible. Allow 30-45 minutes per loop. Located within Pacific Rim National Park Reserve.

Wild Pacific Trail

A stunning 8.6 km coastal trail near Ucluelet with sections through rainforest and along dramatic cliff-top viewpoints. The Lighthouse Loop (2.6 km) is the most popular section. Moderate difficulty with some uneven terrain.

Big Tree Trail

A short but impressive 800-metre boardwalk loop through massive old-growth cedar and spruce trees in the Meares Island Tribal Park. Some of the largest trees in Clayoquot Sound. Access by water taxi from Tofino.

Schooner Cove Trail

A 1 km trail through rainforest to a secluded beach in Pacific Rim National Park Reserve. The trail descends through old-growth forest and bog before reaching a beautiful crescent of sand. Moderate difficulty with stairs.

What You'll See

The old-growth rainforest is a feast for the senses. Here's what to look for on the trails:

  • Western Red Cedars: The giants of the forest, with shaggy bark and gracefully drooping branches. Some are over 800 years old and 3-4 metres in diameter.
  • Sitka Spruce: Towering above 60 metres, these massive trees often have buttressed roots that look like natural architecture.
  • Nurse Logs: Fallen trees that become nurseries for new growth. Look for rows of young trees growing in a perfect line along a decaying log.
  • Epiphytes: Every surface is covered in moss, lichens, and ferns. Some branches support entire gardens of aerial plants.
  • Wildlife: Watch for black bears (especially spring and fall), bald eagles, ravens, Steller's jays, banana slugs, and Roosevelt elk.
  • Mushrooms: The forest floor erupts with fungi, especially in fall. Dozens of species are visible along the trails.

Hiking Tips

Bring rain gear year-round -- this is a rainforest! Boardwalk trails can be slippery when wet. Wear sturdy shoes with good grip. Bug spray is useful in summer. The forest is beautiful in rain, so don't let wet weather stop you -- the misty atmosphere makes the experience even more magical.
