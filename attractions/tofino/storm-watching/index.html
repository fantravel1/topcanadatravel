About Tofino Storm Watching

Tofino pioneered storm watching as a tourism experience, and it remains one of the most thrilling natural spectacles in Canada. From November through February, massive low-pressure systems born in the Pacific Ocean barrel toward Vancouver Island's exposed west coast, bringing enormous swells, howling winds, and skies that churn with raw elemental power.

There is nothing quite like watching waves exceeding 6 metres crash against rocky headlands while you sit warm and dry behind floor-to-ceiling windows, a hot drink in hand. The sheer force of the ocean during a major storm is humbling: spray shoots 20 metres into the air, the ground shakes with each impact, and the sound of the surf is a deep, primal roar.

The Wickaninnish Inn is famous worldwide for popularizing this experience. In the 1990s, the inn began marketing the "off-season" storms as a destination experience rather than something to avoid. The idea caught on spectacularly, and now storm season is one of Tofino's busiest periods. Multiple oceanfront lodges and resorts offer storm watching packages with spa treatments, fine dining, and front-row seats to nature's most dramatic show.

A Tofino Original

Tofino essentially invented storm watching tourism in the 1990s. What was once the quiet off-season is now one of the most popular times to visit, with hotels often fully booked during peak storm periods in December and January.

Best Storm Watching Spots

Wickaninnish Inn

The original storm watching hotel, perched on a rocky point at Chesterman Beach. Every room faces the ocean. The Pointe restaurant offers fine dining with storm views. Rooms from $300+ in storm season.

Cox Bay Viewpoints

Free public viewpoints along Cox Bay offer spectacular storm watching. The beach is fully exposed to the Pacific and receives the full force of incoming swells. Stay well back from the water.

Black Rock Oceanfront Resort

A modern resort built into the rocky shoreline with heated outdoor viewing areas. Storm watching packages include spa credits and dining. A more affordable alternative to the Wickaninnish Inn.

Long Beach Lodge Resort

Directly on Cox Bay with great rooms overlooking the surf. The Great Room lounge has massive windows perfect for storm watching with a glass of wine. Storm season packages available.

What to Expect During a Storm

Pacific storms vary in intensity, but a major storm event is truly spectacular. Here's what a typical storm day looks like in Tofino:

  • Morning: Dark skies roll in from the west. Wind picks up, rain begins in sheets. The ocean swells build steadily, with sets growing larger as the storm approaches.
  • Peak Storm: Waves smash the coastline with tremendous force. Spray and foam fill the air. Winds can reach 100+ km/h, bending trees and rattling windows. The noise is extraordinary.
  • Between Squalls: Brief breaks in the weather allow for bundled-up walks on the beach. The ocean remains wild, and the sky can produce stunning light effects.
  • Evening: Cozy up by the fire in your lodge. Many hotels serve hot chocolate and feature fireplaces in every room. Spa treatments are a popular way to unwind.

Safety Warning

Rogue waves are a real and deadly danger on Tofino's beaches during storms. Never turn your back on the ocean. Stay well above the high-tide line and behind driftwood log barriers. People have been swept away and killed by unexpected surges. Watch from safe viewpoints and designated areas only.

Storm Watching Tips

  • Book Early: Storm season accommodations sell out months in advance, especially December-January at oceanfront properties
  • Pack Properly: Bring waterproof layers, rubber boots, and warm clothing for beach walks between squalls
  • Check Forecasts: Environment Canada marine forecasts help you anticipate the biggest storms
  • Bring Binoculars: Watching waves from a safe distance is enhanced with optics
  • Camera Gear: Waterproof your camera gear. Salt spray travels far inland during big storms
  • Embrace the Rain: You will get wet. That's part of the experience. Warm up afterward in your lodge

Budget Tip

Storm watching from the beaches is completely free. If oceanfront lodges are out of your budget, stay at a more affordable accommodation in town and drive to the beach viewpoints. The storms are just as impressive from public access points at Cox Bay and Chesterman Beach.
