About Whale Watching in Tofino

Tofino sits along one of the richest marine corridors on the Pacific coast, making it one of Canada's premier whale watching destinations. Over 20,000 gray whales pass by on their annual migration between Mexico and Alaska, and many pause to feed in the nutrient-rich waters around Clayoquot Sound. Humpback whales have made a remarkable comeback to these waters, and orca pods patrol the coast throughout summer.

With a sighting rate exceeding 90%, you're almost guaranteed an encounter with these magnificent creatures. Tours operate from Tofino harbour on both open zodiac boats (for a more intimate, adventurous experience) and covered cruiser vessels (more comfortable for families and those prone to seasickness).

Beyond whales, these tours frequently encounter Pacific white-sided dolphins, harbour porpoises, Steller sea lions, harbour seals, sea otters, and bald eagles. The backdrop of old-growth rainforest and rugged coastline makes every tour a visual feast even between wildlife sightings.

The Great Migration

Every spring, approximately 20,000 gray whales migrate past Tofino on their 16,000-kilometre journey from breeding lagoons in Baja California to feeding grounds in Alaska. This is one of the longest mammal migrations on Earth, and Tofino is front-row seating.

Whale Species You'll See

Gray Whales (Mar-Oct)

The stars of Tofino whale watching. These 15-metre giants feed close to shore, often in water just 5-10 metres deep. Some "resident" grays stay all summer rather than continuing to Alaska. Watch for their heart-shaped blow.

Humpback Whales (May-Sep)

These acrobatic giants are known for spectacular breaches, tail slaps, and bubble-net feeding. Growing up to 16 metres, humpbacks have made a remarkable recovery in BC waters. Their songs can be heard underwater.

Orcas (Summer)

Both transient (Bigg's) and resident orca populations frequent these waters. Transients hunt marine mammals while residents feed on salmon. Seeing a pod of orcas with dorsal fins slicing the surface is unforgettable.

Other Marine Life

Sea otters floating on their backs, Steller sea lions hauled out on rocks, Pacific white-sided dolphins surfing the bow wave, harbour porpoises, and bald eagles soaring overhead round out the marine safari.

Tour Operators

Jamie's Whaling Station

Tofino's original and most established whale watching company, operating since 1982. Jamie's offers both zodiac and covered cruiser tours departing from their harbourfront location. Their experienced captains and naturalist guides know exactly where to find whales. They also offer combination whale watching and Hot Springs Cove tours.

Tour Boat Options

  • Zodiac Tours: Open inflatable boats that get you closer to the water and wildlife. More adventurous and exciting, but you'll get spray. Best for those without mobility issues who enjoy an active experience.
  • Covered Cruiser Tours: Enclosed vessels with heated cabins, washrooms, and covered decks. More comfortable for families, seniors, and those prone to seasickness. Slightly less intimate but more sheltered.

Booking Tips

Book at least a few days ahead in summer, as popular morning tours sell out. Morning departures typically have calmer seas. Take motion sickness medication 30 minutes before departure if you're prone to it. Dress in warm layers even in summer -- it's always cooler on the water.

Seasonal Whale Watching Guide

Spring (March - May): Gray Whale Migration

The peak of the northbound gray whale migration. Over 20,000 whales pass through, often visible from shore. Many pause to feed in shallow waters around Tofino. This is the best time for guaranteed gray whale sightings and the annual Pacific Rim Whale Festival celebrates the migration in March.

Summer (June - August): Maximum Diversity

The best time for whale watching variety. Resident gray whales, humpbacks, and orcas are all present. Calmer seas make for more comfortable tours. Sea otters, dolphins, and sea lions are also abundant. This is peak season, so book well in advance.

Early Fall (September - October): Late Season

Humpbacks are still feeding before their migration south. Gray whales remain in the area. Fewer tour boats and smaller crowds make for a more intimate experience. Weather can be variable but dramatic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Tofino offers sightings of gray whales (March-October), humpback whales (May-September), and orcas (killer whales) during summer months. You may also spot Pacific white-sided dolphins, harbour porpoises, Steller sea lions, sea otters, and bald eagles on every tour.

Adult whale watching tours start from approximately $110 CAD per person. Children's rates are typically $70-90 CAD. Tours last 2-3 hours and include all safety equipment. Combination tours with Hot Springs Cove or bear watching are also available at higher prices.

Tofino whale watching operators report sighting rates exceeding 90% during the main season (March-October). While wildlife is never guaranteed, the rich waters around Clayoquot Sound support such abundant marine life that some form of spectacular wildlife sighting is almost certain.

Zodiacs offer a more thrilling, intimate experience closer to the water -- great for adventurous types and photographers. Covered cruisers are more comfortable with heated cabins and washrooms -- better for families, seniors, and those concerned about seasickness. Both get equally close to the whales.

The open Pacific can be choppy, especially on windy days. If you're prone to motion sickness, take medication 30 minutes before departure, choose a covered cruiser tour, and book a morning tour when seas are typically calmer. Sitting in fresh air and looking at the horizon also helps.
