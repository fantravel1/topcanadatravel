About Bear Watching in Tofino

Tofino and the surrounding Clayoquot Sound are home to a healthy population of black bears that have become one of the area's most popular wildlife attractions. These magnificent animals are regularly spotted foraging on remote beaches, turning over rocks for crabs, grazing in estuaries, and moving through the old-growth rainforest.

What makes Tofino bear watching special is the boat-based approach. Guided tours travel by boat through Clayoquot Sound to remote beaches and inlets where bears feed, particularly during low tide. Observing from the water provides a safe, non-intrusive viewing distance that keeps both visitors and bears comfortable. The bears here are well-habituated to boats and typically continue their natural behaviour as tours watch from a respectful distance.

With a sighting rate exceeding 95%, bear watching tours in Tofino are among the most reliable wildlife viewing experiences in British Columbia. Guides know the bears individually, understand their seasonal patterns, and can share fascinating insights about their behaviour and ecology.

Ethical Wildlife Viewing

Tofino's bear watching operators follow strict ethical guidelines established in partnership with wildlife biologists. Tours maintain safe distances, limit viewing time per bear, keep engines quiet, and never bait or attract bears. This approach ensures the bears remain wild and the experience remains authentic.

What You'll See

Beach Foraging

The most common sight: bears walking along remote beaches at low tide, flipping rocks to find crabs, clams, and other marine invertebrates. They're remarkably dexterous and entertaining to watch as they crack open shells.

Mothers & Cubs

Spring and early summer bring mother bears with cubs to the beaches. Watching tiny cubs learn to forage while their watchful mother feeds nearby is one of the most heartwarming wildlife experiences in Canada.

Estuary Grazing

Bears graze on sedges and grasses in the estuaries of Clayoquot Sound, sometimes wading into shallow water. These lush feeding areas attract bears throughout the season and offer excellent viewing opportunities.

Salmon Season (Fall)

In late summer and fall, bears gather along streams and rivers where salmon spawn. Watching bears catch salmon is a dramatic spectacle. Bears gorge on fish to build fat reserves for winter hibernation.

Planning Your Bear Watching Tour

  • Best Season: May through October. Spring (May-June) is excellent for bears with cubs. Late summer and fall are best for salmon-fishing bears.
  • Time of Day: Tours are timed to coincide with low tide, when bears are most active on beaches. Morning and evening tours often have the best sightings.
  • What to Bring: Binoculars, camera with a zoom lens (200mm+), warm layers, rain gear, and patience.
  • Group Size: Most operators limit group sizes to 10-12 people for a more intimate experience.
  • Children: Most tours welcome children aged 5+. It's a wonderful educational experience for young wildlife enthusiasts.
  • Combo Tours: Many operators offer combination bear and whale watching tours, maximizing your wildlife viewing in a single outing.

Photography Tips

Bring a camera with at least a 200mm zoom lens for the best bear photos from the boat. Increase your ISO to accommodate for boat movement. Shoot in burst mode to capture action shots of foraging and feeding. Overcast days actually produce the best lighting for wildlife photography -- no harsh shadows.
