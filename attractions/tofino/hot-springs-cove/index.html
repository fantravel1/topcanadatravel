About Hot Springs Cove

Hot Springs Cove is one of the most magical natural experiences in British Columbia. Located in Maquinna Provincial Park, about 37 kilometres northwest of Tofino, these geothermal hot springs are accessible only by boat or floatplane, making the journey itself part of the adventure.

The hot springs emerge from the earth at approximately 50¬∞C and flow down a series of naturally formed rock pools before reaching the Pacific Ocean. The pools range in temperature depending on their position: upper pools closest to the source are the hottest, while lower pools are cooled by ocean water, especially at high tide. The ideal soaking temperature of about 37¬∞C is found in the middle pools.

The setting is extraordinary. Imagine soaking in steaming mineral water surrounded by ancient rainforest, with the sound of waves crashing on rocks just metres away. At high tide, cool ocean waves wash into the lowest pools, creating a natural temperature mix that's truly unique in the world.

The Journey Is the Adventure

The 90-minute boat ride to Hot Springs Cove passes through Clayoquot Sound, one of the largest remaining tracts of old-growth temperate rainforest on Earth. Whale sightings are common along the way, making the journey almost as memorable as the destination itself.

Getting There

By Boat (Most Popular)

Tour boats depart from Tofino harbour for the approximately 90-minute journey through Clayoquot Sound. The ride passes stunning coastline with frequent wildlife sightings. Tours from $120 per adult, typically 6-8 hours round trip.

By Floatplane

A spectacular 20-minute flight over the rainforest and coastline. More expensive ($250+) but saves hours of travel time, allowing more soaking. The aerial views of Clayoquot Sound are breathtaking.

The Boardwalk Hike

From the dock, a beautiful 2 km boardwalk trail winds through old-growth rainforest to the hot springs. The walk takes about 30 minutes and is relatively flat. Ancient cedars and lush ferns line the path.

Combination Tours

Many operators offer combo tours that include whale watching on the way to or from Hot Springs Cove. Jamie's Whaling Station and other operators bundle the experience for better value.

What to Know Before You Go

  • Bring a Swimsuit: The springs are clothing-optional, but most visitors wear swimwear. Bring a towel and a dry bag for your belongings.
  • Tide Matters: At low tide, the lower pools are warmer and more separated from the ocean. At high tide, waves crash into the lowest pools, creating a thrilling hot-cold contrast. Both are fantastic.
  • Pack Lunch: Most tours include time for a picnic. Bring food and water as there are no concessions at the springs.
  • Respect the Site: Hot Springs Cove is a sacred site for the Ahousaht First Nation. Pack out all garbage, stay on trails, and respect the natural environment.
  • Water Shoes: The rocks around the pools can be slippery. Water shoes or sandals with good grip are recommended.
  • Weather: Rain adds to the atmosphere. Soaking in hot springs during a rainfall is a quintessential West Coast experience.

Best Time to Visit

The hot springs are best visited from March to October when boat tours operate. Summer offers the calmest seas for the boat ride, but spring and fall have fewer crowds. Aim for a mid-tide visit for the best pool temperatures and the most pools available for soaking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Hot Springs Cove is accessible only by water or air. Most visitors take a boat tour from Tofino (approximately 90 minutes each way) or a floatplane (20 minutes each way). Several tour operators offer day trips that include the boat ride, time at the springs, and often whale watching along the way.

Boat tours start from approximately $120 CAD per adult. Floatplane tours are more expensive, from around $250-300 per person. Most boat tours are full-day excursions (6-8 hours) that include travel time, the boardwalk hike, and 1-2 hours of soaking time at the springs.

The geothermal source water emerges at about 50¬∞C, but it cools as it flows over rocks into natural pools. The main soaking pools average around 37¬∞C (body temperature). Pools closest to the source are hottest, while those near the ocean are cooled by waves, especially at high tide.

Hot Springs Cove has traditionally been clothing-optional, and some visitors do go without swimwear. However, the majority of visitors, especially those on organized tours, wear swimsuits. There are no changing facilities at the springs, so come prepared.

Most tour operators run from March to October only, as winter seas can be too rough for the boat journey. Some floatplane operators may offer winter trips on calmer days, but availability is limited. The best and most reliable access is during summer and early fall.
