About the Royal Tyrrell Museum

The Royal Tyrrell Museum of Palaeontology is one of the world's greatest dinosaur museums and the only museum in Canada dedicated exclusively to palaeontology. Located in the dramatic Alberta Badlands near Drumheller, the museum houses over 130,000 fossils ‚Äî including one of the largest collections of dinosaur specimens on Earth.

Named after geologist Joseph Burr Tyrrell, who discovered Alberta's first dinosaur bones in 1884, the museum opened in 1985 and has since welcomed millions of visitors. It's a must-see destination for anyone visiting Alberta, and well worth the 90-minute drive from Calgary.

The museum isn't just about displays ‚Äî it's an active research facility where scientists continue to uncover and study fossils from the surrounding badlands. Visitors often see palaeontologists at work in the preparation lab, cleaning and preserving new discoveries.

The World's Best-Preserved Dinosaur

The museum's crown jewel is "Borealopelta," a nodosaur discovered in 2011 with remarkably preserved skin, armour, and even stomach contents. Scientists called it "the most well-preserved armored dinosaur ever found" ‚Äî a 110-million-year-old time capsule.

Must-See Exhibits

Dinosaur Hall

The main gallery features over 40 complete dinosaur skeletons including T-Rex, Triceratops, and the famous "Black Beauty" T-Rex ‚Äî one of the most complete ever found.

Cretaceous Garden

Walk through a recreated prehistoric landscape with life-size dinosaur models among living plants that existed in the dinosaur age.

Preparation Lab

Watch scientists clean and prepare real fossils behind glass. Ask questions through the intercom ‚Äî the technicians love sharing their work.

Burgess Shale

Explore the famous Cambrian fossils from BC, including specimens from the Burgess Shale ‚Äî one of the most important fossil discoveries in history.

Lords of the Land

Meet Alberta's legendary Albertosaurus, the province's official dinosaur. Multiple specimens show growth stages from juvenile to adult.

Ice Ages

Discover the megafauna that roamed Alberta after the dinosaurs ‚Äî woolly mammoths, sabre-toothed cats, and giant ground sloths.

Programs & Experiences

Dig Programs (Seasonal)

The museum offers hands-on fossil dig experiences where you can search for real fossils alongside palaeontologists. Programs range from 2-hour introductory digs to multi-day immersive experiences. Advanced booking is essential ‚Äî popular dates sell out months ahead.

Badlands Interpretive Bus Tour

A guided bus tour through the surrounding badlands explains the geology and fossil history of the region. You'll stop at fossil sites and hoodoo formations with expert interpretation.

Summer Programs for Kids

Day camps and special programming keep young palaeontologists engaged with hands-on activities, games, and exclusive behind-the-scenes access.

Day Trip from Calgary

Most visitors combine the Royal Tyrrell Museum with other Drumheller attractions for a full day trip from Calgary. Here's a suggested itinerary:

  • 9:00 AM: Leave Calgary via Highway 2 North, then Highway 72 East
  • 10:30 AM: Arrive at Royal Tyrrell Museum (spend 3-4 hours)
  • 2:30 PM: Drive the Dinosaur Trail scenic loop, stopping at hoodoos and viewpoints
  • 3:30 PM: Photo stop at the World's Largest Dinosaur statue in downtown Drumheller
  • 4:00 PM: Explore Drumheller's shops and cafes, or visit the Atlas Coal Mine
  • 6:00 PM: Return to Calgary
