About Notre-Dame Basilica

Rising majestically in the heart of Old Montreal, Notre-Dame Basilica is one of the most beautiful churches in North America and a masterpiece of Gothic Revival architecture. Built between 1824 and 1829, this National Historic Site of Canada takes your breath away the moment you step inside.

The interior is a symphony of colour and light. The sanctuary glows in deep blue, decorated with thousands of 24-karat gold stars that create the impression of a heavenly night sky. Intricate wooden carvings, dramatic polychrome sculptures, and stunning stained glass windows depicting scenes from Montreal's religious history surround visitors on all sides.

The basilica was designed by Irish-American architect James O'Donnell in the Gothic Revival style, inspired by the great cathedrals of Europe. O'Donnell was so moved by his creation that he converted to Catholicism and is the only person buried in the church's crypt.

Celebrity Connection

Celine Dion married Rene Angelil at Notre-Dame Basilica in 1994 in a ceremony broadcast across Canada. The basilica has also hosted state funerals and major religious events, cementing its place in Canadian cultural history.

The AURA Light Show Experience

AURA is a groundbreaking multimedia experience that transforms Notre-Dame Basilica into a canvas of light, colour, and sound. Combining projection mapping, orchestral music, and immersive technology, AURA illuminates the basilica's architecture in ways you've never imagined.

The 45-minute experience takes visitors on a journey through the basilica's artistic details. Light projections dance across the ceiling, illuminate the intricate woodwork, and bring the stained glass windows to life. The orchestral soundtrack builds to emotional crescendos as the space transforms around you.

AURA Details

  • Duration: Approximately 45 minutes
  • Schedule: Multiple evening shows (typically starting at 6 PM)
  • Tickets: From $35 CAD; advance booking recommended
  • Seating: Guests are seated in the nave facing the altar
  • Photography: Non-flash photography permitted

AURA Booking Tip

Book AURA tickets online in advance, especially for weekend shows and peak tourist season. Arrive 15-20 minutes early to find your seat and soak in the atmosphere before the show begins.

Architectural Highlights

The Great Organ

One of the largest pipe organs in North America with 7,000 pipes. Built by Casavant Freres in 1891, it remains in active use for concerts and services.

The Altar & Reredos

The stunning altarpiece features intricate wooden carvings, religious sculptures, and a magnificent crucifix set against the blue and gold backdrop.

Stained Glass Windows

Unlike typical biblical scenes, the windows depict Montreal's religious history, including the founding of the city and life of Marguerite Bourgeoys.

The Pulpit

An elaborate wooden spiral staircase leads to the ornate preaching platform, a masterwork of woodcarving that took years to complete.

Visiting Tips

  • Best time for photos: Mid-morning when natural light illuminates the stained glass
  • Respectful dress: As an active church, modest dress is appreciated (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Combine with AURA: Visit during the day for photos, return in the evening for the light show
  • Guided tours: 20-minute tours included with admission provide historical context
  • Quiet contemplation: The basilica maintains a reverent atmosphere; speak softly
  • Sunday services: Free to attend, but tourist access is limited during mass

Getting There

Notre-Dame Basilica is located in the heart of Old Montreal, facing Place d'Armes square.

By Metro

Take the Orange Line to Place-d'Armes station. The basilica is directly above the metro exit. This is the easiest and most convenient option.

By Foot

From downtown Montreal, walk south on Rue Saint-Urbain or Boulevard Saint-Laurent through Chinatown (about 15 minutes). From the Old Port, the basilica is a 5-minute walk up Rue Saint-Sulpice.

By Car

Street parking is very limited in Old Montreal. Use the underground parking at the Palais des Congres (5-minute walk) or the Notre-Dame parking garage. Expect to pay $15-25 for several hours.
