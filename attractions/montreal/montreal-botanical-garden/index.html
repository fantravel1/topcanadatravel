About the Botanical Garden

The Montreal Botanical Garden is one of the world's largest and most spectacular botanical gardens, ranking alongside Kew Gardens in London and the Berlin Botanical Garden. Spanning 75 hectares adjacent to Olympic Park, this living museum is home to over 22,000 plant species and cultivars from around the globe.

Founded in 1931 during the Great Depression as a job-creation project, the garden has grown into a world-renowned institution. Its 20+ themed outdoor gardens and 10 exhibition greenhouses transport visitors from the tropics to the high Arctic, from ancient China to contemporary Quebec.

The garden is part of Space for Life (Espace pour la vie), Montreal's natural sciences museum complex that also includes the Biodome, Planetarium, and Insectarium. Together, these attractions form one of Canada's most important natural science destinations.

UNESCO Recognition

The Chinese Garden at Montreal Botanical Garden is the largest Chinese garden outside of China and was designed with the collaboration of Shanghai's botanical garden. It's considered one of the most authentic Chinese gardens in the Western world.

Must-See Gardens

Chinese Garden

The largest Chinese garden outside Asia, built in Ming Dynasty style. Pavilions, pagodas, a dream lake, and classical Chinese landscaping create a serene retreat. Stunning during Gardens of Light.

Japanese Garden

A peaceful sanctuary with traditional Japanese design: koi ponds, stone lanterns, a tea garden, and meticulously maintained bonsai. Perfect for meditation and photography.

First Nations Garden

Celebrates the botanical knowledge of Quebec's Indigenous peoples. Features plants used for food, medicine, and crafts by Inuit, Algonquian, and Iroquoian nations.

Rose Garden

Over 10,000 rose bushes featuring 2,500 varieties. Peak bloom in June transforms this garden into a fragrant paradise of colour.

More Gardens to Explore

  • Alpine Garden: Rock garden featuring high-altitude plants from mountains worldwide
  • Flowery Brook: Naturalistic garden with native Quebec wildflowers
  • Shade Garden: Plants that thrive under tree canopy
  • Economic Plants Garden: Plants used for food, fiber, and medicine
  • Toxic Plants Garden: Fascinating collection of poisonous species (safely fenced)
  • Leslie Hancock Garden: Sustainable urban gardening inspiration

The Greenhouses

Ten exhibition greenhouses allow year-round exploration of tropical and exotic environments, even during Montreal's harsh winters.

  • Tropical Rainforest: Lush jungle environment with towering palms and tropical birds
  • Arid Regions: Cacti and succulents from deserts around the world
  • Orchids & Aroids: Stunning collection of orchids and exotic flowers
  • Ferns: Prehistoric-feeling greenhouse featuring fern diversity
  • Begonias & Gesneriads: Colourful flowering plants
  • Bonsai & Penjing: Miniature tree art from Japan and China

Winter Visit Tip

The greenhouses are perfect for winter visits when outdoor gardens are dormant. Warm up among tropical plants while snow covers Montreal outside. The contrast is magical!

Gardens of Light

From mid-September through October, the Montreal Botanical Garden transforms into an enchanted nighttime wonderland during the Gardens of Light (Jardins de Lumiere) festival.

Hundreds of silk lanterns illuminate the Chinese and Japanese gardens, creating a magical atmosphere. Each year features different artistic themes and installations. Live performances, music, and cultural activities complement the stunning visual experience.

Gardens of Light Details

  • When: Mid-September to late October (evenings only)
  • Hours: 7 PM to 11 PM (typically)
  • Tickets: Special evening admission required (separate from day tickets)
  • Best Strategy: Arrive at dusk to watch the gardens transition from day to night
  • Book Ahead: Popular weekends sell out; advance tickets recommended

Getting There

The Botanical Garden is located next to Olympic Park in east Montreal.

By Metro

Take the Green Line to Pie-IX station. The garden entrance is a 5-minute walk. Follow signs for Jardin botanique / Space for Life.

By Bus

Several bus routes serve Olympic Park, including the 139 from downtown. The bus stops directly at the Botanical Garden entrance.

By Car

Parking is available in the Olympic Park lots. Enter from Pierre-de-Coubertin Avenue or Sherbrooke Street. Expect to pay $15-20 for the day.

Combined Visits

Consider combo tickets with the Biodome (indoor ecosystems), Insectarium (insect museum), or Planetarium. All are within walking distance in the Space for Life complex.

Tips for Visiting

  • Wear comfortable shoes: The gardens cover a large area with many paths
  • Bring sun protection: Much of the garden is open with limited shade
  • Plan your route: Pick up a map at the entrance and prioritize your interests
  • Peak seasons: May-June for spring blooms, September-October for Gardens of Light
  • Photography: Bring your camera! Tripods allowed outside peak hours
  • Picnic option: Limited food options; consider bringing snacks or a picnic
  • Mobility options: Rent a golf cart for those with mobility challenges
