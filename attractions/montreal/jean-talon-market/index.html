About Jean-Talon Market

Jean-Talon Market is the beating heart of Montreal's food culture and the largest open-air public market in North America. Since 1933, this beloved institution has been the place where Montrealers come to buy fresh produce directly from Quebec farmers, discover artisan foods, and immerse themselves in the city's culinary traditions.

Located in the vibrant Little Italy neighbourhood, the market sprawls across several buildings and outdoor areas with hundreds of vendors. In summer, the outdoor stalls overflow with fresh fruits, vegetables, flowers, and herbs from local farms. In winter, the action moves indoors where specialty shops, cheese mongers, butchers, and artisan food producers keep the market alive year-round.

More than just a place to shop, Jean-Talon Market is an experience. Chefs from the city's best restaurants arrive early morning to select the freshest ingredients. Families browse for weekend groceries. Food lovers sample Quebec cheeses, maple products, and regional specialties. It's the best place in Montreal to taste authentic terroir.

A Market for Montrealers

Unlike tourist-oriented markets, Jean-Talon is where real Montrealers shop. Prices are reasonable, quality is exceptional, and the atmosphere is authentically local. Vendors often speak primarily French, though most can help in English too.

What to Find at the Market

Fresh Produce

Quebec-grown fruits and vegetables in season: strawberries, corn, tomatoes, apples, squash, and more. Farm-fresh eggs, herbs, and edible flowers.

Quebec Cheeses

Artisan fromageries offering hundreds of Quebec cheeses. Try Oka, local cheddars aged for years, and creamy soft-ripened varieties.

Maple Products

Pure Quebec maple syrup in all grades, maple butter, maple sugar, maple candies, and seasonal products from local sugar bushes.

Cured Meats & Charcuterie

European-style salumis, local pork products, pates, terrines, and specialty meats from traditional producers.

More Market Specialties

  • Bakeries: Fresh bread, pastries, and traditional Quebec baked goods
  • Flowers: Seasonal blooms, potted plants, and garden supplies
  • Spices: Bulk spices from around the world at Olives & Epices
  • Coffee & Tea: Local roasters and specialty tea shops
  • Fish & Seafood: Fresh catches and smoked fish
  • Prepared Foods: Ready-to-eat meals, sandwiches, and market snacks
  • Wines & Ciders: Quebec wines and local apple ciders

Must-Visit Vendors

  • La Fromagerie Hamel: Over 500 cheeses from Quebec and around the world, with expert staff to guide your selection
  • Les Delices de l'Erable: Everything maple - syrups, butters, candies, and seasonal treats
  • Boucherie du Marche: Quality meats and Quebec game including venison and bison
  • Olives & Epices: Bulk spices, olive oils, and specialty ingredients from around the globe
  • Premiere Moisson: Artisan bakery with exceptional bread and pastries
  • Birri: Italian deli with house-made pasta and imported goods

Shopping Tip

Bring cash - while many vendors accept cards, smaller stalls and farmers often prefer cash. Also bring reusable bags, as the market encourages sustainable shopping.

Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring (April-May): Maple season! Fresh syrup arrives. Early greens like fiddleheads appear. Greenhouse flowers fill the stalls
  • Summer (June-August): Peak season with overflowing produce. Quebec strawberries in June, corn in August. Outdoor stalls at maximum capacity
  • Fall (September-October): Apple season with dozens of Quebec varieties. Squash, root vegetables, and harvest abundance
  • Winter (November-March): Market moves indoors. Focus on preserved foods, cheeses, meats, and specialty products. Cozy and less crowded

Getting There

Jean-Talon Market is located in the Little Italy neighbourhood, easily accessible by metro.

By Metro

Take the Orange or Blue Line to Jean-Talon station. The market is a 3-minute walk north. Exit the station and follow the signs - you'll smell the fresh produce before you see it.

By Bike

BIXI stations are located near the market. Bike racks are available on site. The market is connected to the city's bike path network.

By Car

The market has a small underground parking garage (enter from Henri-Julien), but it fills quickly on weekends. Street parking is available but competitive. Taking the metro is strongly recommended.

Tips for Visiting

  • Go early: Arrive before 10 AM for the best selection and smaller crowds
  • Saturday mornings: Busiest but most vibrant time. Come for the atmosphere, expect crowds
  • Weekday visits: Quieter shopping experience with the same great products
  • Come hungry: Sample as you go and grab lunch from the prepared food vendors
  • Explore Little Italy: The surrounding neighbourhood has excellent Italian restaurants and cafes
  • Ask questions: Vendors love discussing their products. Don't be shy!
  • Check hours: Individual vendor hours vary, especially in winter. Core hours are reliable
