About Mount Royal Park

Mount Royal Park is the green heart of Montreal, a 200-hectare urban oasis that rises above the city and gives it its name. The "mountain" (technically a large hill) has been a beloved gathering place for Montrealers since it was designated a public park in 1876.

The park was designed by Frederick Law Olmsted, the visionary landscape architect behind New York's Central Park. Commissioned in 1874, Olmsted created a park that preserved the mountain's natural character while making it accessible to everyone. His winding paths, strategic viewpoints, and seamless integration of nature remain the park's defining features today.

Whether you're hiking forest trails, taking in panoramic views from Kondiaronk Belvedere, paddle boating on Beaver Lake, or enjoying a picnic on the grass, Mount Royal offers an escape from urban life just minutes from downtown Montreal.

The Mountain's Gift to the City

Montreal takes its name from Mount Royal ("Mont Real" meaning "Royal Mountain" in Old French). Jacques Cartier named it in 1535 when he climbed to the summit and saw the surrounding landscape stretching to the horizon.

Must-See Highlights

Kondiaronk Belvedere

The crown jewel of Mount Royal, this semi-circular lookout offers the most iconic view of downtown Montreal. Named after a Huron-Wendat chief who signed the Great Peace of Montreal in 1701, the belvedere features a stone terrace, a chalet with refreshments, and sweeping views of the St. Lawrence River, downtown skyscrapers, and beyond.

The Mount Royal Cross

The illuminated 31.4-metre steel cross has been a symbol of Montreal since 1924, replacing earlier wooden crosses dating back to 1643. Visible from across the city, it's especially striking at night. A trail leads to the base for close-up views.

Beaver Lake (Lac aux Castors)

This man-made lake is a year-round destination. In summer, rent paddle boats or relax on the surrounding lawn. In winter, the lake transforms into a refrigerated outdoor skating rink with a cozy chalet serving hot chocolate.

Smith House

The park's welcome centre offers exhibits about the mountain's history, ecology, and Olmsted's design vision. Pick up trail maps and get oriented before exploring.

Sunset Tip

Kondiaronk Belvedere is spectacular at sunset, when the downtown skyline glows golden before the city lights flicker on. Arrive 30-45 minutes before sunset to secure a good viewing spot.

Activities by Season

Summer

Hiking, picnicking, paddle boating on Beaver Lake, Tam-Tams drumming circles on Sundays, cycling, birdwatching, and outdoor yoga sessions.

Fall

Spectacular autumn foliage covering the mountain in reds, oranges, and golds. Perfect for photography and peaceful hiking on cooler days.

Winter

Cross-country skiing on 22 km of trails, snowshoeing, ice skating at Beaver Lake, tobogganing hills, and magical snow-covered scenery.

Spring

Watch the mountain come alive with blooming flowers and migrating birds. Great for moderate-weather hiking before summer crowds arrive.

Hiking Trails

Mount Royal offers trails for all fitness levels, from paved paths to forest trails.

  • Olmsted Trail: The main path from the George-Etienne Cartier Monument to Kondiaronk Belvedere. Gentle slope, about 30 minutes
  • Chemin de Ceinture (Belt Road): 5.5 km loop around the mountain, mostly flat, great for jogging
  • Summit Loop: Steeper trails through forest to the highest point and cross. Moderate difficulty
  • Escarpment Trail: The most challenging route with stairs and elevation changes. Great workout with forest views
  • Serpentine Path: Gentle switchbacks from Avenue du Parc to the chalet. Good for families

Getting There

Mount Royal is easily accessible by transit, bike, car, or on foot from downtown.

By Metro & Bus

Take the Orange Line to Mont-Royal station, then bus 11 to the summit area near Beaver Lake and Kondiaronk Belvedere. The bus runs frequently and saves you the uphill hike.

On Foot

The most scenic approach starts at the George-Etienne Cartier Monument on Avenue du Parc (near the Tam-Tams gathering spot). Climb the wooden stairs and follow Olmsted's winding path to the belvedere (30-45 minutes).

By Bike

BIXI stations are located near the park entrances. The climb is steep but rewarding. E-bikes make it easier. Bike paths connect to the city network.

By Car

Drive up Chemin Remembrance to parking lots near Beaver Lake and the chalet. Parking is metered and spaces fill quickly on nice weekends. Consider arriving early or taking transit.

Visiting Tips

  • Wear layers: The summit is often windier and cooler than downtown
  • Bring water: Especially for summer hikes. Water fountains available but limited
  • Sunday Tam-Tams: Every Sunday from spring to fall, drummers gather at the Cartier Monument for an impromptu festival
  • Winter gear: Trails can be icy. Wear boots with good grip or bring ice cleats
  • Avoid driving weekends: Parking fills quickly. Transit is easier
  • Bring a picnic: The lawns near Beaver Lake are perfect for lunch
