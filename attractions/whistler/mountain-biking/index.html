About Whistler Mountain Bike Park

The Whistler Mountain Bike Park is widely recognized as the birthplace of lift-accessed mountain biking and remains the world's premier destination for the sport. Since opening in 1999, it has set the standard for bike parks globally, with professionally designed and maintained trails that cater to everyone from first-time riders to World Cup competitors.

Spanning over 80 trails across 1,500 metres of vertical descent, the bike park offers an incredible variety of terrain. Wide, smooth flow trails wind through old-growth forests. Technical rock gardens test advanced riders. Massive dirt jumps launch experts into the air. And the legendary A-Line‚Äîpossibly the most famous mountain bike trail on Earth‚Äîdelivers endless rollers, jumps, and berms.

What makes Whistler special is its combination of quantity, quality, and lift access. The Whistler Village Gondola whisks riders and bikes to the mid-station or top of the mountain in minutes, allowing for maximum vertical in a day. Professional trail crews maintain every feature to exacting standards, ensuring consistent conditions all season long.

Birthplace of Bike Parks

Whistler pioneered the lift-accessed bike park concept in 1999, proving that ski resorts could thrive in summer. The success here inspired hundreds of bike parks worldwide. Many of the sport's legends‚Äîincluding Brandon Semenuk, Steve Smith, and Finn Iles‚Äîdeveloped their skills on these trails.

Trails by Skill Level

Green (Beginner)

Start with Easy Does It and Crank It Up in the Fitzsimmons zone. These gentle trails feature smooth surfaces, wide berms, and small rollers. Perfect for learning body position, braking, and basic cornering on a downhill bike.

Blue (Intermediate)

Progress to B-Line, Blue Velvet, and Smoke and Mirrors. Steeper grades, faster speeds, and more challenging features await. Tabletop jumps and gap options let you push your limits safely as skills develop.

Black (Advanced)

The legendary A-Line is the crown jewel‚Äîmassive jumps, perfect berms, and a non-stop flow from top to bottom. Freight Train, Dirt Merchant, and Angry Pirate demand commitment and advanced bike handling.

Double Black (Expert)

Top of the World accesses the alpine zone with technical rock gardens and stunning views. No Joke, Goat's Gully, and In Deep test even the best riders with steep chutes, massive gaps, and unforgiving consequences.

Legendary Trails

A-Line

The most famous bike trail in the world, A-Line is a masterpiece of trail building. From the Mid-Station to the Fitzsimmons zone, it features perfectly sculpted jumps, berms, and rhythm sections. Most riders can clear all the jumps with moderate speed‚Äîit's surprisingly accessible for an advanced trail. Riding A-Line at sunset is a bucket-list experience.

Top of the World

Taking the Peak Chair into the alpine zone opens up incredible high-altitude riding with panoramic views. The trail combines technical rock features with flow sections, all above the treeline. It's a unique experience you won't find at any other bike park‚Äîbe prepared for changing weather and longer runs.

Dirt Merchant

One of the original jump trails, Dirt Merchant is a continuous descent of tables, step-ups, and step-downs through the forest. It's steeper and more technical than A-Line, requiring committed jumping skills. The final section features massive jumps that attract spectators and photographers.

Pro Tip: Trail Progression

Start with green trails even if you're an experienced cross-country rider‚Äîdownhill bikes handle very differently. Progress to B-Line before attempting A-Line. Most crashes happen when riders advance too quickly. Take a lesson if you're new to bike parks, even if you've ridden trails before.

Rentals & Equipment

Bike Rentals

Don't have your own bike? No problem. Multiple shops in Whistler Village offer downhill-specific rentals, from beginner hardtails to high-end carbon full-suspension machines.

  • Basic Rental: From $129 CAD - Entry-level downhill bike, suitable for beginners
  • Performance Rental: From $179 CAD - Higher-end suspension and components
  • Premium Rental: From $229 CAD - Top-spec bikes, carbon frames, best suspension
  • E-Bike Rental: From $249 CAD - Electric assist for maximum laps

Protective Gear

Full-face helmets are mandatory in the bike park. Rental packages typically include a helmet and often gloves. Additional armor (knee pads, elbow pads, back protector) is highly recommended, especially for beginners. Most rental shops offer armor packages.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable closed-toe shoes (flat pedal) or clipless MTB shoes
  • Long pants or knee pads (crashes happen!)
  • Water bottle or hydration pack
  • Energy bars or snacks
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Light rain jacket (mountain weather changes quickly)

Book Rentals Early

Peak season weekends often sell out of rental bikes by mid-morning. Reserve your bike online at least 24 hours in advance to guarantee availability, especially for premium bikes. Same-day rentals may have limited size options.

Lessons & Camps

Even experienced mountain bikers benefit from lessons when first riding a downhill bike park. The skills‚Äîespecially body position on steep terrain and jumping technique‚Äîare quite different from trail riding.

Lesson Options

  • Intro to Bike Park: 2-hour group lesson, $99+ CAD - Learn fundamental body position, braking, and cornering
  • Intermediate Skills: 3-hour group lesson, $149+ CAD - Progress jumping skills and tackle blue trails
  • Private Lessons: From $189+ CAD/hour - Personalized instruction for faster progression
  • Multi-Day Camps: Various options - Intensive skill development over 2-5 days
  • Women's Clinics: Supportive environment for female riders of all levels

Lesson Recommendation

Book an Intro to Bike Park lesson for your first day even if you're an experienced rider. The two hours will teach you efficient technique and appropriate trail selection. Most riders find they progress faster and have more fun after proper instruction.

Multi-Day & Season Passes

Multi-day passes offer significant savings‚Äîa 3-day pass is typically 15-20% cheaper than three single days. The Bike Park Season Pass provides unlimited access and is worth it if you plan to ride 5+ days. Epic Pass holders receive substantial discounts on bike park passes.

Top of the World Access

Access to the alpine trails via the Peak Chair requires an additional fee (approximately $25 CAD) or is included with the Top of the World upgrade pass. The terrain is more challenging and remote‚Äîrecommended only for confident advanced riders.

Best Time to Ride

Early Season (May-June)

Limited terrain as snow melts. Lower trails open first. Smaller crowds and cooler temperatures. Some trails may be muddy. Great for locals and early-season tune-ups.

Peak Season (July-August)

All trails and lifts operating. Best conditions and longest hours. Largest crowds, especially weekends. Book rentals and lessons well in advance. Crankworx festival brings the world's best riders in August.

Late Season (Sept-Oct)

Beautiful fall colors and fewer crowds. Trails in great condition. Shorter operating hours. Weather can be variable. Excellent time for photography and relaxed riding.

Weekdays vs Weekends

Weekdays offer significantly smaller lift lines and more available rentals. Weekends, especially long weekends, can see 15-20 minute waits for lifts. Arrive early on weekends for best conditions.
