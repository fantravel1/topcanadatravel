About the PEAK 2 PEAK Gondola

The PEAK 2 PEAK Gondola is one of the most remarkable engineering achievements in the ski industry. Opened in December 2008, this aerial tramway connects the upper elevations of Whistler and Blackcomb mountains, creating a continuous link between two of North America's greatest ski areas without requiring skiers to descend to the village.

The gondola holds three world records: the longest unsupported span between two towers (3.024 km/1.88 miles), the highest lift of its kind at 436 metres (1,430 feet) above the valley floor, and the longest continuous lift system in the world when combined with the base gondolas at 11.66 km total.

Beyond its engineering achievements, the PEAK 2 PEAK offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all types. Skiers use it to effortlessly explore both mountains, while sightseers and hikers ride it for the spectacular panoramic views of Coast Mountains peaks, glaciers, volcanic cones, and the village far below.

Engineering Marvel

The gondola required 400 tonnes of steel cable and took nearly 3 years to construct. The 3.024 km unsupported span required revolutionary engineering‚Äîthe longest such span previously built was only 2 km. The system uses 28 cabins carrying up to 4,100 passengers per hour in each direction.

The Glass-Bottom Experience

Two of the 28 gondola cabins feature stunning glass floors, offering passengers an unobstructed view straight down to the valley 436 metres below. Known as the "Silver Cabins," these special cars are painted silver (rather than the standard red) so they're easily identifiable.

Standing on the glass floor as the gondola crosses the valley is a thrilling experience. You can see the tiny dots of skiers on the slopes below, the winding Fitzsimmons Creek, and if you're lucky, wildlife like bears in the valley during summer months.

Getting a Glass-Bottom Cabin

Glass-bottom cabins arrive approximately every 8-10 minutes. Let the operator know you'd like to wait for one‚Äîthere's no extra charge. In busy times, consider riding during off-peak hours (early morning or late afternoon) for a better chance at a glass cabin without a long wait.

What You'll See

Mountain Peaks

360-degree views of the Coast Mountains including Whistler Peak (2,182m), Blackcomb Peak (2,284m), and dozens of surrounding summits stretching to the horizon.

Glaciers & Snowfields

See the Horstman Glacier on Blackcomb and permanent snowfields that remain year-round. In clear weather, spot glaciers on distant peaks up to 50 km away.

Volcanic Cones

Look south toward Mount Garibaldi and Black Tusk, distinctive volcanic peaks in Garibaldi Provincial Park. Black Tusk's jagged spire is unmistakable.

Valley Views

Look down at Whistler Village 436 metres below, Fitzsimmons Creek, the Whistler Golf Course, and the winding Sea-to-Sky Highway approaching from Vancouver.

Tickets & Pricing

For Skiers & Snowboarders

The PEAK 2 PEAK Gondola is included with any Whistler Blackcomb lift ticket or season pass. Use it throughout the day to explore both mountains‚Äîmany skiers ride over for lunch or to find better snow conditions.

Sightseeing Tickets (Non-Skiers)

  • Adult (19-64): From $69 CAD
  • Senior (65+): From $59 CAD
  • Youth (13-18): From $59 CAD
  • Child (7-12): From $35 CAD
  • Child (6 & under): Free with paying adult

Sightseeing tickets include the base gondola from the village (either Whistler or Blackcomb), access to the Roundhouse or Rendezvous Lodge, the PEAK 2 PEAK crossing, and descent on either mountain. You can spend as much time as you like at alpine viewing areas.

Best Value Tip

Book sightseeing tickets online in advance for the best rates‚Äîyou can save up to 25% compared to day-of window prices. Tickets are valid for the selected date only, but you can ride multiple times throughout the day.

Best Time to Ride

Clear Winter Days

Cold, clear days after a weather system offer the best visibility. Mountain peaks sparkle with fresh snow and you can see for hundreds of kilometres. The contrast between blue sky and white snow is spectacular.

Summer Mornings

Summer offers longer operating hours and the best chance to spot wildlife below. Morning rides avoid afternoon clouds that can build up around the peaks. Wildflowers bloom in alpine meadows from July to August.

Sunset Rides

Late afternoon rides catch golden light on the peaks. Check sunset times and plan to be mid-crossing as the sun gets low. The alpenglow on surrounding glaciers is magical.

Early/Late Season

Shoulder seasons (late November, late April) offer smaller crowds and lower sightseeing prices. Snow still covers the peaks, but you'll often have cabins nearly to yourself.

Photography Tips

  • Clean Windows: Windows are cleaned regularly, but bringing a microfiber cloth can help with interior smudges
  • Glass Floor Shots: For dramatic shots looking down, use a wide-angle lens and shoot vertically
  • Minimize Reflections: Wear dark clothing and cup your hands around the lens when shooting through glass
  • Video Sweeps: The slow, steady movement is perfect for smooth panning video of the panorama
  • Face Both Directions: Don't just look at your destination‚Äîlook back for different perspectives as you cross
  • Capture Fellow Passengers: People reacting to the height and views make compelling photos (ask permission first)

Pro Photography Tip

For the best interior shots of the cabin with the view, position yourself in a corner facing out. This captures both the engineering of the gondola and the dramatic scenery. The glass-bottom cabins offer unique straight-down perspectives you can't get anywhere else.

Combining with Other Activities

Winter Activities

Use the PEAK 2 PEAK to maximize your ski day‚Äîstart on Whistler, ride across after lunch to explore Blackcomb's bowls, and return for final runs on fresh terrain. Non-skiers can enjoy the alpine restaurants and viewing decks at both the Roundhouse (Whistler) and Rendezvous Lodge (Blackcomb).

Summer Activities

The gondola provides access to alpine hiking trails on both mountains. Start with the High Note Trail on Whistler, ride across, and explore the Overlord Trail on Blackcomb. Sightseeing tickets allow unlimited rides, so you can mix hiking with gondola travel throughout the day.
