Discover Jasper

Jasper National Park is the largest and wildest of the Canadian Rocky Mountain parks. Established in 1907, this UNESCO World Heritage Site covers over 11,000 km¬≤ of pristine wilderness ‚Äî larger than Banff, Yoho, and Kootenay combined. While Banff draws the crowds, Jasper offers a quieter, more authentic mountain experience.

What sets Jasper apart? The world's second-largest Dark Sky Preserve offers stargazing that rivals anywhere on Earth. The park boasts the most diverse wildlife in the Rockies, from grizzly bears to caribou. And spectacular attractions like the Columbia Icefield, Maligne Lake, and the scenic Icefields Parkway make this a must-visit destination.

Frequently Asked Questions

The best time depends on your interests. Summer (June-August) offers the best weather with all trails and attractions open ‚Äî expect highs of 20-25¬∞C but also the most visitors. Fall (September-October) brings golden larches, fewer crowds, and the Dark Sky Festival. Winter (December-March) is ideal for skiing at Marmot Basin and frozen waterfall ice climbing. Spring (April-May) has melting snow and wildlife emerging, but some roads may be closed.

Park entry fees: $11.00 CAD/adult daily, $9.50/senior (65+), free for youth 17 and under. Families/groups (up to 7 in one vehicle) pay $22.00 daily. The Discovery Pass ($145.50 adult, $298.50 family) provides unlimited access to ALL Canadian national parks for one year ‚Äî highly recommended if visiting multiple parks. The same pass works at Banff, Yoho, Kootenay, Waterton, and others. Buy passes online or at park gates.

Jasper has the Rockies' most diverse wildlife. Commonly seen: elk (often in town!), mule deer, bighorn sheep, mountain goats, black bears, and grizzly bears. Lucky sightings: wolves, moose, coyotes, lynx, and caribou. The park has 50+ mammal species. Best viewing times are dawn and dusk along roadsides and meadows. Safety: Keep 100m from bears and wolves, 30m from other animals. Never feed wildlife.

Spirit Island is only accessible by boat via guided cruises operated by Maligne Tours. The 90-minute cruise costs ~$80 CAD for adults and runs from late May to early October. Book in advance ‚Äî especially for July-August when tours sell out. There's no trail to Spirit Island as it's 14 km from the shore. The cruise stops at the island for photos. This is one of Canada's most photographed scenes.

Both are incredible, but they offer different experiences. Choose Jasper for: fewer crowds, more wildlife, dark sky stargazing, and a quieter mountain town vibe. Choose Banff for: more iconic lakes (Louise, Moraine), closer to Calgary, more dining/shopping options, and Lake Louise ski resort. Ideal: Visit both! They're connected by the stunning Icefields Parkway (3-4 hour drive with stops). Most travelers spend 2-3 days in each park.
