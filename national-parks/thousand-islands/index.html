Thousand Islands National Park

1,864 Islands of Adventure

üèùÔ∏è 1,864 Islands üõ∂ Kayaking Paradise üè∞ Historic Castles ‚õ∫ Island Camping

Thousand Islands National Park protects a stunning archipelago of granite islands scattered across the St. Lawrence River where Ontario meets New York State. This UNESCO Biosphere Reserve offers kayaking through island channels, camping on remote islands, exploring historic castles, and scenic cruises through one of North America's most beautiful waterways.

From tiny rock outcrops with a single windswept pine to larger islands with grand summer estates, the Thousand Islands region has enchanted visitors since the Gilded Age. Today, it's a paradise for paddlers, boaters, and anyone seeking island adventures just 3 hours from Toronto or Ottawa.

Activities

Kayaking & Canoeing

Explore sheltered channels between islands. Perfect for beginners to advanced paddlers. Multi-day trips possible with island camping.

Boat Tours & Cruises

1-5 hour cruises from Gananoque or Kingston. See castles, mansions, wildlife, and the international border from the water.

Scuba Diving

Exceptional freshwater diving with shipwrecks, clear visibility, and underwater parks. Dive charters from Brockville and Kingston.

Cycling the Parkway

37 km dedicated cycling path along the Thousand Islands Parkway. Flat, scenic, with river views and beach stops.
