Waterton Lakes National Park

Where the Mountains Meet the Prairie

üèîÔ∏è UNESCO World Heritage üåç International Peace Park ü¶å Abundant Wildlife üè® Iconic Hotel

Waterton Lakes National Park is where the dramatic Rocky Mountains rise straight from the prairies with no foothills in between‚Äîcreating some of the most stunning scenery in Canada. This intimate park is part of the world's first International Peace Park, connected to Glacier National Park in Montana.

Smaller and less crowded than Banff or Jasper, Waterton offers an authentic mountain experience with the iconic Prince of Wales Hotel, brilliant turquoise lakes, abundant wildlife, and the unique ecosystem where prairie meets mountain creates exceptional biodiversity. A UNESCO World Heritage Site not to be missed.

Best Hikes in Waterton

Crypt Lake Trail

One of Canada's best hikes! Boat access, tunnel, ladder, and cable along cliffs to a hidden lake. Thrilling and unforgettable.

17.2 km return | 700m gain | 6-8 hours | Challenging

Bear's Hump

Short but steep hike with incredible views over Waterton townsite, Upper Lake, and mountains. Best sunrise/sunset spot.

2.8 km return | 225m gain | 1-2 hours | Moderate

Carthew-Alderson Trail

Epic point-to-point through alpine meadows, past lakes, over a summit. Shuttle from Cameron Lake to townsite.

20 km one-way | 640m gain | 6-8 hours | Challenging

Bertha Lake

Beautiful lake in an alpine cirque. Pass Bertha Falls en route. Popular but worth it for the stunning scenery.

10.4 km return | 460m gain | 4-5 hours | Moderate

ü¶å Wildlife Watching

Waterton's unique prairie-mountain ecosystem supports incredible wildlife diversity:

  • Black & Grizzly Bears: Common throughout the park, especially spring/fall
  • Bighorn Sheep: Often seen on Red Rock Parkway
  • Deer & Elk: Wander right through the townsite‚Äîdon't approach!
  • Bison: Guaranteed viewing in the paddock near the entrance
  • Mountain Goats: High alpine areas and Crypt Lake trail

üåç Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park

Waterton and Glacier National Park (Montana, USA) together form the world's first International Peace Park, designated in 1932 as a symbol of peace between Canada and the United States.

  • Cruise to USA: Take the M.V. International to Goat Haunt, Montana (passport required)
  • Drive to Glacier: Many Glacier is 1 hour, Going-to-the-Sun Road is 2 hours
  • Combined UNESCO Site: Protected as a single World Heritage Site since 1995

üí° Insider Tips for Waterton

  • Wind warning: Waterton is one of the windiest places in Alberta‚Äîbe prepared!
  • Book Crypt Lake early: The boat fills up fast in summer‚Äîreserve in advance.
  • Deer in town: They're everywhere and seem tame, but keep your distance.
  • Afternoon tea: The Prince of Wales Hotel serves elegant afternoon tea with lake views.
  • Fire recovery: The 2017 Kenow Fire burned 40% of the park‚Äîregrowth is fascinating to see.
