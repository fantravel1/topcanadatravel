Welcome to one of Earth's most geologically significant places. Gros Morne National Park showcases the raw power of plate tectonics‚Äîwhere ancient ocean crust and Earth's deep mantle were thrust onto the surface 500 million years ago.

üåç Why Geologists Love Gros Morne

Gros Morne is a living textbook of plate tectonics. Here's why it earned UNESCO status:

  • The Tablelands: Exposed mantle rock (peridotite) normally found 500 km below surface
  • Ophiolite sequence: Complete cross-section of oceanic crust pushed onto a continent
  • 500 million years of history: From ancient oceans to continental collision to glacial carving
  • Rare minerals: Serpentine, chromite, and other deep-Earth minerals visible at surface

The orange-brown color of the Tablelands comes from iron oxidizing in peridotite‚Äîthe same process as rust on iron.
