Fundy National Park showcases one of nature's most dramatic phenomenaâ€”the world's highest tides. Twice daily, 160 billion tonnes of water flow in and out of the Bay of Fundy, creating tidal swings of up to 16 metres (as tall as a 5-storey building). At low tide, you can walk on the ocean floor among seaweed-covered rocks and tidal pools.

Beyond the tides, the park offers 120+ km of hiking trails through Acadian forests, cascading waterfalls, covered bridges, and a rich marine ecosystem. It's one of Eastern Canada's most unique natural experiences.

ğŸŒŠ Understanding the Tides

The Bay of Fundy tides are among Earth's greatest natural wonders:

  • Tidal range: Up to 16 metres (53 feet)â€”world's highest!
  • Volume: 160 billion tonnes of water, twice daily
  • Cycle: About 6 hours between high and low tide
  • Ocean floor: Walkable 3 hours before to 3 hours after low tide
  • Safety: ALWAYS check tide tablesâ€”water returns quickly!
  • Best viewing: Arrive 1 hour before low tide for the full experience

Check tides at: waterlevels.gc.ca or park visitor centre

Best Hikes

Dickson Falls Trail

Most popular trail with stunning waterfall. Accessible boardwalk through lush forest. Great for all abilities.

1.5 km loop | Easy | 45 min

Third Vault Falls Trail

Park's highest waterfall (16m). Moderate forest hike with rewarding cascade at the end.

7.4 km return | Moderate | 3 hours

Coastal Trail

Dramatic clifftop views of the bay. Watch for seabirds and whales. Can be combined with other trails.

Various lengths | Moderate | 2-6 hours

Laverty Falls Trail

Beautiful 7m waterfall with swimming hole. Popular on hot days. Easy-moderate terrain.

5 km return | Easy-Moderate | 2 hours
