About Banff National Park

Banff National Park is Canada's crown jewel and the country's first national park, established in 1885. Located in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, it spans 6,641 square kilometers of unparalleled mountain scenery.

The park is famous for its impossibly turquoise glacial lakes - most notably Lake Louise and Moraine Lake - dramatic mountain peaks, abundant wildlife, and the scenic Icefields Parkway connecting to Jasper National Park.

Must-See Lakes

Banff's turquoise lakes are fed by glacial meltwater containing rock flour, creating their stunning color. Peak color occurs from late June to early September.

Frequently Asked Questions

June to September is best for hiking when trails are snow-free and lakes reach peak turquoise color. July-August is busiest. September offers fall colors and fewer crowds. December to March is ideal for skiing at Banff Sunshine, Lake Louise, and Mt. Norquay.
Day visits don't require reservations, but parking at Lake Louise and Moraine Lake requires a Parks Canada Timed Entry Pass from May-October. Campsite reservations are highly recommended - popular sites book within minutes when reservations open in January.
Daily admission is $10.50 CAD for adults, $9.00 for seniors (65+), and free for youth 17 and under. The Discovery Pass ($72.25/adult/year) provides unlimited access to all Canadian national parks and historic sites.
