Cape Breton Highlands National Park is where the ancient Appalachian Mountains plunge dramatically into the Atlantic Ocean. Home to one-third of the world-famous Cabot Trail, this park offers some of Eastern Canada's most spectacular scenery‚Äîtowering coastal cliffs, forested highlands, cascading waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.

Set on Cape Breton Island in Nova Scotia, this is a place of Celtic and Acadian culture, where whale-watching boats set out from tiny harbours, moose graze in highland meadows, and fiddle music fills the pubs each night. It's been called one of the most beautiful islands in the world‚Äîand one visit will show you why.

üõ£Ô∏è Cabot Trail Driving Tips

  • Direction: Clockwise recommended for best cliff-side ocean views
  • Time needed: 1 day minimum, 2-3 days ideal for hiking and exploring
  • Distance: 298 km loop, about 5 hours non-stop driving
  • Fuel up: Fill your tank before entering‚Äîgas stations are limited in park
  • Best photography: Morning on east side, afternoon/sunset on west side
  • Fall tip: Late September to mid-October for peak foliage

Best Hikes

Skyline Trail

The park's signature hike. Boardwalk leads to cliff's edge with Atlantic views. Moose often spotted in meadows. Book sunset slots in advance!

7.5 km loop | 350m gain | 2-3 hours | Moderate

Franey Trail

Steep climb rewarded with panoramic views of the highlands and Atlantic. Less crowded than Skyline. Best at sunrise.

7.4 km loop | 425m gain | 2-3 hours | Challenging

Middle Head Trail

Easy out-and-back to peninsula tip. Ocean views on both sides, seals often spotted. Near Ingonish Beach.

3.6 km return | Minimal gain | 1-2 hours | Easy

Fishing Cove Trail

Steep descent to remote ocean beach with wilderness camping. One of the park's most scenic overnight destinations.

12 km return | 335m descent | 4-5 hours | Challenging

ü¶å Wildlife Watching

Cape Breton Highlands is one of Eastern Canada's best wildlife destinations:

  • Moose: Very common! Dawn/dusk along boggy areas and Skyline Trail
  • Whales: Pilot whales, minkes, humpbacks (June-October)
  • Bald Eagles: Frequently seen soaring along the cliffs
  • Black Bears: Occasionally spotted‚Äîkeep food secured
  • Seals: Harbour seals at Middle Head and coastal areas

The Cabot Trail is a 298 km scenic highway looping around northern Cape Breton Island, passing through the national park. It's considered one of the world's most beautiful drives with dramatic coastal cliffs and mountain vistas. Allow 2-3 days to fully experience it.

Summer (July-August) offers warmest weather and peak whale watching. Fall (late September-October) provides spectacular foliage and Celtic Colours festival. Spring (May-June) brings wildflowers and newborn moose calves.

The Skyline Trail is Cape Breton's most famous hike‚Äîa 7.5 km loop leading to a cliff-top boardwalk overlooking the Gulf of St. Lawrence. It's renowned for stunning sunset views and frequent moose sightings. Reserve in advance for sunset hikes.
