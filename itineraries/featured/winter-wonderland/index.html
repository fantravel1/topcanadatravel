Day-by-Day Itinerary

Days 1-4: Whistler, BC

North America's #1 Ski Resort

  • Fly into Vancouver, transfer to Whistler (2 hours)
  • 3 full days of skiing/snowboarding on 8,100+ acres
  • Ride the Peak 2 Peak Gondola between mountains
  • Evening zipline tour through snowy forest
  • Relax in outdoor hot tubs with mountain views
  • Explore Whistler Village's shops and restaurants
  • Try snowshoeing or cross-country skiing for variety
Ski tip: Buy lift tickets online in advance for discounts. Whistler Blackcomb has terrain for all levels!

Days 5-8: Banff & Lake Louise

Rocky Mountain Winter Magic

  • Fly Vancouver to Calgary, drive to Banff (1.5 hrs)
  • Ski the Big 3: Sunshine Village, Lake Louise, Mt. Norquay
  • Dog sledding adventure through frozen wilderness
  • Ice walk through Johnston Canyon's frozen waterfalls
  • Soak in Banff Upper Hot Springs under the stars
  • Photograph frozen Lake Louise and Fairmont castle
  • Cozy dinners by fireplace in Banff town
Winter tip: Lake Louise is stunning in winter‚Äîwalk on the frozen lake and see the ice castle sculptures!

Days 9-11: Ottawa

Winterlude & Canal Skating

  • Fly Calgary to Ottawa
  • Skate the Rideau Canal Skateway‚Äîworld's largest skating rink (7.8 km!)
  • Warm up with BeaverTails (iconic Canadian pastry)
  • If February: Winterlude festival with ice sculptures
  • Explore Byward Market for winter comfort food
  • Parliament Hill covered in snow
  • Day trip to Gatineau Park for cross-country skiing
Skating tip: Canal skateway is FREE and usually open January-March depending on ice conditions. Check ncc-ccn.gc.ca for status!

üé™ Quebec Winter Carnival

The world's largest winter carnival (since 1955!):

  • When: Late January to mid-February (17 days)
  • Where: Quebec City, centered on Plains of Abraham
  • Bonhomme: The beloved snowman mascot appears everywhere
  • Activities: Night parades, ice sculptures, snow baths, canoe races
  • Effigy: Buy the carnival "effigy" (button) for access to events
  • Caribou: Traditional carnival drink (red wine, whisky, maple syrup)

üß• Winter Packing List

Canadian winter requires proper gear:

  • Base layers: Merino wool or synthetic, avoid cotton
  • Insulation: Down or synthetic jacket, fleece mid-layer
  • Outer layer: Waterproof/windproof jacket and pants
  • Accessories: Warm hat, neck gaiter, insulated gloves
  • Footwear: Insulated, waterproof winter boots
  • Ski gear: Rent at resorts or bring your own
  • Hand/toe warmers: Disposable warmers are essential!
