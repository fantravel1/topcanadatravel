Western Canada Road Trip

21 Days Vancouver to Calgary

Trip Overview

This epic 21-day journey takes you from the Pacific Ocean to the Rocky Mountains, through some of the most spectacular scenery on Earth. Start in vibrant Vancouver, wind through Whistler's mountains, explore BC's interior, then cross into Alberta for the legendary Jasper-Banff corridor and the world-famous Icefields Parkway.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Days 1-3: Vancouver, BC

Pacific Gateway & Urban Nature

  • Explore Stanley Park's seawall by bike
  • Visit Granville Island for food, art, and atmosphere
  • Walk Gastown's historic streets and see the steam clock
  • Take the Sea Bus to North Vancouver's Lonsdale Quay
  • Capilano Suspension Bridge or Lynn Canyon (free alternative)
  • Explore diverse neighborhoods: Chinatown, Commercial Drive
  • Sunset at English Bay or Kitsilano Beach
Tip: Get a Vancouver attractions pass if planning multiple paid sites. Stay downtown or in Yaletown for best location.

Days 4-6: Whistler, BC

Mountain Resort Paradise

  • Drive the Sea-to-Sky Highway (2 hours of stunning scenery)
  • Stop at Shannon Falls and the Sea-to-Sky Gondola
  • Ride the Peak 2 Peak Gondola between mountains
  • Hike alpine trails with panoramic views
  • Mountain biking (summer) or skiing (winter)
  • Explore Whistler Village shops and restaurants
  • Bear watching tour or ziplining
Tip: The Peak 2 Peak Gondola is worth the price‚Äîit's the longest unsupported cable car in the world!

Days 7-9: Kamloops & Okanagan Valley

Wine Country & Desert Landscapes

  • Drive east through dramatic canyon scenery
  • Stop at Kamloops for wildlife park or rafting
  • Head south to the Okanagan Valley wine region
  • Wine tasting in Kelowna, Penticton, or Oliver
  • Swimming and beaches on Okanagan Lake
  • Visit the Kettle Valley Rail Trail
  • Fresh fruit stands along the highway
Tip: The Okanagan has 200+ wineries. Pick 2-3 per day and use a designated driver or tour.

Days 10-13: Jasper National Park

Wildlife, Waterfalls & Wilderness

  • Enter Jasper via Yellowhead Highway from BC
  • Stop at Mount Robson (highest peak in Canadian Rockies)
  • Explore Maligne Canyon and walk through the gorge
  • Boat cruise on Maligne Lake to Spirit Island
  • Hike Valley of the Five Lakes or Wilcox Pass
  • Soak in Miette Hot Springs
  • Wildlife watching (elk, bears, mountain goats common)
  • Stargazing in Canada's largest Dark Sky Preserve
Tip: Jasper has excellent wildlife viewing. Dawn and dusk are best. Never approach animals!

Day 14: Icefields Parkway

The World's Most Beautiful Highway

  • Start early‚Äîthis 232 km drive needs a full day
  • Stop at every viewpoint (there are 100+!)
  • Marvel at Athabasca Falls
  • Walk on the Athabasca Glacier at Columbia Icefield
  • Photograph Peyto Lake's impossible blue
  • Stop at Bow Lake and Bow Summit
  • Watch for bears, elk, and bighorn sheep
  • Arrive at Lake Louise area for overnight
Tip: Don't rush this drive! Stop often. The Glacier Skywalk and Ice Explorer tours are worth the extra cost.

Days 15-19: Banff & Lake Louise

Iconic Canadian Rockies

  • Sunrise at Lake Louise (arrive before 6 AM for parking!)
  • Hike to Lake Agnes Tea House or Plain of Six Glaciers
  • Canoe on Moraine Lake in the Valley of Ten Peaks
  • Explore Banff town's shops and restaurants
  • Ride the Banff Gondola up Sulphur Mountain
  • Hike Johnston Canyon to the ink pots
  • Soak in Banff Upper Hot Springs
  • Wildlife safari in the Bow Valley
  • Visit the Cave and Basin National Historic Site
Tip: Lake Louise and Moraine Lake parking fills by 6 AM in summer. Use Parks Canada shuttles or arrive very early.

Days 20-21: Calgary, AB

Cowboy Culture & Departure

  • Drive to Calgary (1.5 hours from Banff)
  • Explore the vibrant downtown and Stephen Avenue
  • Visit the Calgary Tower for panoramic views
  • Explore Studio Bell and the National Music Centre
  • Walk along the Bow River pathways
  • If timing aligns: Calgary Stampede (July)
  • Depart from Calgary International Airport
Tip: Return your rental car with time to spare. Calgary airport is about 20 min from downtown.

üöó Driving Tips

  • Parks passes: Buy Discovery Pass for unlimited access to national parks
  • Gas up: Fill tank before entering parks‚Äîgas is expensive inside
  • Wildlife: Slow down, especially at dawn/dusk. Animals have right of way!
  • Weather: Mountain weather changes fast‚Äîcheck forecasts daily
  • Winter driving: Winter tires required Oct 1-Apr 30 on mountain highways
  • Road conditions: Check DriveBC and 511 Alberta before driving
