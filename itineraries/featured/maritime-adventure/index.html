Trip Overview

Experience the best of Atlantic Canada on this epic 14-day road trip through Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, and New Brunswick. From Halifax's maritime heritage to Cape Breton's Celtic culture, from PEI's red sand beaches to the Bay of Fundy's highest tides‚Äîthis itinerary captures everything that makes the Maritimes magical.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Days 1-2: Halifax, Nova Scotia

Maritime Heritage & Fresh Seafood

  • Explore the Halifax Waterfront Boardwalk and Maritime Museum
  • Visit the Halifax Citadel National Historic Site
  • Walk the colorful streets and photograph historic architecture
  • Get "Screeched In" at a George Street pub (okay, that's St. John's tradition but Halifax has great pubs too!)
  • Day trip to Peggy's Cove for the iconic lighthouse
  • Enjoy lobster dinner on the waterfront
Tip: Visit Peggy's Cove at sunrise to avoid crowds and get the best photos. Stay in downtown Halifax for easy walking access.

Days 3-4: South Shore & Lunenburg

UNESCO Town & Lighthouse Route

  • Drive the scenic Lighthouse Route south from Halifax
  • Explore Lunenburg, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • See the Bluenose II (when in port) at the Fisheries Museum
  • Visit LaHave Bakery for legendary cinnamon rolls
  • Explore Mahone Bay's three-church waterfront
  • Optional: Kayak around the islands of Chester
Tip: Lunenburg is best explored on foot. Book a room in a heritage B&B for the full experience.

Days 5-7: Cape Breton Island & Cabot Trail

Celtic Culture & Dramatic Scenery

  • Drive to Cape Breton (4 hours from Halifax)
  • Stop at Alexander Graham Bell Museum in Baddeck
  • Drive the complete Cabot Trail loop (allow 2 days)
  • Hike the Skyline Trail for sunset and moose sightings
  • Whale watching from Pleasant Bay or Ch√©ticamp
  • Enjoy Celtic music in local pubs (every night!)
  • Visit the Highland Village Museum for Scottish heritage
Tip: Drive the Cabot Trail clockwise for best ocean views. Book Skyline Trail sunset reservation in advance for summer visits.

Days 8-10: Prince Edward Island

Anne, Lobster Suppers & Red Sand Beaches

  • Cross the Confederation Bridge (13 km!) to PEI
  • Visit Green Gables Heritage Place in Cavendish
  • See the Anne of Green Gables Musical in Charlottetown
  • Experience a traditional PEI lobster supper
  • Relax on PEI National Park's red sand beaches
  • Explore Charlottetown's Victorian downtown
  • Bike the Confederation Trail
  • Try Cow's Ice Cream (a PEI institution!)
Tip: Book lobster supper and Anne musical tickets in advance. New Glasgow Lobster Suppers is the most famous venue.

Days 11-12: Bay of Fundy & Hopewell Rocks

World's Highest Tides

  • Take the Confederation Bridge back to New Brunswick
  • Visit Hopewell Rocks at low tide to walk the ocean floor
  • Return at high tide to see the dramatic difference
  • Explore Fundy National Park's waterfalls and trails
  • Drive the Fundy Trail Parkway for coastal scenery
  • Visit St. Martins sea caves at low tide
Tip: Check tide tables before visiting Hopewell Rocks‚Äîthe difference between high and low tide is up to 16 meters! Plan to visit twice (low and high tide).

Days 13-14: Saint John & Return

Historic Port City & Departure

  • Explore Saint John's uptown historic district
  • Watch the Reversing Falls at tide change
  • Visit the New Brunswick Museum
  • Walk along the beautiful Fundy coastline
  • Drive or fly home from Halifax (or Saint John)
Tip: If flying out of Halifax, it's about 4 hours from Saint John. Leave time for a final seafood meal!
