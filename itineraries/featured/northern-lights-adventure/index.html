Trip Overview

Yellowknife, capital of Canada's Northwest Territories, sits directly beneath the auroral oval ‚Äî making it one of Earth's best places to witness the Northern Lights. With 240+ aurora-visible nights per year, this itinerary maximizes your chances of experiencing this bucket-list phenomenon.

This 5-day trip balances nightly aurora viewing with incredible daytime winter activities: dog sledding, snowmobiling, and exploring the unique culture of Canada's North. Multiple nights ensure high success rates even if weather doesn't cooperate initially.

Day 1
Arrive Yellowknife
First Aurora Hunt

Afternoon: Arrival

  • Fly into Yellowknife (YZF) ‚Äî 2-hour flight from Edmonton or Calgary
  • Check into your hotel or aurora lodge
  • Pick up extreme cold weather gear if renting (most tours provide)
  • Rest and prepare for your first late-night aurora session

Evening: First Aurora Tour (10 PM - 2 AM)

  • Your guide picks you up from your hotel
  • Drive to aurora viewing site outside city lights
  • Warm up in heated cabin/teepee while watching for lights
  • Hot drinks and snacks provided
  • Photography tips from your guide
Tip: The aurora is often most active between 10 PM and 2 AM local time. Nap in the afternoon to prepare for late nights!
Day 2
Dog Sledding + Aurora
Adventure Day

Morning/Afternoon: Dog Sled Adventure

  • Sleep in after your late night
  • Afternoon dog sled tour through the boreal forest
  • Meet the sled dogs and learn about mushing culture
  • Drive your own team or ride as a passenger
  • Hot chocolate break on the frozen lake

Evening: Second Aurora Tour

  • Second night of aurora viewing at a different location
  • Learn to photograph the Northern Lights
  • Indigenous storytelling about the aurora (some tours)
Note: Multiple nights dramatically increase your chances. Yellowknife claims a 95%+ success rate over 3+ nights.
Day 3
Explore Yellowknife
Culture Day

Daytime: Old Town & Culture

  • Explore Yellowknife's colorful Old Town ‚Äî houseboats, bush planes, unique architecture
  • Visit the Prince of Wales Northern Heritage Centre (free!)
  • Walk on frozen Great Slave Lake (one of the world's largest)
  • Browse local Indigenous art and crafts
  • Try local cuisine ‚Äî bison, Arctic char, bannock bread

Evening: Third Aurora Tour

  • Third night of aurora hunting
  • By now you'll be an expert at spotting incoming aurora!
  • Final photos and memories
Day 4
Snowmobile Adventure
Final Aurora Night

Daytime: Snowmobile Excursion

  • Morning/afternoon snowmobile tour on frozen lakes and trails
  • See the stunning winter landscape from a new perspective
  • Visit ice fishing spots or frozen waterfalls
  • Warm up with a campfire lunch on the trail

Evening: Final Aurora Viewing

  • Last chance to see the Northern Lights
  • Celebrate your aurora success!
  • Exchange photos and contact info with fellow travelers
Solar Maximum: 2024-2025 is peak solar activity cycle, meaning stronger and more frequent aurora displays than usual!
Day 5
Departure
Farewell

Morning

  • Sleep in after your final aurora night
  • Enjoy a leisurely breakfast
  • Return rental cold weather gear
  • Last-minute souvenir shopping ‚Äî Northern art, aurora photos
  • Transfer to Yellowknife Airport (YZF)
  • Fly to Edmonton/Calgary for connections home
