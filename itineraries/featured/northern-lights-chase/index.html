Trip Overview

This ultimate Northern Lights itinerary takes you to both of Canada's premier aurora destinations‚ÄîYellowknife in the Northwest Territories and Whitehorse in Yukon. With 7 nights of potential viewing, you'll have an exceptional chance of witnessing the aurora borealis dance across the sky, plus experience Arctic adventures like dog sledding, ice fishing, and soaking in hot springs under the lights.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Days 1-3: Yellowknife, NWT

Aurora Capital of North America

  • Day 1: Arrive Yellowknife, check into hotel, explore Old Town and houseboats
  • Night 1: First aurora viewing tour (10 PM - 2 AM) with heated cabin and hot drinks
  • Day 2: Dog sledding adventure through frozen wilderness (morning), Prince of Wales Heritage Centre (afternoon)
  • Night 2: Aurora hunting tour at different location
  • Day 3: Ice fishing on Great Slave Lake, visit Snowking's Castle (if March)
  • Night 3: Final Yellowknife aurora session
Why Yellowknife: Directly under the auroral oval with 240+ aurora nights per year. 3 nights here gives you 90%+ chance of seeing the lights!

Day 4: Travel Day

Yellowknife to Whitehorse

  • Morning flight Yellowknife ‚Üí Edmonton or Calgary
  • Connecting flight to Whitehorse
  • Arrive Whitehorse late afternoon
  • Check into hotel, explore downtown, dinner
  • Early night to rest‚Äîmore aurora adventures ahead!
Flight tip: Book flights well in advance. Consider stopping in Edmonton for a night if connections are tight.

Days 5-7: Whitehorse, Yukon

Aurora & Hot Springs Paradise

  • Day 5: Miles Canyon hike, SS Klondike tour, Yukon Wildlife Preserve
  • Night 5: Aurora viewing from Takhini Hot Springs‚Äîwatch lights while soaking!
  • Day 6: Day trip to Carcross and Emerald Lake, shopping for Yukon souvenirs
  • Night 6: Guided aurora tour to remote viewing location
  • Day 7: Sleep in, MacBride Museum, final Yukon exploration
  • Night 7: Final aurora viewing opportunity, hot springs soak, farewell dinner
Whitehorse magic: The combination of aurora viewing FROM the hot springs is unforgettable. Takhini Hot Springs offers this unique experience!

Day 8: Departure

End of Adventure

  • Morning flight from Whitehorse
  • Connect through Vancouver to your home destination
  • Or extend: Drive the Alaska Highway, visit Dawson City, or add Banff

üåå Aurora Viewing Tips

  • Best months: December-March for darkest skies and best displays
  • Best time of night: 10 PM to 2 AM, but can appear anytime when dark
  • Moon phase: New moon = darkest skies = best viewing (but aurora is bright enough to see even with full moon)
  • Book tours: Guides know weather patterns and best locations
  • Stay multiple nights: Weather is unpredictable‚Äîmore nights = better odds
  • Apps: Use Aurora Forecast apps to track activity levels

‚ùÑÔ∏è What to Pack

  • Extreme cold gear: Most operators rent parkas, pants, boots‚Äîuse them!
  • Layers: Base layers, fleece, down jacket (for under rental gear)
  • Accessories: Balaclava, hand warmers, toe warmers, multiple gloves
  • Camera: Wide-angle lens, sturdy tripod, extra batteries (cold drains them fast)
  • Snacks: Energy bars for long nights of viewing
  • Swimsuit: For Takhini Hot Springs!
