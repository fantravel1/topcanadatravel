How to Apply for a Canada Visa

General steps for visitor visa applications. See country-specific guides for detailed instructions.

1

Check Your Requirements

Determine if you need an eTA or visitor visa based on your citizenship. Use the official IRCC tool to check.

2

Gather Required Documents

Collect your valid passport, photos, proof of funds, travel itinerary, employment letter, and ties to home country. Requirements vary by application type.

3

Create Online Account

Register at the IRCC online portal to start your application. You can save and continue later.

4

Complete Application Forms

Fill out all required forms accurately. Double-check all information matches your passport exactly. Answer all questions honestly.

5

Pay Fees

Pay the application fee ($7 CAD for eTA, $100 CAD for visitor visa) plus biometrics fee ($85 CAD) if required. Fees are non-refundable.

6

Provide Biometrics

If required, visit a Visa Application Centre (VAC) within 30 days to give fingerprints and photo.

7

Wait for Decision

Track your application status online. Processing times vary by country. You may be asked for additional documents or an interview.

8

Receive Decision & Travel

If approved, you'll receive your eTA confirmation email or visa stamp in your passport. Remember: a visa doesn't guarantee entry‚Äîborder officers make final decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

It depends on your citizenship. Citizens of visa-exempt countries (UK, EU, Australia, Japan, etc.) need an eTA ($7 CAD) when flying to Canada. Citizens of other countries need a visitor visa ($100 CAD). US citizens need only a valid passport. Check our country-specific guides for your exact requirements.

Processing times vary significantly. eTAs are typically approved within minutes to 72 hours. Visitor visas range from 2 weeks to 6+ months depending on your country of residence, time of year, and individual circumstances. Check IRCC's official processing times for current estimates.

An eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) is a simple online authorization for citizens of visa-exempt countries flying to Canada. It costs $7 CAD and is usually approved within minutes. A visitor visa (Temporary Resident Visa) is required for citizens of non-exempt countries. It costs $100 CAD plus $85 for biometrics, requires more documentation, and has longer processing times.

Most visitors can stay for up to 6 months. The border officer determines your allowed stay when you enter Canada. If you need to stay longer, you can apply to extend your stay from within Canada before your status expires. Extensions cost $100 CAD and should be submitted at least 30 days before your status expires.

Required documents typically include: valid passport (valid for your entire stay), completed application forms, two recent passport photos, proof of financial support (bank statements, employment letter), detailed travel itinerary, proof of ties to home country (property, family, job), and letter of invitation if visiting family/friends. Some applicants may need medical exams.

No. A tourist visa (visitor visa or eTA) does not permit you to work in Canada. If you want to work, you need a work permit. There are some limited exceptions for business visitors attending meetings, conferences, or negotiating contracts, but you cannot perform actual work for a Canadian employer.

If refused, you'll receive a letter explaining the reasons. Common reasons include insufficient ties to home country, inadequate financial proof, or incomplete documentation. You can reapply with stronger documentation addressing the refusal reasons. There's no limit on how many times you can apply, but each application requires new fees.
