Accepted Travel Documents

US citizens can enter Canada with these documents:

üìò US Passport

Required for air travel. Your passport must be valid for the duration of your stay. Recommended for all travel to Canada.

ü™™ US Passport Card

Land and sea crossings only. Cannot be used for air travel. Convenient wallet-sized alternative for border crossings.

üöó Enhanced Driver's License (EDL)

Land and sea crossings only. Available in MI, MN, NY, VT, WA. Denotes identity and citizenship.

üåê NEXUS Card

All entry modes. Trusted traveler program for expedited border crossing. Can use dedicated NEXUS lanes.

üì¶ FAST Card

Land crossings. Free and Secure Trade program for pre-approved travelers. Commercial focus but valid for personal travel.

üõ°Ô∏è SENTRI Card

Land and sea crossings. Trusted traveler program primarily for US-Mexico border but valid for Canada land crossings.

Important: Regular Driver's License NOT Accepted

A standard US driver's license is not sufficient to enter Canada. You need a passport, passport card, enhanced driver's license, or trusted traveler card. This applies to all entry methods including driving.

Crossing the Border

‚úàÔ∏è By Air

  • Valid US passport required (passport card not accepted)
  • No eTA needed for US citizens
  • Complete customs declaration form (given on plane or at kiosk)
  • Be prepared to state purpose of visit and length of stay
  • May need to show return ticket or proof of onward travel

üöó By Land

  • Passport, passport card, EDL, or trusted traveler card accepted
  • Have vehicle registration and proof of insurance ready
  • Declare all goods, alcohol, tobacco, and firearms
  • Be prepared for possible vehicle inspection
  • NEXUS members can use dedicated lanes for faster crossing

üö¢ By Sea (Cruise/Ferry)

  • Passport recommended; passport card accepted for closed-loop cruises
  • For private boats: report to nearest CBSA marine office
  • Have passenger list ready for inspection
  • Declare all goods brought into Canada
  • May need to clear customs at first Canadian port

‚è∞ Best Times to Cross

  • Avoid weekends and holidays for shorter wait times
  • Early morning (before 7am) often has lighter traffic
  • Tuesday-Thursday typically less busy than Monday/Friday
  • Check CBSA wait times before traveling
  • Consider NEXUS enrollment for frequent travelers

What to Expect at the Border

  • üõÇ
    Questions You'll Be Asked Purpose of visit, length of stay, where you're staying, employment status, and if you're bringing goods to declare. Answer honestly and concisely.
  • üö´
    Items Restricted or Prohibited Firearms require declaration and may need permits. Cannabis is illegal to bring across the border (even if legal in your state). Certain foods, plants, and animals restricted.
  • üí∞
    Currency Declaration Must declare if carrying $10,000 CAD or more (or equivalent). This includes cash, checks, money orders, and other monetary instruments.
  • üç∫
    Alcohol & Tobacco Limits Duty-free: 1.5L wine OR 1.14L spirits OR 8.5L beer. Tobacco: 200 cigarettes, 50 cigars, 200g tobacco. Exceed limits = pay duty.
  • üéÅ
    Gifts & Purchases Gifts up to $60 CAD each are duty-free. Keep receipts for purchases to show when returning to the US. Know your US duty exemption limits.

Criminal Record? Plan Ahead

Canada may deny entry to anyone with a criminal record, including DUI convictions. If you have any criminal history, check your admissibility before traveling. You may need a Temporary Resident Permit (TRP) or Criminal Rehabilitation application.
