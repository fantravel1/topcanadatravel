Good News: No Visa Required!

UK citizens are visa-exempt for Canada. You only need an eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) if flying to Canada. It's a simple online application that takes about 5 minutes and costs just $7 CAD (approximately ¬£4).

What You Need to Apply

  • üìò
    Valid British Passport
    Must be valid for the duration of your stay in Canada. Your eTA will be linked to this passport.
  • üìß
    Email Address
    You'll receive your eTA confirmation by email. Make sure to use an address you check regularly.
  • üí≥
    Credit or Debit Card
    Visa, Mastercard, or American Express accepted. The fee is $7 CAD (approximately ¬£4).

Only Apply at the Official Website

The only official eTA website is canada.ca/eta. Many third-party websites charge ¬£30-50+ for the same application that costs $7 CAD on the official site. These sites are not affiliated with the Canadian government.

How to Apply for Your eTA

1

Visit the Official Website

Go to canada.ca/eta ‚Äî the only official eTA application site.

2

Complete the Application Form

Enter your personal details, passport information, employment status, and travel plans. Takes about 5 minutes.

3

Pay the $7 CAD Fee

Use Visa, Mastercard, or American Express. The fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome.

4

Receive Your Approval

Most eTAs are approved within minutes. You'll receive confirmation by email. Save or print it for your records.

5

Travel to Canada

Your eTA is electronically linked to your passport. Just travel with the same passport you used to apply.

Your eTA is valid for 5 years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. It allows multiple entries to Canada, with each stay up to 6 months. If you get a new passport, you'll need to apply for a new eTA.

UK citizens can typically stay up to 6 months per visit. The border officer determines your allowed stay when you enter. If you need to stay longer, you can apply to extend your stay from within Canada.

Some applications require additional processing, which can take up to 72 hours. In rare cases, you may be asked to submit additional documents or attend an interview. Don't book non-refundable travel until your eTA is approved.

If you have both UK and Canadian citizenship, you must enter Canada using your Canadian passport. If you're a dual UK/other nationality citizen, apply using the passport you'll travel with. Each passport needs its own eTA.
