Important: Indian Citizens Need Visitor Visa

Indian passport holders are not eligible for the eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization). You must apply for a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) also known as a visitor visa. Processing takes approximately 49 days as of January 2025‚Äîapply well in advance!

If you've previously traveled to USA, UK, Schengen countries, Australia, or other developed nations, include copies of those visas and entry stamps. Strong travel history increases your chances of approval.

Step-by-Step Application Process

1

Create IRCC Online Account

Go to the official IRCC website and create your account. You can use GCKey or Sign-In Partner (bank credentials).

Create Account at IRCC ‚Üí
2

Answer Eligibility Questions

Use the "Find out if you're eligible" tool to determine your application type. Select "Visitor visa, study and/or work permit" and answer the questions about your trip.

3

Complete Application Forms

Fill out IMM 5257 and IMM 5645 carefully. All information must match your passport exactly. Save forms regularly as you work.

4

Upload Documents

Scan and upload all required documents in the specified formats. Maximum file size is usually 4MB per document. Use clear, legible scans.

5

Pay Application Fees

Pay $185 CAD (visa + biometrics) using credit card, debit card, or prepaid card. You'll receive a receipt number to track your application.

6

Provide Biometrics at VFS

Within 30 days of receiving your Biometrics Instruction Letter (BIL), visit a VFS Global centre in India to give fingerprints and photo.

Book VFS Appointment ‚Üí
7

Wait for Processing

Processing takes approximately 49 days. Track your application status online. You may be asked to submit additional documents or attend an interview.

8

Submit Passport for Stamping

If approved, you'll receive a Passport Request Letter. Submit your passport to VFS for visa stamping (5-10 business days). Choose pickup or courier delivery.

Book Your Appointment in Advance

VFS centres can be busy, especially during peak travel seasons (May-July for summer travel). Book your biometrics appointment as soon as you receive your instruction letter. Walk-ins may not be accepted.

Tips for Successful Visa Approval

‚úÖ Show Strong Ties to India

Demonstrate compelling reasons to return: stable employment, property ownership, family dependents, ongoing business, education commitments. This is the #1 factor in visa decisions.

‚úÖ Provide Solid Financial Proof

Show consistent bank balance (not sudden large deposits), regular income through ITR, and enough funds to cover your trip. Rule of thumb: $100 CAD per day of stay minimum.

‚úÖ Be Honest and Consistent

Never provide false information or forged documents. Inconsistencies between your application, documents, and interview (if called) can result in permanent refusal.

‚úÖ Write a Strong Cover Letter

Explain the purpose of your visit, your detailed travel plans, your ties to India, and why you will definitely return. Address any potential concerns proactively.

‚úÖ Include Travel History

Previous travel to USA, UK, Schengen, Australia, or Canada shows you've respected immigration laws. Include copies of all previous visas and stamps.

‚úÖ Apply at the Right Time

Apply 3-4 months before your planned travel date. Avoid peak seasons if possible. Having confirmed plans shows you're a genuine traveler, not someone "testing" the system.

Common Reasons for Rejection

Insufficient ties to India: Can't demonstrate strong reasons to return
Inadequate funds: Insufficient proof of financial support
Unclear travel purpose: Vague or unconvincing reason for visit
Immigration history: Previous overstays or violations in any country
Incomplete application: Missing documents or information
