Culinary Capital

Montreal

French Flair Meets North American Innovation

North America's culinary darling, Montreal blends French sophistication with creative energy. From legendary smoked meat at Schwartz's to world-famous bagels at St-Viateur, the city offers everything from cozy bistros to Michelin-starred tasting menus. Don't miss the BYOB restaurants on Plateau Mont-Royal.

Toronto

200+ Cuisines in One City

As one of the world's most multicultural cities, Toronto's food scene is unparalleled in diversity. Explore authentic Chinatown, Little Italy, Greektown, and Kensington Market. From legendary peameal bacon sandwiches at St. Lawrence Market to haute cuisine on King West, Toronto delivers every flavor imaginable.

Vancouver

Where the Ocean Meets Asia

Vancouver's location gifts it with the Pacific's finest seafood and North America's best Asian cuisine outside of Asia. Feast on spot prawns, wild salmon, and sushi-grade fish steps from the ocean. Richmond's Chinese food rivals Hong Kong, while Granville Island Public Market showcases local artisan producers.

Quebec City

400 Years of French-Canadian Gastronomy

North America's most European city offers an authentic French-Canadian dining experience. Charming bistros line cobblestone streets, serving classic dishes like tourtiere, cretons, and sugar pie. The nearby Ile d'Orleans supplies artisan cheeses, ciders, and produce. Winter means sugar shack season.

FAQ

Common Questions About Dining in Canada

While Canada doesn't have an official national dish, poutine is widely considered the most iconic Canadian food. Originating in Quebec in the 1950s, this comfort food consists of crispy fries topped with fresh cheese curds and smothered in hot gravy. You'll find it everywhere from fast food chains to gourmet restaurants with creative toppings like foie gras, pulled pork, or lobster. Other contenders for national dish status include butter tarts, Nanaimo bars, and Montreal smoked meat.
Montreal and Toronto are consistently ranked as Canada's top food cities. Montreal excels in French-influenced cuisine, bagels, and smoked meat, with a thriving BYOB scene and numerous award-winning restaurants. Toronto offers incredible diversity with over 200 cuisines represented, from authentic Chinatown to Little India. Vancouver is famous for Pacific seafood and has North America's best Asian food outside of Asia. Quebec City provides the most authentic French-Canadian fine dining experience.
Must-try Canadian seafood includes: Atlantic lobster from Nova Scotia and PEI (best May-July), Pacific salmon (especially sockeye) from BC, oysters from both coasts (Malpeque from PEI, Kusshi from BC), Arctic char from the North, Digby scallops from the Bay of Fundy, fresh cod from Newfoundland, and spot prawns from Vancouver (in season May-June). Each region has unique preparations - lobster rolls on the East Coast, salmon candy in BC, and fish and chips in Newfoundland.
Yes, tipping is customary and expected in Canada. The standard tip for restaurant service is 15-20% of the pre-tax bill. 15% indicates satisfactory service, 18% is good service, and 20% or more is for excellent service. Many payment terminals now suggest tip amounts of 18-25%, but you can always enter a custom amount. For takeout or counter service, tipping is optional but appreciated (10-15%). Tips are a significant part of server income as base wages are often lower in the hospitality industry.
Canadians typically eat dinner between 5:30 PM and 7:30 PM. Restaurant peak hours are usually 6:00-8:00 PM, so make reservations for popular spots. Many restaurants stop seating by 9:00-10:00 PM, though major cities have more late-night options. Montreal tends to dine later (7-9 PM) with a more European rhythm. Sunday brunch is very popular, typically served 10 AM - 2 PM, and reservations are highly recommended.
Yes, major Canadian cities have excellent vegetarian and vegan options. Toronto and Vancouver are particularly vegan-friendly, with numerous dedicated plant-based restaurants. Most restaurants now offer vegetarian dishes, and many can accommodate dietary restrictions with advance notice. Apps like HappyCow help locate vegan-friendly restaurants. Note that traditional Quebec cuisine tends to be meat-heavy, but modern Montreal restaurants offer plenty of plant-based alternatives.
